# Village Hub Design

**Hub Type**: Recurring safe space
**First Appearance**: Chapter 2 (Oakhaven — initial)
**Recurring In**: Chapters 5, 8, 10 (each visit reflects the chapter's emotional state)
**POV Characters**: Evelyn and Evan (alternating), party members present
**Purpose**: Rest, shop, bond, prepare, breathe between encounters

---

## Overview

The village hub is the game's emotional anchor — the place the player returns to between chapters to rest, shop, interact with NPCs, bond with party members, and prepare for the next encounter. It is the safest space in the game: no combat, no time pressure, no enemies. But it is not just a shop menu with a backstory. The village is a living, evolving space that changes across chapters to reflect the game's emotional arc.

In Chapter 2, the village is warm, innocent, and unaware. In Chapter 5, it is thriving with party activity and the joy of found family. In Chapter 8, it shows signs of the Witch's shadow influence — darker, more anxious, but still holding. In Chapter 10, it carries the weight of finality — NPCs speak as if saying goodbye, and every interaction feels like it might be the last.

The hub is designed to make the player want to return. Not because of the shops (though those are important), but because of the people. The village is where Evelyn belongs — and where the player falls in love with the world that will eventually take her away.

## Hub Layout

### Core Village Areas

```
                    [Village Gate]
                         |
                  [Central Square]
                  /      |       \
           [Store]  [Well]   [Blacksmith]
              |                 |
       [Traveling Trader]  [Forge + Evelyn's Room]
              |
       [Elder's House] --- [Chapel]
              |
       [Residences] --- [Village Perimeter Wall]
              |
       [Gardens] --- [Path to World Map]
```

Every village hub shares this core layout. The specific visual theme changes based on the village type (farming, mining, trading), but the functional areas remain consistent.

### Functional Zones

#### Central Square
- **Purpose**: Social hub, notice board, ambient NPC interactions
- **Features**:
  - **Notice Board**: Displays active quests, side quests, and story-relevant notices. Updates each chapter.
  - **Well**: Central gathering point. NPCs congregate here. Ambient dialogue triggers.
  - **Rest Spot**: Player can sit at the well to trigger party bond sequences
- **Chapter Evolution**:
  - Ch 2: Clean, peaceful, children playing
  - Ch 5: Bustling with party activity, new NPCs, festival preparations
  - Ch 8: Tense, fewer children outside, dark clouds on the horizon
  - Ch 10: Quiet, solemn, NPCs lingering as if memorizing the moment

#### Blacksmith's Forge
- **Purpose**: Weapon/armor upgrades, special weapon quests, Evelyn's bond NPC
- **Features**:
  - **Upgrade Bench**: Player spends materials to upgrade weapons and armor
  - **Special Project Board**: Tracks ongoing blacksmith quests (weapon forging, material gathering)
  - **Evelyn's Room**: Small room above the forge. Player can interact with Evelyn's personal items (triggers internal monologues that evolve across chapters)
  - **Forge Fireplace**: Warm, active, the heart of the village
- **Chapter Evolution**:
  - Ch 2: Welcoming, personal, the scarf gift scene
  - Ch 5: Busy forging weapons for the party, new projects
  - Ch 8: Working overtime, worried expression, "Stay safe out there"
  - Ch 10: Final weapon upgrade. The blacksmith knows something is coming. "Whatever happens... you come back. Understand?"

#### General Store
- **Purpose**: Consumables, crafting materials, cosmetics
- **Features**:
  - **Consumable Shelf**: Health potions, buff items, utility items
  - **Material Rack**: Crafting components for upgrades
  - **Cosmetic Display**: Clothing variants, accessories, camp decorations
  - **Stock Board**: Shows available items, restocks each chapter
- **Chapter Evolution**:
  - Ch 2: Basic stock — health potions, basic materials, simple cosmetics
  - Ch 5: Expanded stock — party-specific items, rare cosmetics from side quests
  - Ch 8: Diminished stock — supply lines disrupted by Witch's shadow
  - Ch 10: Stocked to capacity — "Take everything you need. Please."

#### Elder's House
- **Purpose**: Main quest direction, lore delivery, story-critical dialogue
- **Features**:
  - **Map Table**: Shows world map, chapter objectives, and available areas
  - **Bookshelf**: Lore fragments, collected journal entries, narrative discoveries
  - **Fireplace Seat**: Story-critical dialogue triggers here
- **Chapter Evolution**:
  - Ch 2: Warm, knowing, the elder suspects but does not ask
  - Ch 5: Celebratory, proud, the elder has never seen the village so alive
  - Ch 8: Concerned, the elder shares information about the Witch's shadow
  - Ch 10: Solemn, the elder gives Evelyn a blessing. "Whatever you face... remember this place. Remember us."

#### Chapel
- **Purpose**: Ambient storytelling, traveling priest appearances, Church state reflection
- **Features**:
  - **Altar**: Quiet space. Player can interact for ambient monologues
  - **Priest Schedule Board**: Shows when the traveling priest visits
  - **Prayer Candles**: Visual indicator of village emotional state (many lit = hopeful, few = anxious)
- **Chapter Evolution**:
  - Ch 2: Peaceful, candles burning, traveling priest visits regularly
  - Ch 5: Full of gratitude, extra candles lit for the party's protection
  - Ch 8: Fewer candles, traveling priest mentions Church troubles
  - Ch 10: One candle burning alone. The priest does not visit. The chapel is quiet.

#### Village Perimeter Wall
- **Purpose**: Party bond sequences, ambient dialogue, emotional moments
- **Features**:
  - **Wall Walk**: Player can walk the perimeter. Triggers ambient dialogue with party members
  - **Watchtower**: Elevated view of the surrounding area. Triggers chapter-specific monologues
  - **Shrine**: Makeshift memorial to villagers lost over time. Updates with new names
- **Chapter Evolution**:
  - Ch 2: Evelyn sits here alone at night. Quiet, lonely
  - Ch 5: Party members gather here for conversations. Lively, warm
  - Ch 8: Evelyn and Evan share a moment here. The mood is heavier
  - Ch 10: The final conversation location. The player should dread coming here.

## Hub Systems

### Shop System

| Shop | Currency | Restock | Special |
|------|----------|---------|---------|
| **Blacksmith** | Copper + Materials | Each chapter | Special weapon quests, upgrade tiers |
| **General Store** | Copper | Each chapter | Cosmetics, consumables, materials |
| **Traveling Trader** | Special Currency (earned via side quests) | Periodic (not every chapter) | Rare items, exclusive cosmetics |

**Upgrade Tiers:**
- Tier 1 (Ch 2): Basic weapons, simple upgrades
- Tier 2 (Ch 5): Party weapons, enhanced upgrades
- Tier 3 (Ch 8): Advanced upgrades, Witch-resistant enchantments
- Tier 4 (Ch 10): Final upgrades — the best the blacksmith can make

### Side Quest System

Side quests are tracked through the Notice Board and assigned by NPCs. Each chapter offers 2-4 side quests.

**Side Quest Types:**
- **Fetch Quests**: Gather materials for the blacksmith or store owner
- **Hunt Quests**: Defeat specific monsters in world areas (outside the hub)
- **Bond Quests**: Spend time with a specific party member (unlocks bond dialogue)
- **Lore Quests**: Discover hidden lore fragments in the world
- **Cosmetic Quests**: Earn unique cosmetics through challenges or puzzles

**Side Quest Rewards:**
- Crafting materials, rare components, cosmetics, bond dialogue unlocks, lore fragments

### Bond System

Party bond sequences are triggered by interacting with party members in the village hub. Each party member has multiple bond dialogues that unlock progressively.

**Bond Sequence Structure:**
```
**[BOND_MEMBER_CH#]** `[PARTY_MEMBER]` — `[Location, context]`

> "Dialogue line under 120 characters."

**Conditions:**
- Minimum Chapter: [X]
- Previous Bond Unlocked: [BOND_ID or None]
- Story Phase: [before_reveal | after_reveal | post_church | witch_campaign | final]
```

**Bond Progression:**
- Chapter 5: Initial bonds — introductions, backstories, getting to know each other
- Chapter 7: Post-Church bonds — processing the truth, deeper conversations
- Chapter 8: Witch campaign bonds — fear, resolve, what we fight for
- Chapter 9: Peak bonds — the deepest connections, the most vulnerable moments
- Chapter 10: Final bonds — conversations that feel like goodbyes without saying goodbye

### Rest System

The player can rest at any time in the village hub. Resting:
- Restores full health and resources
- Advances the in-game clock (may trigger time-sensitive events)
- Occasionally triggers dream sequences or internal monologues for the POV character

**Dream Sequences** (Evelyn only):
- Rare, triggered by resting at specific story points
- Fragments of memories: the Weald, the Cat Beast, the lab, the escape
- Never nightmares — more like echoes. They build Evelyn's emotional landscape.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Hub Evolution

### Chapter 2: "The Secret Guardian" — First Hub Visit

**Emotional State**: Warm, innocent, unaware
**Visual Tone**: Golden hour lighting, children playing, smoke from chimneys, flowers in gardens
**NPCs Present**: Blacksmith, Elder, Store Owner, Priest, Children, Baker
**Available Systems**:
- Blacksmith: Basic upgrades, scarf gift (story)
- General Store: Basic consumables and materials
- Elder: Story direction, first side quests
- Side Quests: 2 available (blacksmith materials, lore fragment)
- Bond Sequences: Blacksmith friendship (story-critical)
**Key Changes from Baseline**: None — this is the baseline
**Atmosphere Notes**: The village is at its most innocent. The player should feel the warmth and safety. This is what Evelyn is protecting.

### Chapter 5: "Bonds" — Party Hub

**Emotional State**: Joyful, bustling, found family
**Visual Tone**: Festival preparations, banners, extra lighting, party camp set up in the square
**NPCs Present**: All Ch 2 NPCs + traveling trader, new side quest NPCs, party members
**Available Systems**:
- Blacksmith: Tier 2 upgrades, party weapon requests
- General Store: Expanded stock, new cosmetics from side quests
- Traveling Trader: First appearance with rare/exclusive items
- Elder: Raid preparation dialogue, side quests connected to party member backstories
- Side Quests: 4 available (one per active party member)
- Bond Sequences: Multiple party members, cross-party interactions
- Party Camp: New feature — rest at the camp triggers group dialogue
**Key Changes from Ch 2**:
- Party camp added to central square
- Festival decorations throughout
- More NPCs, more activity, more life
- Notice board is full — the village is thriving
**Atmosphere Notes**: This is the game's happiest chapter. The hub should feel alive, warm, and full of possibility. The player should never want to leave.

### Chapter 8: "The Witch's Shadow" — Darkening Hub

**Emotional State**: Anxious but resilient, determined, heavier
**Visual Tone**: Darker lighting, overcast skies, fewer decorations, some boarded windows
**NPCs Present**: Core NPCs (fewer side characters), party members, worried villagers
**Available Systems**:
- Blacksmith: Tier 3 upgrades, Witch-resistant enchantments
- General Store: Diminished stock (supply lines disrupted), essentials prioritized
- Elder: Information about the Witch's shadow, side quests focused on village defense
- Side Quests: 3 available (defense-focused, Witch's shadow investigation)
- Bond Sequences: Deeper conversations about fear and resolve
**Key Changes from Ch 5**:
- Party camp is smaller, more utilitarian
- Festival decorations removed
- Some residences boarded up
- Fewer children visible (parents keeping them inside)
- Prayer candles in the chapel are fewer
- The perimeter wall has new reinforcement
**Atmosphere Notes**: The village is holding. It is not broken, but it is under pressure. The NPCs are worried but not panicking. The elder is concerned but resolute. The player should feel the weight building.

### Chapter 10: "The Last Hunt" — Final Hub

**Emotional State**: Solemn, reflective, carrying the weight of finality
**Visual Tone**: Dusk lighting, quiet streets, NPCs standing in groups, speaking in lower voices
**NPCs Present**: Core NPCs, party members (all present), no children, no traveling trader
**Available Systems**:
- Blacksmith: Final upgrades — "Take everything you need."
- General Store: Stocked to capacity — "Please, take it all."
- Elder: Final blessing, no side quests (all must be completed before this chapter)
- Side Quests: None new — any uncompleted side quests from previous chapters remain available
- Bond Sequences: Final bonds — the deepest, most vulnerable conversations
- Party Camp: Final gathering — the party shares one last meal together
**Key Changes from Ch 8**:
- Village is quiet, almost reverent
- NPCs linger as if memorizing the moment
- The blacksmith's forge burns late — final preparations
- The chapel has one candle burning. Just one
- The perimeter wall is fully reinforced. The village is preparing for the worst
- The notice board is blank. There is nothing left to post
**Atmosphere Notes**: This is the last time the player will be here. Every interaction should feel like it might be the last. NPCs speak with the weight of people who sense an ending. The blacksmith's "you come back" is the most important line in the chapter. The player should not know whether Evelyn will.

## NPC Roster and Dialogue Triggers

### Core NPCs (Present in All Visits)

| NPC | Role | Shop | Bond? | Dialogue Triggers |
|-----|------|------|-------|-------------------|
| **Blacksmith** | Upgrades, special weapons | Yes | Yes (Evelyn) | Greeting, upgrade available, quest complete, chapter-specific emotional lines |
| **Village Elder** | Story direction, side quests | No | No | Main quest briefing, side quest assignment, chapter-specific wisdom |
| **Store Owner** | Consumables, cosmetics | Yes | No | Greeting, stock update, chapter-specific concern |
| **Traveling Priest** | Church state reflection | No | No | Doctrine dialogue (Ch 2), troubled dialogue (Ch 8), absence (Ch 10) |

### Conditional NPCs

| NPC | Chapters Present | Notes |
|-----|-----------------|-------|
| **Children** | Ch 2, Ch 5 | Absent in Ch 8 (kept inside), Ch 10 (moved to safety) |
| **Traveling Trader** | Ch 5, Ch 8 | Rare/exclusive items, appears periodically |
| **Party Members** | Ch 5+ | Present in hub, trigger bond sequences, ambient dialogue |
| **Baker** | Ch 2, Ch 5, Ch 8 | Absent in Ch 10 (evacuated with children) |
| **New Side Quest NPCs** | Per chapter | Appear for specific quests, disappear after completion |

### Dialogue Evolution by Chapter

Each core NPC has dialogue variants for each chapter visit:

**Blacksmith:**
- Ch 2: "Swing with me, girl." (warm, inviting)
- Ch 5: "Your friends are welcome here. All of them." (happy, inclusive)
- Ch 8: "I forged something special for you. Witch-resistant. Not much, but... it helps." (concerned, practical)
- Ch 10: "Whatever happens... you come back. Understand? I am not making another scarf for someone who does not come back." (firm, emotional)

**Village Elder:**
- Ch 2: "You protect us. I do not need to know how." (grateful, knowing)
- Ch 5: "I have never seen this village so alive. So many people. So much life." (joyful, proud)
- Ch 8: "The shadow is spreading. I can feel it in the air. But we are still here." (concerned, resolute)
- Ch 10: "Whatever you face... remember this place. Remember us. We will remember you." (solemn, loving)

**Store Owner:**
- Ch 2: "Welcome! What can I get you today?" (cheerful)
- Ch 5: "We have so much stock! The traders have been generous." (excited)
- Ch 8: "Supplies are... limited. But I have what you need." (concerned)
- Ch 10: "Take everything. All of it. Please." (desperate, generous)

## Dependencies

- **Hub State System**: Village state persists and evolves across chapters (visual changes, NPC availability, stock, dialogue)
- **Shop System**: Upgrade tiers, currency management, stock rotation, cosmetic inventory
- **Side Quest System**: Quest tracking, completion tracking, reward distribution
- **Bond System**: Party member bond dialogue triggers, progression tracking, conditions
- **Rest System**: Health restoration, dream sequences, internal monologues
- **Day/Night Cycle**: Affects hub atmosphere, NPC routines
- **Character Models**: All NPCs, party members, Evelyn, Evan
- **Environmental Assets**: Village hub with chapter-specific visual variants (4 versions)
- **Audio**: Ambient village sounds, chapter-specific music, NPC voice lines, blacksmith forge, chapel bells
- **Cosmetic System**: Scarf, clothing variants, accessories, camp decorations
- **Notice Board UI**: Quest display, side quest assignment, story notices

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Village hub is accessible from the world map and returns the player to the world map
- [ ] Core functional zones are present: Blacksmith, General Store, Elder's House, Chapel, Perimeter Wall, Central Square
- [ ] Shop system works with currency, upgrades, stock rotation, and cosmetic inventory
- [ ] Side quests are assignable from the Notice Board and trackable
- [ ] Party bond sequences trigger based on chapter, story phase, and previous bond completion
- [ ] Rest system restores health and occasionally triggers dream sequences
- [ ] Village hub has 4 distinct visual variants: Ch 2 (warm), Ch 5 (festive), Ch 8 (darkened), Ch 10 (solemn)
- [ ] NPC dialogue evolves by chapter for all core NPCs
- [ ] Ambient dialogue system triggers area + character + story phase specific lines
- [ ] Blacksmith's scarf gift in Ch 2 is the first cosmetic item and has narrative significance
- [ ] Party camp is present in Ch 5, 8, and 10 with group rest dialogue
- [ ] Traveling Trader appears periodically with rare/exclusive items requiring special currency
- [ ] Ch 10 hub carries the weight of finality — NPCs speak as if saying goodbye
- [ ] All dialogue lines are under 120 characters
- [ ] Hub has no combat, no time pressure, and no enemies
- [ ] Player can freely explore the hub, interact with all NPCs, and trigger all available systems
- [ ] Uncompleted side quests from previous chapters remain available in subsequent visits
- [ ] Elder provides main quest direction and chapter-appropriate story context
