## 1. Overview

Chapter 4 is the pivot on which the entire story turns. Every suspicion the player has harbored — the Church's deflections, Evelyn's mercy, the strange magical signatures — is confirmed in the span of one night. But the truth is not delivered through exposition. It is discovered. Piece by piece. In documents. In files. In the quiet voice of a woman reading her own experiment log.

Evan and Evelyn enter this chapter on opposite sides. They leave it as allies. Between those two points: an infiltration, a revelation, a chase, and a choice.

Evan investigates a Church facility in the forest — the same type of Small Lab where Evelyn was transformed. Evelyn tracks him there, knowing what awaits people who discover the Church's secrets. They converge inside the facility. Together they find the records: the Church creates the monsters it claims to fight. Evelyn reads her own file — Subject E-V-7 — and learns the full scope of what was done to her. The Church's response arrives. Veyss marks Evan as a traitor. Evelyn saves him. Two new party members are freed: Silas, a healer held against his will, and Kaelen, a Church guard who defects when he sees the truth.

The chapter ends in a forest camp. Firelight. Exhaustion. Two people who have chosen each other over the institutions that made them.

## 2. Player Fantasy

### As Evan — The Investigator
The player experiences Evan's transformation from true believer to shattered investigator. He enters the archives confident in his training. He cross-references documents with the same competence that made him a good hunter. Each discovery chips away at his certainty. By the time he reads the creation program overview, the player feels his world fracturing — not with drama, but with the quiet horror of evidence that cannot be un-read.

### As Evelyn — The Pursuer
The player controls Evelyn in a time-sensitive pursuit through the forest. She is not here for revenge. She is here because she knows what the Church does to people who find the truth, and she cannot let it happen again. The urgency is the enemy. Every combat encounter is an obstacle to push through, not a challenge to savor. The player should feel Evelyn's determination: *I have to reach him before it is too late.*

### As Both — The Discoverers
When Evan and Evelyn converge inside the facility, the player experiences the horror of documentation. Every room tells a story of suffering. Every file is a life reduced to a number. Evelyn reading her own file is the emotional apex — not rage, but the hollow recognition of a wound confirmed. Evan reading the creation program is the intellectual apex — the moment every clue clicks into a pattern he cannot unsee.

### As Evelyn — The Protector
The Church response forces Evelyn and Evan to fight together for the first time. The player controls Evelyn with Evan as AI ally. This is the first taste of the party dynamic that will define the rest of the game. The combat should feel different from every previous encounter — not just fighting alongside someone, but fighting *for* them.

### As Both — The Allies
The final camp scene is the emotional resolution. The adrenaline fades. The weight settles. Evelyn offers partnership, not command. Evan accepts, not out of obligation but out of earned conviction. The player should feel that something fundamental has changed: the story has shifted from mystery to mission.

## 3. Narrative Arc

### 3.1 Evan's Investigation (Minutes 0-16)

Evan returns from Oakhaven with his Chapter 3 report filed. But the mercy Evelyn showed him has not stopped working on him. A monster would have killed him. She did not. Every doctrine he was taught says monsters kill. She broke the rule.

He begins researching in the Church archives, cross-referencing his Chapter 1 findings with archived facility locations. The detector readings, the strange energy signatures, the Church's deflections — the pieces align. The anomalies point to a Small Lab hidden in the forest, disguised as an abandoned chapel.

Evan makes his choice. He leaves his Church insignia on his desk — symbolic, deliberate, the first break he does not yet fully understand. He departs in secret.

He infiltrates the facility's outer area. Stealth-focused gameplay. He finds experiment records that confirm his worst fears: the Church creates magical creatures. His voice throughout is measured but increasingly strained. Questions replace statements. Shorter sentences. The formal structure of his training is cracking.

### 3.2 Evelyn's Pursuit (Minutes 16-30)

Evelyn's enhanced senses pick up Church activity in the forest — unusual energy concentrations, increased patrol activity. She recognizes the signature pattern: a Small Lab is active. And the Church hunter is walking straight into it.

She knows what happens to people who discover the Church's experiments. They do not leave.

She pursues him. The player controls Evelyn in a time-sensitive sequence. She fights through a Church patrol and a Church-made creature — obstacles, not challenges. The urgency is the real enemy. Along the way, her internal monologue reveals her motivation: she is not here for revenge. She is here because someone is in danger and she cannot stand by.

She reaches the Small Lab. She can sense Evan inside. And she can sense the Church's response approaching — a full squad, minutes away.

### 3.3 The Discovery (Minutes 30-43)

Evan and Evelyn converge inside the facility. Their initial encounter is tense — weapons drawn, neither striking. The moment passes. They explore together.

The records room is the heart of the chapter. What they find is devastating:

- The Church's creation program documentation
- Subject lists, transformation logs, termination records
- Essence harvesting procedures, control seal research
- And one specific file: Subject E-V-7. Evelyn's file.

Evelyn reads her own file. She learns the details — the essence sources, the Mage's essence used in her transformation, the Control Litany, the seal failure. She always knew she was experimented on. Now she knows exactly how.

Evan reads the broader records. The Church creates monsters, deploys them to villages, then sends knights to "protect" those villages. The entire system is manufactured. His life's work was a lie.

Evelyn tells Evan her story in full. Human, cursed, captured, transformed. The Control Litany failed. She escaped. The most detailed dialogue sequence in the chapter.

### 3.4 The Church Response (Minutes 43-57)

Alarms trigger. The Church has detected the breach. A full response squad arrives.

Inquisitor Veyss communicates remotely — cold, clinical, ordering the facility's defense. Veyss recognizes Evelyn: "Subject E-V-7. Reacquisition priority." Veyss marks Evan as a traitor for unauthorized access.

Evelyn and Evan must escape together. Chase sequence through facility corridors. The first time they fight as allies — not against each other, but alongside each other.

They break through the Church squad at the exit. They escape into the forest.

### 3.5 The Alliance (Minutes 57-70)

They reach a safe distance. Stop. Catch their breath. The reality of what they found and what they are now settles in.

Evan processes his shattered faith. He does not rage — he goes quiet, methodical, asking questions. Evelyn is gentle but honest. She does not soften the truth, but she does not let him drown in it.

Evan makes his choice: he is done with the Church. Permanently. He removes his Church insignia completely.

Evelyn offers him a place at her side. Not as a servant. As a partner. He accepts.

Two new party members join: Silas, freed from a restraint chamber where he was forced to treat experiment subjects, and Kaelen, a Church guard who witnessed the truth and chose to defect.

The chapter ends with the first quiet moment as a team.

## 5. Dialogue Sequences

### SEQUENCE: ch4_evelyn_story — The Full Backstory Reveal

This is the most important dialogue sequence in the chapter. Evelyn reveals her entire backstory to Evan: human, cursed by the Cat, captured by the Church, transformed into a vampire, control seal failure, escape. The sequence is multi-node, played in the records room after Evelyn has read her file. Evan has the broader Church records in his hands. The evidence supports everything she says.

**Setting**: Records room. Fluorescent cold light. Filing cabinets open. Evelyn's file — Subject E-V-7 — open on the table between them. Both are standing. Neither is sitting. This is not a casual conversation.

**Emotional Arc**: Guarded → Direct → Vulnerable → Resolute. Evelyn starts protective of herself and opens as the sequence progresses. Evan's responses shift from investigator to witness to ally.

---

**[ch4_evelyn_story_01]** `EVELYN` — [Records room, looking at her file, voice controlled but tight]

> "Subject E-V-7. That is me. I am a number in their ledger."

**Notes:** She reads her own file number. The dehumanization hits first. Her voice is steady but there is a wire underneath.

**[ch4_evelyn_story_02]** `EVAN` — [Standing across from her, holding Church records. Voice careful.]

> "Evelyn. Tell me. In your words. Not theirs."

**Notes:** Evan gives her agency over her own story. He does not interrogate. He asks. This matters.

### Camera Direction Standards

All Chapter 4 cutscenes follow these camera conventions:

| Shot Type | Use | Duration | Transition |
|-----------|-----|----------|------------|
| Wide two-shot | Establishing both characters | 3-5s | Slow push |
| Medium close-up | Emotional beats | 4-6s | Cut or slow pan |
| Over-shoulder | Perspective shifts | 3-4s | Cut |
| Object close-up | Documents, vials, insignia | 2-3s | Cut back to face |
| Slow pull-back | Chapter endings | 5-10s | Continuous |

**Forbidden**: Dutch angles, shaky cam, whip pans. Chapter 4 is about clarity emerging from confusion. The camera reflects this: steady, deliberate, never chaotic even during action.

**POV Switch Rule**: When the POV switches from Evan to Evelyn during gameplay, the camera field-of-view changes subtly: Evelyn's FOV is 5 degrees wider (reflecting her enhanced spatial awareness). The transition is a 0.5s crossfade, not a hard cut.

## 9. Emotional Progression Notes

### Evan's Emotional Map — Chapter 4

| Beat | Emotional State | Voice Characteristics | Physical Tells |
|------|----------------|----------------------|----------------|
| Archives research | Focused unease | Formal but questioning | Grip tightens on detector |
| Decision to depart | Resolve | Short sentences, direct | Removes insignia from desk (not chest) |
| Forest trail | Anticipation + dread | Hunting jargon returns | Detector held tighter |
| Facility infiltration | Tension | Terse. Statements, not questions. | Scanning constantly |
| Discovery — records | Shock | Sentences fragment | Hands shake (visible) |
| Evelyn's story | Processing | Questions. Shorter sentences. | Looks at Evelyn, not files |
| Veyss communication | Betrayal | Voice cracks. Quieter, not louder. | Touches insignia on chest |
| Church response | Action | Direct. No hesitation. | Draws weapon. Ready. |
| Evelyn saves him | Gratitude + grief | Barely above whisper | Removes insignia fully |
| Camp alliance | Resolve | Warm. Contractions return. | Hands bare. Relaxed posture. |
| Chapter end | New purpose | Steady. Clear. | Firelight on open face |

**Key Rule**: Evan's anger is always quiet. The angrier he gets, the quieter his voice. When he is truly shattered (reading the creation program), his sentences fragment. When he rebuilds (camp scene), his sentences become whole again but warmer — the formality is gone.

### Evelyn's Emotional Map — Chapter 4

| Beat | Emotional State | Voice Characteristics | Physical Tells |
|------|----------------|----------------------|----------------|
| Detects lab | Urgency | Direct. Imperatives. | Ears forward. Tail still. |
| Pursuit | Determination | Short. Action-focused. | Running. No deflection. |
| Convergence with Evan | Caution | Guarded. Measuring. | Ears tracking. Tail neutral. |
| Reading her file | Hollow recognition | Quiet. Factual. | Touches ears. Tail flicks. |
| Telling her story | Guarded → Open | Starts controlled, opens gradually | Tail loosens progressively |
| Church response | Protectiveness | Combat voice. Clear. | Eyes glow. Ears forward. |
| Saves Evan | Resolve | "I am here." Direct. | Between Evan and threat. |
| Camp alliance | Vulnerable warmth | Genuine. No deflection. | Tail curls — the happy thing. |
| Chapter end | Connection | Quiet. Content. | Ears relaxed. Tail loose. |

**Key Rule**: Evelyn deflects less in Chapter 4 than in any previous chapter. The truth is too important for humor here. Her tail betrays her throughout — the player should learn to read it. When it curls loosely, she is happy. When it is still, she is resolved. When it flicks, she is agitated.

### Veyss's Emotional Map — Chapter 4

| Beat | Emotional State | Voice Characteristics |
|------|----------------|----------------------|
| Facility alert | Efficiency | Flat. No urgency. Just process. |
| Identifies Evan | Classification | Like reading a barcode. |
| Marks Evan traitor | Procedure | Not personal. Just the next step. |
| Recognizes Evelyn | Clinical interest | "Subject E-V-7." A number. |

**Key Rule**: Veyss never shows emotion. Not surprise, not anger, not fear. They are the most dangerous person in the Church precisely because they feel nothing. Their voice is always the same: cold, clipped, efficient.

### Silas's Emotional Map — Chapter 4 (Introduction)

| Beat | Emotional State | Voice Characteristics | Physical Tells |
|------|----------------|----------------------|----------------|
| Found treating patients | Focused care | Short. Medical vocabulary. | Hands steady on patient. |
| Recognizes Evelyn | No surprise | Warm. "You are bleeding." | Reaches for bandages, not weapons. |
| Offered freedom | Considered | Quiet. Names the choice. | Looks at patients first. |
| Joins the party | Purpose renewed | Soft. "I have been alone." | Takes his satchel. |

**Key Rule**: Silas's first priority is always the patient. He will finish treating someone before he answers a question about himself. His hands are his emotional barometer — steady means controlled, trembling means at the edge.

### Kaelen's Emotional Map — Chapter 4 (Introduction)

| Beat | Emotional State | Voice Characteristics | Physical Tells |
|------|----------------|----------------------|----------------|
| Blocking corridor | Duty | Formal. No contractions. | Shield up. Stance solid. |
| Processes the truth | Internal reckoning | Slower. Pauses between sentences. | Thumb on shield crack. |
| Lowers shield | Choice | First contractions appear. | Shield lowers. Steps aside. |
| Follows the party | Uncertainty + hope | Dry observation. "That is unfamiliar." | Shoulders drop slightly. |

**Key Rule**: Kaelen's defection is physical before it is verbal. The shield lowers first. The words follow. His body language is his first language — decades of military training make it involuntary.

### Cross-Character Emotional Beats

The chapter has three shared emotional peaks:

1. **Evelyn's file reading**: Both characters are present. Evelyn is reading about herself. Evan is reading about what his Church did to her. The shared silence is the beat. No dialogue needed beyond "Subject E-V-7. That is me."

2. **The insignia removal**: Evan removes his Church insignia. Evelyn witnesses it. Silas and Kaelen may or may not be present depending on trigger order, but if they are, they each react in character: Silas nods quietly (recognition of someone choosing to stop serving a harmful system), Kaelen looks at his own discarded insignia in his pocket and touches it.

3. **The camp fire**: All four characters. The first ensemble emotional beat. Evelyn built the fire for Evan. Silas sits heavily. Kaelen stands watch until told to sit. Evan asks "are we a team?" The answer is yes.
