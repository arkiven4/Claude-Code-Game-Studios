# The Church — The Sacred Cross

**Canon Level**: Established (core canon)
**Dependencies**: None (foundational faction)
**Used In**: Prologue, Chapter 1, Chapter 3, Chapter 4, Chapter 5, Chapter 6, Chapter 7

---

## Overview

The Sacred Cross — known to the public simply as "the Church" — presents itself as humanity's righteous shield against the monstrous. It hunts magical creatures, protects villages, and preaches the sanctity of human life. This is not entirely a lie. The Church genuinely believes in its own righteousness. But beneath its holy facade lies a darker truth: the Church *creates* the very monsters it claims to fight. Through clandestine experimentation, it transforms captured humans and harvested magical essence into controllable magical creatures, then deploys them to manufacture crises that justify its own expanding power. The Church does not hunt monsters to save the world. It creates monsters to control it.

## Detailed Lore

### The Public Face

To the common villager, the Church is a blessing. Its knights patrol trade roads, clearing out beasts that would otherwise devour travelers. Its priests bless crops, tend the sick, and preach that humanity is special — chosen by the divine to steward the world. When a magical creature attacks, the Church arrives. When a village needs protection, the Church sends a garrison.

The Church's public doctrine is simple and emotionally resonant:

> *"The world was made for humanity. Monsters are a corruption of that design — an aberration that must be cleansed. We are the hand that does the cleansing."*

This doctrine is not propaganda in the cynical sense. The average Church knight believes it. The Bishop who delivers sermons believes it. The villagers who donate their copper coins to the Church's collection plate believe it. The Church has spent generations building genuine goodwill, and that goodwill is real.

That is what makes the deception so effective.

### The True Nature

The Church's leadership — a closed circle of senior clerics and researchers — discovered centuries ago that magical creatures could be *created*, not merely found. By harvesting the residual magical energy left behind when a magical creature dies and combining it with a living human subject, they could produce a new magical creature. With the right ritual imprints, this creature would bear a **control seal** — a magical chain binding it to the Church's will.

The logic was cold but, to them, pragmatic:

1. Magical creatures are powerful. Power is useful.
2. Wild magical creatures are unpredictable. Controlled ones are not.
3. If the Church controls all magical creatures, the Church controls every threat to humanity — and therefore controls humanity itself.
4. Villages that need protection are villages that owe the Church. Manufacture the threat, and the village will beg for the shield.

The Church does not see itself as evil. It sees itself as **responsible**. Someone must manage the world's magical ecosystem, and humanity — guided by the Church — is the only force capable of doing so responsibly. The fact that this requires torturing humans and creating monsters is, to them, an unfortunate necessity. A surgeon cuts flesh to heal the body. The Church cuts morality to save the species.

### How Experimentation Works

The Church's creation process follows three stages:

**Stage 1: Essence Harvesting.** When a magical creature dies, its body dissolves into ambient magical energy over several hours. The Church developed techniques to *capture* this energy before it dissipates, storing it in crystalline vessels called **essence vials**. The potency of a vial depends on the creature's power level. The Mage's execution (see [Witch and Mage Lore](witch-and-mage-lore.md)) produced the most concentrated essence the Church has ever harvested — enough to power dozens of experiments.

**Stage 2: Subject Preparation.** A living human subject is restrained and prepared for transformation. The Church sources subjects from prisoners, captured enemies, kidnapped villagers, and — in their most ambitious experiments — willing volunteers who believe they are receiving a holy blessing. The subject's magical resistance is suppressed through a combination of physical restraints inscribed with nullification runes and chemical sedatives derived from Weald plants.

**Stage 3: Infusion and Sealing.** Essence is introduced into the subject's bloodstream through a ritual process. The Church's ritualists chant the **Control Litany** — a precise sequence of magical commands that imprint a control seal onto the subject's soul as the transformation occurs. If successful, the subject becomes a magical creature that obeys the Church. If the seal fails, the creature is uncontrollable and must be terminated.

### Key Figures

**Inquisitor Veyss** — The Church's chief researcher and Evelyn's captor. Veyss is not sadistic. Sadism implies enjoyment of suffering. Veyss views suffering as a data point: sometimes necessary, never personal. They speak in clinical terms about transformation processes, refer to subjects by number rather than name, and have never expressed remorse. Veyss is the most dangerous person in the Church not because they are powerful (though they have significant magical ability), but because they are *right* about how the world works and *wrong* about what that means.

**Bishop Aldric** — The Church's public voice. Aldric is a genuinely good man defending a terrible institution. He preaches compassion, visits plague villages, and personally led the defense of three villages against wild magical creatures. He does not know about the experiments. If he did, he would be the Church's greatest internal threat. The Conclave keeps him carefully managed — given real victories against real monsters so that his faith remains unshakeable.

**The Pontiff** — Never appears in-game. May not even exist. The Pontiff is a mythic figurehead invoked in prayers and official documents but never seen. Some within the Church believe the Pontiff is dead and the Conclave rules in their name. Others believe the Pontiff is a council pretending to be a person. The truth is deliberately left unknown.

### Tracking and Capture

The Church tracks magical creatures through several methods:

- **Essence Resonance Detectors:** Devices that pulse when magical energy of a certain frequency is nearby. The Church plants these in villages and along trade routes. They can detect active magical creatures but cannot distinguish between Church-made and wild creatures.
- **Informant Networks:** Missionary priests, traveling merchants, and paid villagers report unusual sightings. The Church maintains a vast, decentralized intelligence network.
- **The Hunt:** When a creature is detected, the Church dispatches a knight squad to investigate, confirm, and either eliminate or capture the target. Capture is preferred for creatures with rare or useful abilities.
- **The Recollection:** A ritual performed quarterly at each Church facility. All knights and priests submit to a magical scan that detects exposure to uncontrolled magical energy. This is how the Church identifies defectors and ensures loyalty.

Evan's detector (the device he uses in Chapter 1 to track magical energy) is a Church-issued Essence Resonance Detector — standard equipment for every knight. The strange readings he finds are evidence of Church tampering, though he does not yet know this.

### Facility Types

The Church operates three tiers of facilities:

**Small Labs (Tier 1):** Hidden installations disguised as abandoned buildings, rural chapels, or waystations. Each contains a single experimentation chamber, enough equipment for 2-3 simultaneous transformations, and a small security detail. Most villagers do not know these exist. Evelyn was held in a Small Lab before her escape.

**Major Facilities (Tier 2):** Larger installations with full research wings, multiple experimentation chambers, essence storage vaults, and garrison-level security. These are the Church's primary production centers. They are located in remote areas — deep forests, mountain valleys, abandoned mine networks. Party raids target these in Chapters 5 and 6.

**The Stronghold (Tier 3):** The Church's headquarters — "The Cross" — is a massive fortress-monastery that serves as both a public cathedral and the Conclave's private seat. It contains the Church's central essence vault, the primary ritual chamber, and the living quarters of the Conclave. Its defenses are formidable: multiple layers of walls, essence-dampening fields, and the Church's elite guard. The assault on The Cross is the climax of Chapter 7.

## Mechanical Implications

- **Church facilities** serve as dungeon instances throughout the game, scaling in complexity from Small Labs (mini-dungeons) to The Stronghold (major multi-stage raid).
- **Essence Resonance Detectors** are the in-world justification for Evan's magical energy detection gameplay mechanic.
- **Control seals** explain why Church-made magical creatures behave differently from wild ones — they may exhibit glitchy, constrained behavior patterns, or break free mid-combat as the seal fails.
- **The Church's dual nature** means Church enemies include both genuine believers (knights who fight with conviction) and cold functionaries (researchers who flee or call for help).
- **Bishop Aldric** serves as a recurring mid-game antagonist who the player may initially hate but later pities when the truth is revealed.
