# Magical Creatures — Taxonomy and Nature

**Canon Level**: Established (core canon)
**Dependencies**: [Cat Magical Beast Lore](cat-magical-beast-lore.md), [Church Lore](church-lore.md)
**Used In**: Chapter 1, Chapter 4, Chapter 6, Chapter 11, Epilogue

---

## Overview

Magical creatures in this world fall into three categories: **Primal Beasts** (ancient, self-originating entities like the Cat), **Natural Magical Creatures** (creatures born from the world's ambient magical energy through spontaneous generation), and **Church-Made Creatures** (humans or animals transformed through the Church's experimentation). All magical creatures share one defining trait: they are animated by magical energy rather than purely biological processes, and when that energy is removed from the world, they cease to exist. This is the mechanism behind the game's ending — the Witch's plan to eliminate all magical creatures is not genocide. It is a fundamental rewriting of the world's rules, and Evelyn, as a magical creature, cannot survive it.

## Detailed Lore

### Taxonomy

Magical creatures are classified by their **origin** and their **form**. These two axes determine their behavior, their power level, and — critically — what happens when they die.

#### By Origin

**Primal Beasts:**
- Self-originating entities that emerged from the world's magical substrate during the age before human civilization.
- Cannot be created, killed, or reproduced by any known means.
- Operate on alien logic that does not map to human morality.
- Examples: the Cat Magical Beast.
- Estimated population: Single digits. Possibly only one per generation.
- **Death behavior:** They do not die. If one were to cease existing, the energy released would be cataclysmic.

**Natural Magical Creatures:**
- Born through **spontaneous generation** — the process by which ambient magical energy coalesces into living form in areas of high magical density.
- This is a natural process, like a spring emerging from underground water. The Church's presence and experimentation has dramatically increased the rate of spontaneous generation by disturbing magical ley lines and releasing uncontained essence.
- Range from mindless beasts to sentient humanoids.
- Reproduce through a combination of biological reproduction (for humanoid types) and continued spontaneous generation (for beast types).
- Examples: forest wraiths, shadow wolves, crystal-backed tortoises, goblin-like trickster spirits.
- **Death behavior:** When a natural magical creature dies, its body dissolves over several hours, releasing its magical energy back into the environment. This energy dissipates naturally unless captured by Church harvesting techniques.

**Church-Made Creatures:**
- Humans or animals transformed through the Church's essence infusion process.
- Bear a **control seal** (or the scar of a failed seal) that marks them as artificial.
- Exhibit behavioral quirks not found in natural creatures: compulsive obedience to Church authority figures, inability to leave designated territories, or — in seal-failure cases — aggressive instability.
- Examples: the monsters Evan hunts in Chapter 1, the creatures that attack villages (many of which the Church deployed deliberately).
- **Death behavior:** Same as natural creatures — body dissolves, energy releases. However, Church-made creatures release *concentrated* essence, making their deaths more valuable to the Church's harvesting program. This creates a perverse incentive for the Church to send creatures to their deaths.

**Cursed Humans:**
- Humans who have been partially transformed by magical forces but retain their human consciousness and autonomy.
- Evelyn is the only known cursed human in the game. Her curse by the Cat Magical Beast made her part magical beast, and the subsequent Church vampire experiment made her part vampire — but neither transformation overwrote her human identity.
- Cursed humans exist in an **ontological gray area**: they are magical creatures by the world's definition, but they retain human psychology, memories, and relationships.
- **Death behavior:** Unknown. No cursed human has ever died naturally. If one were to die, the question of whether their energy would dissipate like a magical creature's or remain like a human's is unanswered — and the Witch's plan ensures it never will be.

#### By Form

**Beasts:** Animal-like magical creatures. Range from wolf-sized to building-sized. Typically non-sentient or minimally sentient. Driven by instinct, hunger, and territorial behavior. Most common type encountered by Church knights.

**Humanoids:** Human-shaped magical creatures. Often sentient, sometimes intelligent. May mimic human behavior, language, or social structures. Some are hostile, some are indifferent, a rare few are benevolent. The Church's propaganda conflates all humanoids with "monsters that wear human skin."

**Elementals:** Creatures composed primarily of a single element (fire, shadow, crystal, water). Rare and dangerous. Often tied to specific geographic locations where their element is concentrated.

**Hybrids:** Creatures that combine traits from multiple categories. Evelyn is a hybrid: human + cat beast (curse) + vampire (Church experiment). The Church has attempted to create other hybrids but lacks the Primal Beast connection that made Evelyn's case unique.

### Magical Energy Signatures

Every magical creature emits a **magical energy signature** — a detectable resonance that identifies its presence, type, and power level. Evan's detector reads these signatures.

The signature has three components:

1. **Frequency** — Identifies the creature's type. Beasts hum low, humanoids resonate at mid-range, elementals pulse at variable frequencies. Evan learns to recognize these patterns through experience.
2. **Intensity** — Measures the creature's power. A shadow wolf might register as a faint glow; a Primal Beast would overload the detector entirely.
3. **Purity** — The most diagnostically useful metric. Natural creatures have "clean" signatures — smooth, organic waveforms. Church-made creatures have "dirty" signatures — jagged, irregular patterns where the control seal interferes with the natural magical flow. This is what Evan notices in Chapter 1: *"This magical energy... it's not natural."* He is detecting the artifact of Church experimentation without yet understanding what it means.

Evelyn's signature is unique: a layered, tripartite resonance that combines human baseline, cat-beast frequency, and vampire intensity. It is unlike anything the Church's detectors have ever recorded, which is one reason they lost track of her after her escape.

### What Happens When a Magical Creature Dies

The death of a magical creature follows a consistent pattern:

1. **Cessation:** The creature's animating magical energy stops cycling. Biologically, this is equivalent to cardiac arrest — the engine stops.
2. **Dissolution:** The physical body begins to break down. Unlike biological decomposition (which takes days or weeks), magical dissolution takes hours. The body becomes translucent, then luminous, then fragments into floating particles of light.
3. **Release:** The magical energy returns to the environment as ambient magical resonance. It disperses naturally, thinning into the world's background magical field.
4. **Remainder:** Nothing physical remains. No bones, no ashes, no corpse. The creature existed, and then it did not. This is unsettling to witness, especially for humans who expect death to leave evidence.

The Church interrupts this process by capturing the released energy during Stage 3 (Release) using essence vials. This is how they build up the magical fuel for new experiments. Every creature the Church kills is a resource to be harvested.

### The Vanishing Phenomenon

The Witch's endgame — and the game's ending — is the **Vanishing**: the complete elimination of magical energy from the world.

**Mechanism:** The Witch has spent a decade developing a ritual of unprecedented scale. Where the Church's experiments manipulate existing magical energy, the Witch's ritual **severs the world's connection to the magical substrate entirely**. Think of magical energy as water flowing from an underground source. The Church builds dams and pipes to control the flow. The Witch is draining the aquifer.

**Execution:** The ritual is tied to the Witch's own life force. She is the anchor, the conductor, the living circuit through which the world's magical energy is channeled and dissolved. When she dies — whether by natural causes or by being killed — the ritual completes automatically. Her death is not a failure condition; it is the **trigger**.

**Effect:** All magical energy in the world dissipates simultaneously. Every magical creature — Primal Beasts, natural creatures, Church-made creatures, and cursed humans like Evelyn — ceases to exist. Their bodies dissolve. Their energy returns to nothing, because there is no longer a magical substrate for it to return to. It simply *ends*.

**Scope:** The Vanishing is global and irreversible. Once the magical substrate is severed, it cannot be reconnected. The world becomes purely physical — no magic, no magical creatures, no curses, no blessings. A world like ours.

**The Tragedy:** The Witch's goal and the game's ending are the **same event**. Defeating the Witch does not stop the Vanishing. Defeating the Witch *completes* it. The player cannot save Evelyn and defeat the Witch. These are not two options — they are one. The Witch knew this. Evelyn suspected it. Evan did not, and the realization destroys him.

**Why "She Was Right, Just Wrong About the Method":** The Witch's assessment was correct: the Church's experimentation was corrupting the world's magical ecosystem, creating suffering on an industrial scale. Magical creatures *were* being born into a world that treated them as resources. The Church's control seals *were* an atrocity. Her diagnosis was right. Her cure — total erasure — was the wrong method. She chose to burn the garden because the weeds were choking the flowers.

## Mechanical Implications

- **Magical energy signatures** justify Evan's detection mechanic and provide the player with a visual/audio feedback system for identifying creature types.
- **The dissolution effect** on creature death is a core visual VFX — glowing particles, fading body, no corpse. This reinforces the "they were never truly physical" nature of magical creatures.
- **The Vanishing** is the ending mechanic. It is not a boss mechanic or a gameplay system — it is a narrative inevitability that the chapter 11 cutscenes dramatize.
- **Evelyn's unique signature** can be referenced by Church detectors as a gameplay element — she may trigger alarms differently than other creatures, or not at all in certain configurations.
- **Church-made vs. natural creatures** can have different enemy behaviors in combat: Church-made creatures may exhibit seal-failure states (berserk, confused, fleeing) that create dynamic combat scenarios.
