# Narrative Design: Prologue — "The Curse," "The Small Lab," and "The Day the Sun Goes Cold"

> **Status**: Draft
> **POV**: Evelyn (human -> cat-cursed -> vampire), The Witch (flashback)
> **Location**: Deep Weald -> Church Small Lab -> Witch's Cabin / Church Outpost
> **Timeline**: Present day (Evelyn) / 10 years before Chapter 1 (Witch)
> **Duration**: 15-20 minutes total playtime

---

## 1. Overview

The Prologue is a three-part opening sequence that establishes the entire emotional and narrative foundation of *My Vampire*. Part One ("The Curse") follows Evelyn as a normal human who stumbles into the Deep Weald, disrespects the Cat Magical Beast through genuine disbelief rather than malice, and is cursed — gaining cat ears, a tail, and abilities she does not understand. Before she can process what has happened, the Church captures her. Part Two ("The Small Lab") depicts Evelyn's imprisonment and forced transformation into a vampire by Inquisitor Veyss. The Control Litany fails because her half-cat nature creates a magical superposition the seal cannot resolve. She shatters free and escapes into the night. Part Three ("The Day the Sun Goes Cold") shifts ten years backward. The player IS the Witch during the most devastating day of her life — the day the Church takes the Mage. They experience her love, her pursuit, her fury, and her vow. Together, these three sequences establish: Evelyn's origin, the Church's cruelty, the Witch's tragedy, and the emotional stakes that make Chapter 11 devastating.

---

## 2. Player Fantasy

**Loss of Control, Then Power Without Consent.** The player begins as Evelyn — ordinary, frightened, and increasingly powerless. The Weald confuses her. The Cat curses her. The Church captures her. Every moment strips away agency. Then, in the lab, she is given terrifying power — but it was forced upon her. She did not choose this. The escape sequence should feel incredible and horrifying at once: the thrill of vampire abilities mixed with the knowledge that they were carved into her without consent.

**The Untameable Grief.** When the Witch Prologue begins, the fantasy shifts. The player IS the most powerful entity in the game. But this power is born from devastation. The domestic opening is the most peaceful three minutes in the entire game — and its destruction is what makes the Witch's vow land. The player should feel catharsis during combat, then hollowness when it matters most. They should understand, viscerally, why she chooses what she chooses.

By the time the title screen appears, the player should be exhausted, emotionally invested, and dreading Chapter 11 — because they know who the Witch is, and they know who Evelyn will become to her.

## 3. Narrative Arc

### Section 1: "The Curse" — Evelyn in the Deep Weald

**Emotional trajectory**: Curiosity -> Confusion -> Alien unease -> Body horror -> Wonder -> Helplessness

| Beat | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 1. Entry | Evelyn flees into the Deep Weald. The reason is deliberately vague — bandits, storm, wrong turn. The Weald itself is the focus. Reality warps immediately. |
| 2. The Loop | Evelyn walks a path that returns to its own beginning. The environment changes subtly on the second pass — teaching the player that the Weald rewards observation. |
| 3. The Cat Appears | The Cat Magical Beast manifests in its clearing. It does not attack. It observes. Evelyn, a rational human facing something impossible, laughs — not cruelly, from disbelief. |
| 4. The Curse | The Cat does not punish her. It imposes consequence. Its layered voice speaks a riddle that is not a riddle but a literal observation. Evelyn's body changes. |
| 5. Transformation | Ears burst through her hair. A tail emerges from her spine. Her senses expand beyond human range. She is terrified and fascinated. |
| 6. Discovery | Brief tutorial sequence. Evelyn learns High Jump (clearing a fallen log), Wall Crawl (scaling a root wall), and Enhanced Senses (hearing distant Church knights). |
| 7. Ambush | Before she can flee the Weald, Church knights arrive. They recognized the magical signature. Evelyn fights with her new abilities but is overwhelmed. Sedated. Captured. |

### Section 2: "The Small Lab" — Evelyn's Imprisonment

**Emotional trajectory**: Disorientation -> Dread -> Violation -> Shattering -> Rage -> Flight

| Beat | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 1. Awakening | Evelyn wakes restrained in a cold stone lab. Essence vials glow on shelves. Inquisitor Veyss speaks — no emotion, only data. |
| 2. The Transformation | Essence infusion begins. The archetype catalyst is administered. Evelyn's body transforms — pale skin, red-tinged eyes, elongated canines. She feels power surge through her. |
| 3. The Control Litany | Veyss chants the litany. A seal forms visually over Evelyn's chest — and shatters. Her half-cat nature creates interference the seal cannot resolve. Veyss: "Fascinating." |
| 4. Termination Order | Veyss calmly orders termination. Evelyn — newly vampire, enraged, and free — breaks her restraints. |
| 5. Escape | Brief playable sequence. Evelyn fights through 2-3 disoriented guards with full vampire abilities. This is power fantasy mixed with terror. |
| 6. Flight | Evelyn bursts through the lab door into the night. She runs. She does not stop. Transition to black. |

### Section 3: "The Day the Sun Goes Cold" — The Witch Prologue

**Emotional trajectory**: Peace -> Warmth -> Devastation -> Pursuit -> Catharsis -> Conflict -> Hollow victory -> Vow

| Beat | Description |
|------|-------------|
| 1. Domestic Morning | The player is the Witch. Morning in the cabin. The Mage is there. They debate magic over tea. This is the only truly safe, warm space in the entire game. |
| 2. Separation | The Mage leaves to teach in a nearby village. The Witch goes to the Weald to gather herbs. Normal departure. Nothing ominous. |
| 3. Discovery | She returns to find the cabin ransacked. The Mage's broken staff on the floor. Her voice changes. |
| 4. Pursuit | Tracking sequence through the Weald. The Witch follows magical residue. Increasing urgency. The path is a montage of running, sensing, desperate hope. |
| 5. The Outpost | She arrives at the Church outpost as the Knight Captain prepares to move the prisoner. Combat begins. |
| 6. Catharsis | The Witch tears through Church defenses. Her power is overwhelming. The player should feel unstoppable — this is not a hard fight, it is grief weaponized. |
| 7. Knight Captain | Boss confrontation. The Knight Captain fights with conviction. He believes he is right. He is a good man doing what he believes is right. He dies believing it. |
| 8. Too Late | She reaches the Mage's cell. The extraction has damaged him beyond recovery. His last words: an apology. |
| 9. The Harvesting | She watches from a distance as the Church completes the extraction posthumously. Essence harvested. Stored. Labeled. |
| 10. The Vow | Standing in the burning ruins, she makes her vow. Camera pulls back. Silence. Then: 10 years forward. Title card: MY VAMPIRE. |

## 4. Cutscene Scripts

### Section 1: "The Curse"

---

#### [prologue_weald_entry] — Evelyn enters the Deep Weald

**[WLD_ENTRY_01]** `EVELYN` — Cutscene: Evelyn pushes through dense foliage into the Weald. Camera follows from behind, then tilts up to show the spiraling tree trunks. Light filters wrong.

> "The path should curve left. It went right."

**[WLD_ENTRY_02]** `EVELYN` — Camera pans to show a shadow pointing toward a light source that cannot cast it.

> "That shadow— that is not possible."

**[WLD_ENTRY_03]** `EVELYN` — She takes a cautious step forward. The air feels thick, like walking through warm water.

> "The air tastes like copper and old leaves."

**[WLD_ENTRY_04]** `EVELYN` — Internal monologue, voice-over. Camera slowly pushes in on her face.

> "I should turn back. But the storm was worse behind me."

**[WLD_ENTRY_05]** `EVELYN` — She squares her shoulders and walks deeper in. A flower blooms in a color that has no name.

> "Well. Forward it is."

**Notes:** The color of the flower should be described through UI text, not dialogue — something like "a violet that hums." This establishes the Weald's reality-warping immediately. Camera should feel slightly tilted, never quite level.

---

#### [prologue_weald_loop] — Environmental trigger, no dialogue

**Implementation:** Player walks a path marked by a distinctive split tree trunk. After ~20 seconds of walking, they arrive back at the same split tree — but now a branch that was whole is snapped. The player should realize they have walked in a loop before the game tells them.

**[WLD_LOOP_01]** `EVELYN` — Triggered when player recognizes the loop (returns to split tree a second time).

> "I walked straight. I know I did."

**[WLD_LOOP_02]** `EVELYN` — She looks at the snapped branch that was previously whole.

> "The tree changed. Or I did."

**Notes:** No cutscene — this is environmental storytelling during gameplay. The snapped branch is the clue. If the player does not notice it on the first pass, the second pass makes it obvious.

---

#### [prologue_cat_encounter] — The Cat Magical Beast appears

**[CAT_ENC_01]** `EVELYN` — Cutscene: Evelyn enters a clearing. An ancient tree dominates the center. At its base, bones lie scattered — not prey bones, but the remains of things that were changed. The Cat appears on a root. It is beautiful and wrong.

> "That is... a cat."

**[CAT_ENC_02]** `EVELYN` — She blinks. The Cat has too many eyes, or the right number in the wrong arrangement. Its fur shifts like water.

> "A very large, very strange cat."

**[CAT_ENC_03]** `EVELYN` — She laughs. Not cruelly — the laugh of someone whose mind cannot accept what it sees.

> "I must be more lost than I thought."

**[CAT_ENC_04]** `CAT_BEAST` — The Cat speaks. Its voice is layered — multiple versions of the same sound spoken simultaneously, echoing. Camera subtly vibrates.

> "You see and you refuse to see."

**[CAT_ENC_05]** `EVELYN` — She steadies herself. The laugh dies. She addresses the creature with cautious respect.

> "I apologize. I have never met your kind."

**[CAT_ENC_06]** `CAT_BEAST` — The Cat tilts its head. The layered voice continues.

> "Kind implies others. I am the shape this place takes."

**[CAT_ENC_07]** `EVELYN` — She processes this. Her rational mind struggles.

> "You are the Weald."

**[CAT_ENC_08]** `CAT_BEAST` — The Cat descends from the root. It circles her once. Each step leaves no footprint.

> "I am what you walk through. Your eyes name me cat. Your mind shrinks the world."

**[CAT_ENC_09]** `EVELYN` — She bristles slightly. Not angry — defensive, the way a cornered person defends their grip on reality.

> "I am trying to understand. Help me."

**[CAT_ENC_10]** `CAT_BEAST` — The Cat stops. It looks at her with all its eyes.

> "You laughed at what you could not sort into a box. That is the refusal."

**Notes:** The Cat should never move like a normal animal. Its motion should be slightly too smooth, slightly too deliberate. The layered voice effect is critical — use pitch-shifted duplicates of the same audio, offset by 50-100ms.

---

#### [prologue_curse_triggered] — The transformation sequence

**[CURSE_01]** `CAT_BEAST` — VFX trigger: the air around Evelyn begins to shimmer. The Cat speaks the words that are not a spell but a statement of fact.

> "You refused to see. Now you will see too much."

**[CURSE_02]** `EVELYN` — She gasps. Her hands fly to her head. VFX: her hair ripples as cat ears push through.

> "What— what is happening?"

**[CURSE_03]** `EVELYN` — She doubles over. Her spine arches. VFX: a tail bursts from her lower back. She cries out.

> "No— please— make it stop!"

**[CURSE_04]** `CAT_BEAST` — The Cat watches. Unmoved. Not cruel — simply present.

> "I do not make. I reveal. You were always this. Now the world knows."

**[CURSE_05]** `EVELYN` — She falls to her knees. Her breathing is ragged. Her ears twitch — she can hear everything now. VFX: her eyes flash red for a single frame.

> "Everything is so loud. The leaves. The dirt. My own heartbeat."

**[CURSE_06]** `EVELYN` — She slowly stands. Her hands tremble as she touches one of her new ears. It is real.

> "These are mine now. Aren't they."

**[CURSE_07]** `CAT_BEAST` — The Cat turns away. It has already lost interest.

> "They were yours when you refused them. That is how refusal works."

**[CURSE_08]** `EVELYN` — She stares at her reflection in a pool of water. Cat ears. Tail. Red-tinged eyes. She is still herself — but she is not only herself.

> "I am still me. I have to be."

**Notes:** This is the most intense body horror sequence in the game. Keep it brief but visceral. The ears pushing through hair should look painful. The tail emergence should feel involuntary and violating. Evelyn's voice should carry genuine fear — not screaming, but the controlled panic of someone trying not to break.

---

#### [prologue_cat_beast_dialogue] — The Cat's riddle

**[CAT_DLG_01]** `EVELYN` — She is still shaking but composed enough to speak. She addresses the Cat's back.

> "Why did you do this? What did I do wrong?"

**[CAT_DLG_02]** `CAT_BEAST` — The Cat pauses. It does not turn around.

> "Wrong implies a rule. There are no rules here. Only consequence."

**[CAT_DLG_03]** `EVELYN` — She wraps her arms around herself. Her tail curls tightly — she cannot control it.

> "I did not ask for this."

**[CAT_DLG_04]** `CAT_BEAST` — The layered voice softens, if anything can soften.

> "The seed does not ask to become the tree. The tree grows anyway."

**[CAT_DLG_05]** `EVELYN` — She is quiet for a long moment. Then, with effort:

> "Will it change back?"

**[CAT_DLG_06]** `CAT_BEAST` — The Cat finally looks at her.

> "Does the river change back after the rain?"

**[CAT_DLG_07]** `EVELYN` — She closes her eyes. Her ears flatten. When she opens them, there is resolve beneath the fear.

> "Then I need to learn what I can do now."

**[CAT_DLG_08]** `CAT_BEAST` — The Cat is already walking away. Its voice fades with distance.

> "You already know. You simply have not listened yet."

**Notes:** The Cat's dialogue should feel like it is speaking literal truths that sound like riddles because human minds cannot process them directly. Every line is technically accurate. None of them are comforting.

---

#### [prologue_ability_tutorial] — Gameplay tutorial prompts

These are gameplay prompts, not cutscene dialogue. They appear as contextual hints during the ability gauntlet section.

**[ABILITY_JUMP_01]** `EVELYN` — Internal monologue, triggered when player approaches the fallen log blocking the path.

> "My legs feel different. Like springs coiled tight."

**[ABILITY_JUMP_02]** `SYSTEM_PROMPT` — UI text.

> "Press [JUMP] to use High Jump."

**[ABILITY_JUMP_03]** `EVELYN` — After successfully clearing the log. Genuine wonder breaks through her fear.

> "I just— that was incredible."

**[ABILITY_CRAWL_01]** `EVELYN` — Triggered at the root wall. Her fingers tingling against the bark.

> "My hands feel the texture of the wood. Like it is calling to me."

**[ABILITY_CRAWL_02]** `SYSTEM_PROMPT` — UI text.

> "Press [INTERACT] on the wall to Wall Crawl."

**[ABILITY_CRAWL_03]** `EVELYN` — After scaling the root wall. She is starting to accept her new reality.

> "I could get used to this. I should not, but I could."

**[ABILITY_SENSE_01]** `EVELYN` — Her ears twitch violently. She hears something distant.

> "Wait. I can hear... boots. Many of them. Far off."

**[ABILITY_SENSE_02]** `SYSTEM_PROMPT` — UI text.

> "Press [SENSE] to focus your Enhanced Senses."

**[ABILITY_SENSE_03]** `EVELYN` — She focuses. The audio shifts — distant chanting and marching become audible.

> "People. Coming closer. I should move."

**Notes:** The Enhanced Senses prompt is the transition into the ambush sequence. The distant chanting the player hears is the Church knights approaching. This creates urgency.

#### [prologue_church_ambush] — Church knights arrive and capture Evelyn

**[AMBUSH_01]** `EVELYN` — Cutscene: Evelyn is running through the Weald. Her enhanced senses have been warning her for thirty seconds. The knights emerge from the trees — armored, organized, carrying detection instruments.

> "Too late. They are already here."

**[AMBUSH_02]** `CHURCH_KNIGHT_LEADER` — The leader raises a detection instrument. It beeps frantically at Evelyn.

> "Confirmed. Primal signature. Secure the subject."

**[AMBUSH_03]** `EVELYN` — She backs up. Her ears flatten against her head. Her tail is bristling — she cannot hide it.

> "I am not— I am a person. I am not a subject."

**[AMBUSH_04]** `CHURCH_KNIGHT_LEADER` — The leader does not engage. This is procedure, not malice.

> "Hold still. The sedative will be painless."

**[AMBUSH_05]** `EVELYN` — She runs. Her new speed carries her fast, but the knights are prepared. A net of woven silver catches her.

> "No— let me go!"

**[AMBUSH_06]** `EVELYN` — She struggles. The net burns where it touches her ears. She cries out — genuine pain.

> "It burns— please!"

**[AMBUSH_07]** `CHURCH_KNIGHT_LEADER` — A knight approaches with a sedation dart. Evelyn's vision blurs.

> "Subject secured. Transport to Small Lab."

**[AMBUSH_08]** `EVELYN` — Her last words before the sedation takes her. Slurred, fading, but defiant.

> "I will not— I will not be your—"

**[AMBUSH_09]** `EVELYN` — Silence. Her eyes close. The screen fades through her blurred vision to black.

**Notes:** The knights are not evil here. They are following procedure. This makes it worse — Evelyn is being dehumanized by people who do not hate her, who are simply doing their jobs. The silver net is the first hint of her holy vulnerability.

### Section 2: "The Small Lab"

---

#### [prologue_lab_awakening] — Evelyn wakes in restraints

**[LAB_AWAK_01]** `EVELYN` — Fade in from black. Evelyn's POV: cold stone ceiling. She is strapped to a chair. Essence vials glow on shelves around her. Her head throbs.

> "Where... what is this place?"

**[LAB_AWAK_02]** `VEYSS` — Inquisitor Veyss stands at an observation desk. He does not look up from his ledger. His voice is flat, clinical.

> "Subject Three-Seven. Conscious. Note the time."

**[LAB_AWAK_03]** `EVELYN` — She pulls at her restraints. They hold. Her cat ears are visible — she cannot hide them.

> "Let me go. I have not done anything."

**[LAB_AWAK_04]** `VEYSS` — Veyss finally looks at her. He adjusts his glasses. His expression is the same as a technician examining a component.

> "You exist as a hybrid entity. That is sufficient."

**[LAB_AWAK_05]** `EVELYN` — She stares at him. The casual cruelty of "sufficient" lands.

> "I have a name."

**[LAB_AWAK_06]** `VEYSS` — He returns to his ledger. He writes something.

> "Names are irrelevant. Your magical signature is not."

**[LAB_AWAK_07]** `EVELYN` — She looks around. The scratch marks on the restraint chair. The faded prayer on the wall. A ledger labeled with subject numbers. Most entries end in TERMINATED.

> "What are you going to do to me?"

**[LAB_AWAK_08]** `VEYSS` — Veyss closes the ledger. He walks to a shelf and selects a vial of concentrated essence. It glows with unnatural light.

> "What we have always done. We will make you useful."

**Notes:** The lab should feel claustrophobic. Low ceiling. Cold stone. The glow from the vials provides the only warmth, and it is not comforting — it is eerie. Veyss should never raise his voice. His clinical detachment is the horror.

---

#### [prologue_transformation] — Vampire transformation sequence

**[TRANS_01]** `VEYSS` — Cutscene: Veyss positions the essence vial above Evelyn's chest. A ritual circle on the floor begins to glow.

> "Administering concentrated essence. Source: Mage-class. Potency: maximum."

**[TRANS_02]** `EVELYN` — The essence flows into her. Her eyes widen. She gasps — not in pain, in overload. VFX: veins of dark energy spread from her chest.

> "What is that— I can feel it inside me—"

**[TRANS_03]** `VEYSS` — He watches the instruments. He does not watch her face.

> "Archetype catalyst engaging. Vampire parameters loading."

**[TRANS_04]** `EVELYN` — Her skin pales. Her breath fogs in suddenly cold air. Her canines elongate — she feels them with her tongue and panics.

> "My teeth— what is happening to my teeth?"

**[TRANS_05]** `VEYSS` — He adjusts a dial. The essence flow increases.

> "Physiological restructuring. Normal responses. Heart rate elevated but within parameters."

**[TRANS_06]** `EVELYN` — Her eyes shift — the irises deepen, a red tint bleeding into the brown. She feels power surging through her. It feels good. It feels wrong.

> "It feels— powerful. It feels wrong. Make it stop."

**[TRANS_07]** `VEYSS` — He makes a note in his ledger.

> "Subject reports power sensation. Expected response. Proceeding to Control Litany."

**[TRANS_08]** `EVELYN` — She looks at her hands. Her nails have sharpened slightly. Her strength is palpable. She could break wood. She is terrified of what she wants to do with this strength.

> "I can feel everything. The stone. The metal. The air."

**Notes:** This sequence should parallel the curse transformation but feel fundamentally different. The curse was violent and chaotic. The vampire transformation is precise, methodical, and industrial. It feels like being manufactured. Veyss's clinical narration makes it worse.

---

#### [prologue_seal_failure] — The Control Litany fails

**[SEAL_01]** `VEYSS` — Veyss begins to chant. His voice is rhythmic, practiced. He has done this many times. The ritual circle flares.

> "By seal and word, by chain and name, I bind the creature to its frame."

**[SEAL_02]** `VEYSS` — A visible seal forms over Evelyn's chest — glowing geometric patterns, interlocking rings. It begins to lock into place.

> "By blood and bone, by mark and sign, the will of subject yields to mine."

**[SEAL_03]** `EVELYN` — She feels the seal trying to lock onto her. It finds her vampire signature — and then it finds her cat signature. The two signatures are in superposition. The seal cannot resolve them.

> "Something is... pulling at me. Two directions. It cannot decide."

**[SEAL_04]** `VEYSS` — The seal flickers. Veyss notices. He does not stop chanting. His voice remains steady.

> "By oath and lock, by— anomaly detected."

**[SEAL_05]** `EVELYN` — The seal cracks. A visible fracture appears in the glowing pattern. Evelyn feels it — the seal is failing.

> "It is breaking. Whatever it is, it is breaking."

**[SEAL_06]** `VEYSS` — The seal shatters. Light explodes outward, then collapses. The ritual circle goes dark. Veyss pauses. For the first time, his expression changes.

> "Fascinating."

**[SEAL_07]** `EVELYN` — The restraints loosen. The seal's failure weakens them. She tests them. They give.

> "I am... still me. Both of me."

**[SEAL_08]** `VEYSS` — Veyss steps back. He speaks into a communication tube. His voice is calm.

> "Control seal failure on Three-Seven. Hybrid interference pattern. Recommend termination."

**[SEAL_09]** `EVELYN` — She hears "termination." Her ears flatten. Her eyes narrow. The new power inside her responds to her rage.

> "You will not terminate me."

**Notes:** The seal shattering should be visually spectacular — like glass cracking in slow motion, each fracture glowing brighter until the whole thing explodes. Veyss saying "Fascinating" should land with chilling casualness. He is not afraid. He is curious. This makes him more terrifying.

---

#### [prologue_escape_combat] — Gameplay sequence, minimal dialogue

This is a playable escape sequence. Dialogue is minimal — environmental and combat barks only.

**[ESCAPE_01]** `EVELYN` — Triggered when player breaks the restraints (interactive prompt).

> "I am getting out of here."

**[ESCAPE_02]** `GUARD_01` — First guard encounters Evelyn. He is unprepared.

> "She is loose! Restrain her!"

**[ESCAPE_03]** `EVELYN` — Combat bark, using her vampire abilities for the first time in a fight.

> "Get out of my way!"

**[ESCAPE_04]** `GUARD_02` — Second guard. More experienced. He knows what she is.

> "Vampire! Holy rounds! Now!"

**[ESCAPE_05]** `EVELYN` — She takes a holy round hit. It burns — more than anything she has felt.

> "That— that burns like the net!"

**[ESCAPE_06]** `VEYSS` — Over a speaking tube, calm to the end.

> "Terminate the subject. Do not let it reach the exit."

**[ESCAPE_07]** `EVELYN` — Final guard before the exit door. She is done talking.

> "I said. Get. Out. Of. My. Way."

**Notes:** This is 30 seconds of pure power fantasy. Evelyn's vampire abilities should feel overwhelming compared to the weak guards. The holy round hit is important — it teaches the player about her holy vulnerability in a safe context. The player should feel both thrilled and slightly sickened by how easily she dispatches these people.

#### [prologue_escape_complete] — Evelyn flees into the night

**[ESC_CMPL_01]** `EVELYN` — Cutscene: Evelyn bursts through the lab door. Cold night air. Stars. Forest. She does not stop running.

> "Keep moving. Do not stop."

**[ESC_CMPL_02]** `EVELYN` — She runs through the treeline. Her new speed carries her fast. Her ears are flat, her tail streaming behind her.

> "I cannot go back. There is nothing to go back to."

**[ESC_CMPL_03]** `EVELYN` — She glances over her shoulder. The lab is already behind her. She will never see it again. Her voice is quiet.

> "I am sorry. For whatever I was before."

**[ESC_CMPL_04]** `EVELYN` — She runs into darkness. The camera holds on the empty space where she stood. Fade to black.

**Notes:** This is the last time the player sees Evelyn as an active character until Chapter 2. The transition to black should hold for three full seconds — long enough for the player to sit in the silence. Then the Witch Prologue begins.

### Section 3: "The Day the Sun Goes Cold"

---

#### [witch_prologue_domestic] — Interactive domestic morning scene

This is the only interactive sequence in the Witch Prologue where the player explores freely. The cabin is warm, sunlit, lived-in.

**[DOM_01]** `MAGE` — Interactive trigger: player (as the Witch) enters the main room. The Mage is at his desk, surrounded by books. Morning light. Tea on the desk.

> "You are awake. I was certain I would finish this chapter before you rose."

**[DOM_02]** `WITCH` — Player approaches. The Mage's desk is covered in notes on magical theory.

> "You have been reading all night again. Your tea is cold."

**[DOM_03]** `MAGE` — He looks up. He is warm, gentle, intellectual. He smiles without looking up from his page.

> "It was a good chapter. The one about resonance frequencies. Let me show you."

**[DOM_04]** `WITCH` — The player can interact with objects in the cabin. Each interaction triggers a line.

> "Your books are everywhere. I will not tidy them again."

**[DOM_05]** `MAGE` — He finally looks at her. The warmth in his expression is the most genuine thing in the game.

> "Do not tidy them. They are happy where they are. As am I."

**[DOM_06]** `WITCH` — Player interacts with the shared bed. Two blankets, one pushed aside.

> "You sleep terribly. You read until dawn. I set your tea down and you do not notice."

**[DOM_07]** `WITCH` — Continuation, quiet, fond.

> "I love you anyway."

**[DOM_08]** `MAGE` — He puts the book down. He looks at her properly.

> "And I you. More than the books. Which is saying something."

**[DOM_09]** `WITCH` — Player interacts with the garden window. Sunlight on half-tended herbs.

> "The garden needs attention. You promised you would help."

**[DOM_10]** `MAGE` — He laughs. Warm, genuine.

> "I promise many things. I keep some of them. Today I teach in the village."

**[DOM_11]** `WITCH` — She pours him fresh tea. The gesture is automatic, practiced, intimate.

> "The village children will learn nothing. They will stare at your hands and giggle."

**[DOM_12]** `MAGE` — He takes the tea. Their fingers brush.

> "They will learn that magic is not frightening. That is enough."

**[DOM_13]** `WITCH` — Player interacts with the ethics books on the shelf.

> "You think magic is a system. I think it is a language. We will argue about this forever."

**[DOM_14]** `MAGE` — He stands. He prepares to leave. He kisses her forehead — a gesture the player will remember in the worst moment of the game.

> "We have been arguing for three years. I am not concerned about forever."

**[DOM_15]** `WITCH` — He heads for the door. She calls after him. Casual. Normal. Nothing ominous.

> "Return before dark. I will make stew."

## 5. Dialogue Sequences

All dialogue nodes listed above conform to the Dialogue Style Guide. Key compliance notes:

- Every line is under 120 characters
- Character voices match their profiles: Evelyn (warm, self-deprecating, pauses), Witch (precise, no contractions, never raises voice), Cat Beast (alien, layered, literal-but-riddling), Veyss (cold, clinical), Mage (warm, gentle, intellectual), Knight Captain (convinced, righteous, not evil)
- Subtext is present but never stated explicitly
- No idioms or wordplay that would not survive translation
- Context notes are included for translator reference
- The Witch uses contractions extremely sparingly (only in the most emotionally loaded moments, and even then rarely)

## 9. Emotional Progression Notes

### Evelyn's Emotional Journey (Prologue Sections 1-2)

```
Fear -> Confusion -> Alien Unease -> Body Horror -> Wonder -> Helplessness -> Disorientation -> Dread -> Violation -> Shattering -> Rage -> Flight
```

The key to Evelyn's prologue is that she never chooses any of this. The curse is imposed. The transformation is forced. The escape is reactive. The player should feel her powerlessness even as she gains power. Every ability she gains is given without consent. This makes her eventual choice in Chapter 2 — to protect the village — the first truly free act of her new life. That choice matters because everything before it did not belong to her.

Her humor does not appear in the Prologue. She is too frightened, too overwhelmed. The first time she makes a self-deprecating joke will be in Chapter 2, and it will feel like a miracle — proof that she is still herself.

### The Witch's Emotional Journey (Prologue Section 3)

```
Peace -> Warmth -> Contentment -> Devastation -> Pursuit -> Urgency -> Catharsis -> Conflict -> Hollow Victory -> Grief -> Vow -> Resolve
```

The Witch's prologue is about the destruction of meaning. The domestic scene is not setup — it is the foundation. Every detail (the cold tea, the books, the garden, the nettles joke) is a brick in the life that will be demolished. The player must feel the weight of what is lost, not just understand it intellectually.

The vow is not revenge. Revenge would be killing the Knight Captain — she does that without a second thought. The vow is larger: she is going to prevent this from ever happening again. Her grief is so vast that it becomes structural — it becomes a plan, a movement, a decade of preparation.

### The Knight Captain's Role

The Knight Captain is the moral complexity of the prologue. He is not evil. He is convinced. He fights well. He dies believing he was right. The player should feel conflicted about killing him — because he does not deserve to die, but he is in the way of the only thing that matters to the Witch. This is the game's core moral tension in miniature: good people, doing what they believe is right, causing terrible harm.

### The Mage's Function

The Mage appears in only two scenes: the domestic morning and the cell. But he is the most important character in the game. Every detail about him is designed to make the player love him quickly and efficiently: his warmth, his intellectual passion, his gentle humor, his love for the Witch. When he dies, the player should grieve — not because he was complex, but because he was good and he did not deserve what happened.
