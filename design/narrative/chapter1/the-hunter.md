# Chapter 1: "The Hunter" — Narrative Design Document

**Chapter Number**: 1
**POV Character**: Evan
**Duration**: 30-40 minutes
**Emotional Arc**: Trust -> Confidence -> Unease
**Prerequisites**: Prologue completion (Witch Prologue)
**Author**: Narrative Team
**Status**: Authoritative
**Last Updated**: 2026-04-13

---

## 1. Overview

Chapter 1 introduces Evan, a Church knight of the Sacred Cross, as he investigates reports of magical creatures dying under unexplained circumstances near Oakhaven village. The player controls Evan through a structured hunting experience: receiving his mission at the Church outpost, tracking through the forest, discovering anomalous magical energy signatures and physical evidence, arriving at the village, and filing his report. The chapter establishes Evan as competent, faithful, and operating within a system the player already knows is corrupt (from the Prologue). The dramatic irony is the chapter's core narrative engine. Evan finds evidence of Church experimentation — dirty magical signatures, empty essence vials, campsite notes referencing "subject transport" — but lacks the framework to understand what he is seeing. The player, having witnessed Evelyn's transformation in the Prologue, recognizes the evidence immediately. This gap between player knowledge and Evan's ignorance creates sustained dramatic tension throughout the chapter.

---

## 2. Player Fantasy

The player experiences two overlapping fantasies:

**As Evan**: The fantasy of being a competent professional. Evan is good at his job. He tracks, he fights, he investigates, he reports. The player feels capable and effective. Combat is satisfying. Detection is engaging. The world responds to Evan's skill. This is a power fantasy grounded in competence rather than dominance.

**As the Player (with Prologue knowledge)**: The fantasy of dramatic irony. The player watched the Church transform a human woman into a vampire. They saw Inquisitor Veyss's clinical cruelty. They know the Church's public face conceals a laboratory. When Evan finds a "dirty" magical signature and notes it is "not natural," the player feels the weight of recognition that Evan cannot. Every warm interaction with a Church NPC carries an undertow of knowledge. Every blessing from the genuinely kind parish priest becomes bittersweet. The player is simultaneously inhabiting Evan's certainty and holding the truth he does not yet possess.

**Core emotional experience**: Confidence slowly threaded with unease. The chapter should never feel confusing or disorienting. Evan knows what he is doing. The player knows what Evan does not. The tension lives in the gap between these two knowledge states.

## 3. Narrative Arc

### Section 1: The Mission (0-10 minutes)

**Emotional State**: Trust, warmth, routine competence

Evan begins in his quarters at a Church outpost — a functional, lived-in military facility. The outpost is the Church at its best: knights training in the yard, a chapel bell ringing, a memorial wall honoring fallen hunters. Evan is respected here. His journal entries demonstrate thoroughness and dedication. The senior knight who briefs him is professional and warm. The parish priest who blesses his journey is genuinely kind. Every interaction reinforces that the Church does real good in the world. The player, knowing the Prologue, experiences this with layered irony.

**Narrative Beats**:
1. Evan's quarters: sparse, functional, a worn hunting journal
2. Briefing: senior knight assigns the Oakhaven investigation
3. Equipment room: Essence Resonance Detector issued and explained
4. Parish priest: genuine blessing, warm farewell
5. Departure: Evan rides out, outpost shrinks behind him

**What the Player Learns**: Evan is competent, faithful, and trusted. The Church's public face is warm and genuine. The mission is straightforward: investigate monster deaths near Oakhaven, report findings.

**What the Player Already Knows (from Prologue)**: The Church creates monsters. The essence vials mentioned in the briefing are harvesting tools. The "purification" language conceals experimentation.

### Section 2: The Trail (10-25 minutes)

**Emotional State**: Confidence -> curiosity -> unease

Evan tracks through forest and moorland between the outpost and Oakhaven. The trail is a semi-open hunting ground where the player practices combat and detection. Combat encounters demonstrate Evan's competence. Then the discoveries begin: dead monsters with "dirty" magical energy signatures, an empty Church vial, an abandoned campsite with clinical notes referencing "essence yield" and "control seal integrity." Each discovery is factual and ambiguous. Evan notes the anomalies but lacks the context to interpret them. The unease is implicit, not explicit.

**Narrative Beats**:
1. Tracking begins: detector tutorial, following magical signatures
2. Shadow wolf combat: Evan fights with Church-trained efficiency
3. First anomaly: dissolved monster, dirty signature detected
4. More bodies: 2-3 additional dead monsters, same anomalous pattern
5. Church vial: empty, discarded, label scratched off
6. Abandoned campsite: notes referencing "subject transport," "essence yield"

**What the Player Learns**: Something is killing magical creatures in a way that leaves unnatural energy residue. Church equipment is present at the scene. Clinical notes suggest organized operations, not random activity.

**What the Player Already Knows**: The dirty signatures are artifacts of Church experimentation. The vials are essence harvesting tools. The notes describe the Church's three-stage transformation process. The "something" killing monsters is likely a Church-made creature or a Church operative.

### Section 3: Oakhaven Village (25-40 minutes)

**Emotional State**: Warmth -> intrigue -> heroic competence -> lingering unease

Evan arrives at Oakhaven, a small farming village on the edge of the Deep Weald. The village is warm, lived-in, and grateful for the Church's attention. Villagers confirm the monster deaths but do not know the cause. Some are relieved; others are uneasy. The village elder speaks privately with Evan and hints at a "presence" that protects the village at night — Evelyn, though neither the elder nor Evan knows her name. A rogue magical creature attacks, and Evan defends the village heroically. After the fight, his detector reads the strongest dirty signature yet. He files his report honestly, including all anomalies. The chapter ends with mission completion and an undertow of unanswered questions.

**Narrative Beats**:
1. Village arrival: warm, normal, safe
2. Villager interviews: 2-3 conversations, pieces of the puzzle
3. Elder conversation: hints at "secret guardian," educated and perceptive
4. Village attack: rogue creature, Evan fights to protect villagers
5. Final detection: strongest dirty signature yet, something powerful is close
6. Report filed: Evan writes honestly, sends to Church, chapter closes

**What the Player Learns**: The village is being protected by something unknown. The dirty signatures are strongest near the village, meaning whatever is creating them is nearby. Evan is a good man who will report what he finds accurately.

**What the Player Already Knows**: The "secret guardian" is almost certainly Evelyn (or a magical creature like her). The dirty signatures near the village suggest Church-made creatures have been deployed here — possibly deliberately, to manufacture the crisis that brought Evan. Evan's honest report will bring him back, setting up Chapter 3.

## 9. Emotional Progression Notes

### Evan's Emotional Journey

| Beat | Time | Emotional State | Player Perception |
|------|------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Quarters | 0-3 min | Calm routine, prepared | Warm, competent |
| Briefing | 3-5 min | Professional trust | The Church is good |
| Equipment | 5-7 min | Confident preparation | Ready for the hunt |
| Blessing | 7-8 min | Grateful, touched | Genuine faith |
| Departure | 8-10 min | Purposeful, open world | Adventure begins |
| Tracking | 10-12 min | Engaged, focused | This is fun |
| First Combat | 12-15 min | Competent, efficient | I am good at this |
| First Anomaly | 15-18 min | Curious, slightly wrong | Wait, what is that? |
| More Bodies | 18-22 min | Pattern recognition, concern | This is not normal |
| Church Vial | 22-24 min | Procedural concern, confusion | That should not be here |
| Abandoned Camp | 24-27 min | Deep confusion, unease | These notes mean something bad |
| Village Arrival | 27-30 min | Relief, warmth | Good people, safe place |
| Villager Interviews | 30-33 min | Gathering information | The pieces exist |
| Elder Conversation | 33-35 min | Intrigue, mystery | Someone is hiding something |
| Village Attack | 35-38 min | Heroic, competent | This is why I fight |
| Final Detection | 38-39 min | Strong unease, building dread | Whatever it is, it is close |
| Report Filed | 39-40 min | Professional duty, undertow | Mission done, but something is wrong |

### Key Emotional Design Principles

1. **Never break Evan's competence.** Even when confused, Evan processes confusion professionally. He does not panic. He investigates. The unease is in the data, not in his reaction to it.

2. **The player's knowledge is the tension.** Evan's emotional state should never exceed "mild professional concern." The dramatic weight comes from the player recognizing what Evan cannot. Do not give Evan realizations he has not earned.

3. **Warmth makes the betrayal hurt.** Every genuinely kind interaction (the priest's blessing, the blacksmith's stew offer, the child's game) is an investment in the player's emotional response when the truth is revealed. Do not rush past these moments.

4. **The ending must feel complete but unresolved.** The player should feel that Evan did his job well (satisfying) but that the job itself was built on a lie (unsettling). Both feelings must coexist.
