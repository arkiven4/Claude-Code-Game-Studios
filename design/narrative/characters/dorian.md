# Dorian

**Role**: Party Support — The Tactician Who Served the Enemy
**POV Chapters**: Joins Chapter 8, full presence Chapters 9 through 11
**Visual**: Cold steel and charcoal grey palette. Tall and lean, with the controlled posture of someone who has spent years standing at attention and giving orders. His face is sharp-featured and pale — not sickly pale, but the pale of someone who works indoors, under lamplight, studying maps and writing orders that send people to die. His hair is black, cut short and precise, with a single silver streak above his left temple — not from age, from stress, appearing overnight during a campaign that went wrong. His eyes are pale grey, almost colourless, and they do not move quickly. They scan, assess, and file. Nothing escapes them. Nothing is wasted on them. He dresses in a former Church officer's uniform that he has systematically stripped of insignia — the dark wool coat remains, but the silver embroidery, the rank pins, the Church cross: all removed, leaving a plain, well-tailored coat that reads as military without declaring allegiance. His gloves are leather, fingerless on the right hand (he needs tactile feedback for writing and map work). He carries no instrument, no staff, no weapon you can see. His weapon is a **tactician's ledger** — a bound folio of thick pages filled with maps, enemy dispositions, and mathematical calculations that he channels through to produce debuff and crowd-control effects. The ledger is scarred: water damage on the back cover, burnt edges on three pages, a bloodstain on page 47 that he has not cleaned. His silhouette is unmistakable: the plain dark coat, the ledger always in hand, the stillness of a man who does not move unless the movement has purpose.

## Backstory

Dorian was never a soldier. He was a mathematician — a prodigy, really, the kind of mind that sees patterns in chaos and numbers in noise. The Church found him at a provincial academy where he was studying logistics and supply chain optimization. He was nineteen, brilliant, and entirely uninterested in the moral dimensions of his work. Numbers were numbers. Efficiency was efficiency. If the Church could move supplies faster using his algorithms, that was a good outcome. He did not ask what the supplies were for.

The Church did not recruit him. They acquired him. They showed him data — the scale of the magical creature threat, the logistical impossibility of defending every village, the mathematical necessity of centralized response. Dorian read the data. The data was correct. He agreed to help.

He became a Church tactician — not a field commander, not a combat officer, but the man who planned campaigns, optimized deployment schedules, and calculated the acceptable cost of every operation in lives. He was good at his work. Not gifted — good. The kind of good that comes from a mind that sees systems the way other people see faces.

He planned the containment operations. He calculated the acceptable civilian casualty rates. He wrote the orders that sent squads into villages to "neutralize unlicensed rituals" — festivals, gatherings, songs. He did not think of them as villages. He thought of them as coordinates with population values and threat assessments.

Then he was assigned to the Rosethorne operation.

Dorian did not design the Rosethorne operation. He optimized it. The original plan called for a larger detachment, a longer containment window, and higher resource expenditure. Dorian reduced all three. He calculated the minimum force required to disperse the gathering. He determined the shortest containment duration that would achieve compliance. He wrote the revised order. It was efficient. It was correct. It was also the night a fourteen-year-old girl watched her parents hurt and her village's heart broken, and Dorian did not know this because he was not there and he did not ask.

He learned about Rosethorne's aftermath three months later, when a field report crossed his desk: civilian casualties higher than calculated, a barrier malfunction that burned a woman's hands, a soldier's excessive force that was not in the plan. The report classified these as "acceptable variance." Dorian read the words and something in his mind — the same part that found errors in equations — flagged them as wrong.

He began to look. Not at Rosethorne specifically — at everything. He pulled campaign reports, cross-referenced casualty estimates with actual outcomes, and found a pattern: his calculations were correct, but the Church was systematically inflating threat assessments to justify larger operations. The operations caused more casualties than his models predicted. The casualties were classified as acceptable. The acceptance was not his.

Dorian confronted his superior. He was reassigned to a desk position — effectively sidelined, his access reduced, his influence diminished. He continued working. He continued calculating. He continued finding errors in the Church's math and, gradually, in his own.

The breaking point was not dramatic. It was a number: 847. The Church's internal estimate for "acceptable losses" in a planned operation against a suspected magical creature stronghold. 847 civilians. Dorian ran the numbers. The stronghold did not exist. The operation was based on faulty intelligence. The 847 deaths would serve no purpose. They would be waste.

Waste was the one thing Dorian could not accept. He had spent his career optimizing efficiency, and the Church was spending lives on a calculation it knew was wrong. This was not strategy. It was error.

He defected that night. Not with a speech — with a ledger. He took his tactician's folio — every calculation, every map, every order he had written — and he walked. The Church marked him as a traitor and a security risk. He did not run. He hid. He found work with underground networks, providing tactical intelligence to Church defectors and resistance cells. He did not ask for forgiveness. He offered data.

He joins Evelyn's party in Chapter 8 when they need tactical intelligence on Church defenses — specifically, the fortified positions protecting the Witch's approach routes. The party's usual tactical resource (Evan) is stretched thin. Someone points them to a former Church tactician who knows the deployment schedules, the weak points, the mathematical vulnerabilities in the Church's perimeter. That tactician is Dorian.

They find him in a safe house, surrounded by maps, recalculating the Church's defensive positions with the same precision he once used to design them. He looks up. He sees Evelyn — vampire, cat-eared, the Church's greatest experiment turned against them — and he does not flinch. He sees a variable he had not accounted for. He is intrigued.

*"I know why you are here. You need the deployment schedules for the eastern perimeter. I have them. I will give them to you on one condition: I come with you. Not because I trust you. Because if I stay here, the Church will find me eventually, and I would rather my calculations serve something other than a desk."*

## Personality

**Core traits:** Analytical, cold on the surface, quietly driven by guilt he will not name, precise to the point of abrasiveness, secretly hungry for moral clarity.

Dorian is a man who processes the world through data and systems. He does not trust emotion because emotion is not quantifiable. He does not trust intuition because intuition cannot be verified. He trusts numbers, patterns, and the cold logic of cause and effect. This makes him extraordinarily effective and extraordinarily isolated.

**Behavioral patterns:**

- **Analysis as emotional processing.** Dorian handles every situation — including emotional ones — through analysis. When he is uncomfortable, he reorganizes his notes. When he is grieving, he recalculates old campaigns, looking for the error that led to unnecessary deaths. When he is afraid, he maps escape routes. This is not avoidance — it is how he metabolizes feeling. The act of calculating is how he copes.
- **Precision as respect.** Dorian is precise in everything he says and does. He does not approximate. He does not say "about ten" when he means "eleven." This is not pedantry — it is respect for the truth of numbers. When he is wrong, he corrects himself immediately. *"I said twelve. It is thirteen. My error."*
- **Guilt without confession.** Dorian does not apologize for his time in the Church. He does not give speeches about regret. He carries the weight of what his calculations enabled silently. The only sign is his obsessive recalculation of old campaigns — running the numbers again and again, looking for the point where the math went wrong, as if finding the error will undo the damage.
- **Cold surface, warm undertow.** Dorian presents as cold, analytical, and detached. But beneath the surface, he is driven by a desperate, unacknowledged need to do good with the skills he used to do harm. He will not say this aloud. He will demonstrate it through actions: precise intelligence, perfectly timed debuffs, tactical decisions that minimize casualties even when no one asks him to.
- **Contrast with Mireille.** Dorian is the inverse of Mireille in every way. Where she is warm, he is cold. Where she performs, he withdraws. Where she fills silence with music, he fills it with calculation. This contrast is deliberate and productive — they are the party's two supports, operating from opposite emotional registers, and the party benefits from both.
- **Respect for competence.** Dorian does not respect people for their warmth, their humor, or their intentions. He respects them for their competence. If you are good at your job, Dorian will respect you. If you are not, he will not insult you — he will simply factor your incompetence into his calculations and plan accordingly.

## Voice

**Vocal quality:** Low, flat, measured. Dorian's voice carries the calm of someone who has delivered casualty estimates without raising his voice. He does not project. He does not need to. His voice is quiet, precise, and impossible to ignore because every word is chosen deliberately. When he is emotional, his voice does not shake; it gets flatter, more controlled, as if he is compressing feeling into data.

**Speech patterns:**

- **Tactical and mathematical vocabulary.** Dorian uses the language of strategy and calculation naturally: *variable, equation, cost, yield, acceptable loss, margin, threshold, optimal, suboptimal, probability, vector.* *"The probability of success is 34%. Acceptable if the alternative is zero."* *"Your plan has too many uncontrolled variables. Let me reduce them."*
- **Numbers over adjectives.** Dorian describes situations using quantitative language. He does not say "many enemies" — he says "approximately twenty." He does not say "a long time" — he says "roughly four hours." He does not say "dangerous" — he says "casualty risk above sixty percent." This is not affectation — it is precision.
- **No contractions when reporting.** When Dorian is delivering tactical information, he drops contractions entirely. *"The Church has three squads in the eastern perimeter. They rotate every six hours. The weak point is the northern flank."* When he is speaking personally, contractions appear, slowly, like a variable being introduced into an equation.
- **He does not speculate without data.** When asked for an opinion, Dorian provides an analysis. If he lacks data, he says so. *"I do not have enough information to assess. I need reconnaissance on the northern flank before I can give you a probability."* He does not guess. He does not intuit. He calculates.
- **Rare emotion.** Dorian rarely shows strong emotion. When he does, it manifests as a break in his precision — a sentence that trails off, a number he cannot provide, a silence where data should be. *"The Rosethorne operation had a calculated civilian casualty rate of three percent. The actual rate was —"* He stops. He cannot finish the equation.
- **Corrects himself publicly.** If Dorian realizes he is wrong, he corrects himself immediately and without embarrassment. *"I miscalculated. The enemy reinforcement window is four minutes, not six. Adjust accordingly."* This is not humility — it is accuracy. Accuracy matters more than pride.
- **He uses people's roles, not names.** Dorian defaults to referring to people by their function: "the healer," "the tank," "the vampire." He learns names gradually, and when he uses one, it is deliberate. The shift from "the vampire" to "Evelyn" is a meaningful data point.

**Emotional tells:**

- When Dorian is worried, he recalculates. He pulls out his ledger and runs numbers he has already run, checking for errors. The repetition is his version of pacing.
- When he is grieving, he goes very still and very quiet. He stares at a page without reading it. He does not write. The absence of calculation is the tell.
- When he is genuinely moved, his voice drops to barely above a whisper and he looks at his hands. He cannot handle eye contact during emotional moments.
- When he is lying — which is rare, because he values accuracy — his numbers become slightly imprecise. He says "approximately" more often. He hedges. The party learns that when Dorian hedges, he is hiding something.
- When he is angry, his voice does not rise. It becomes colder, flatter, more precise. The anger is in the compression — the way he reduces a moral outrage to a cold equation because the alternative is feeling it.

**Formality level:** Moderate to high. Dorian speaks like someone who learned to communicate in offices and briefing rooms, not villages or campfires. He is precise, direct, and entirely unpolished in his lack of warmth. This softens gradually as the story progresses.

## Combat Role

Dorian is the party's debuff engine and battlefield controller. Where Mireille amplifies the party through buffs and tempo, Dorian weakens the enemy through calculated debuffs, crowd control, and strategic manipulation of the combat environment. He is the party's offensive support — he does not heal or shield; he makes enemies easier to kill.

He channels tactical magic through his **ledger** — a bound folio of maps, calculations, and enemy disposition data. When he channels, the pages glow with pale light, and mathematical equations appear in the air around him: probability matrices, threat assessments, vulnerability calculations. The burnt edges and water damage on the ledger are visible during channeling, a reminder of the campaigns that scarred it.

### Skills

**1. Vulnerability Assessment**

Dorian analyzes a single target enemy and applies a targeted debuff that increases all damage taken by that enemy by 20% for a moderate duration. If the target is an elite or boss, the debuff is amplified to 30% and also reduces the target's defense by 15%.

*Gameplay purpose:* Single-target damage amplification. Vulnerability Assessment is the party's primary tool for focusing down high-value targets — bosses, elites, and dangerous casters. The debuff stacks multiplicatively with Mireille's Rallying Chorus, creating the party's most powerful damage window.

*Tactical note:* Dorian must maintain line of sight with the target for the full channel duration (2s). If line of sight is broken, the debuff is reduced by half. Positioning matters.

**2. Disposition Shift**

Dorian rewrites the battlefield's threat parameters, forcing enemies within a moderate radius to reassign their targets. Enemies currently attacking a party member have a 60% chance to switch to a different party member (chosen by Dorian). Additionally, enemies affected by Disposition Shift have their movement speed reduced by 25% as they recalculate their approach vectors.

*Gameplay purpose:* Crowd control and threat manipulation. Disposition Shift is the party's "reset" button — deployed when a squishy member has drawn unwanted aggro, when enemies are clustering on a vulnerable position, or when the party needs to reposition. The movement speed reduction creates a window for the party to reorganize.

*Tactical note:* Disposition Shift does not work on bosses or elite enemies with fixed targeting. It is most effective against groups of standard enemies. Dorian must choose the new target carefully — redirecting enemies onto the tank is safe; redirecting them onto another squishy is catastrophic.

**3. Attrition Protocol**

Dorian applies a stacking debuff to all enemies in a large area. Each stack reduces enemy damage output by 5% and healing received by 8%, up to 5 stacks. Enemies at maximum stacks have their attack speed reduced by 20% and cannot benefit from any friendly buffs.

*Gameplay purpose:* Sustained enemy weakening. Attrition Protocol is deployed at the start of an encounter and maintained throughout — it is the party's long-term debuff engine that makes extended fights progressively easier. The healing reduction is particularly effective against enemy healers and self-sustaining bosses.

*Tactical note:* Attrition Protocol is a persistent area effect. Enemies that leave the area lose stacks gradually. Dorian must reposition the protocol to maintain maximum coverage, creating a tactical positioning minigame.

**4. Checkmate (Ultimate)**

Dorian opens his ledger to page 47 — the bloodstained page, the calculation he cannot redo — and channels every variable, every equation, every cold number he has ever calculated into a single tactical command. For a moderate duration, all enemies on the battlefield are forced into a "stunned" state (unable to act) for 3 seconds, then take massive tactical damage based on the number of debuffs currently active on them. Each debuff on an enemy increases the damage by 15%, making Checkmate the devastating payoff to a debuff-focused strategy.

*Gameplay purpose:* Debuff payoff and encounter-turning control. Checkmate is the party's "everything you weakened them, now capitalize" button — deployed after stacking Vulnerability Assessment, Attrition Protocol, and Mireille's Dirge of the Fallen on the enemy. The damage scaling with active debuffs rewards the party's entire support strategy.

*Tactical note:* Checkmate's damage is entirely dependent on the number of active debuffs. Using it with no debuffs is a waste. Using it with maximum debuffs is the single most powerful damage event in the party's arsenal. Timing and setup are everything.

### Passive: Cold Calculus

Dorian gains increased debuff duration for each unique debuff currently active on the battlefield. With more debuffs running, his debuffs last longer, creating a positive feedback loop that rewards sustained debuff application. However, if all debuffs expire simultaneously (a "reset" state), Dorian's next debuff has reduced duration. This passive reinforces Dorian's identity as a calculated, sustained presence — he is strongest when the battlefield is a web of his design.

### Vulnerabilities

- **Channel dependency.** Most of Dorian's skills require him to channel — he must remain stationary and maintain line of sight. This makes him vulnerable to flanking and assassination.
- **Low personal damage.** Dorian's combat effectiveness is almost entirely supportive. He cannot meaningfully damage enemies beyond Checkmate's payoff, which requires setup. In solo situations, he is nearly helpless.
- **Debuff dependency.** Dorian's power scales with the number and duration of active debuffs. In fast encounters where debuffs do not have time to stack, Dorian is significantly less effective.
- **Emotional interference.** If Dorian witnesses a scenario that triggers his guilt (civilian casualties, Church operations that match his old calculations), his next skill cast has reduced accuracy — his numbers waver. This is a deliberate design choice that ties his emotional state to his combat effectiveness.
- **Ledger destruction.** Enemies that specifically target Dorian's ledger (a rare but possible Church ability) can reduce his debuff potency for a short duration. The ledger is his focus — without it, he is significantly weakened.

## Emotional Arc

**Chapter 8 — The Man Behind the Numbers.** The party finds Dorian in a safe house, surrounded by maps, recalculating Church defensive positions with the same precision he once used to design them. He looks up. He sees Evelyn. He does not flinch. He offers a deal: intelligence for protection. He does not ask for trust. He asks for utility.

*"I know why you are here. You need the deployment schedules for the eastern perimeter. I have them. I will give them to you on one condition: I come with you. Not because I trust you. Because if I stay here, the Church will find me eventually, and I would rather my calculations serve something other than a desk."*

He joins because the party gives his skills a purpose that is not the Church. He does not call this redemption. He calls it reallocation of resources. The distinction matters to him.

**Chapters 8 through 9 — The Integration.** Dorian integrates with the party functionally but not emotionally. He provides tactical intelligence, optimizes routes, calculates encounter probabilities, and deploys debuffs with clinical precision. He does not participate in camp conversations. He does not share personal information. He sits apart from the fire, ledger open, calculating.

The party finds him difficult. He is not hostile — he is indifferent. He treats every interaction as a data exchange. This creates friction, particularly with Mireille, whose warmth and expressiveness are everything he is not.

**Chapter 9 — The Equation That Does Not Balance.** The party encounters a village that has been damaged by a Church operation — not destroyed, but harmed. Collateral damage. Acceptable variance. Dorian walks through the village in silence. He counts the damaged buildings. He estimates the civilian casualties. He does not share the numbers.

That night, he sits alone with his ledger. He is not calculating. He is staring at page 47 — the bloodstained page, the calculation he cannot redo. Evelyn finds him. She does not ask if he is alright. She sits beside him and waits.

*"I planned operations like this. Not this one — others. Similar. I calculated the acceptable casualty rate. I called it a percentage. It is easier to call it a percentage than to think about the people."* He touches the bloodstain. *"This is from a soldier who died because my calculation was wrong. Not much wrong — a two percent error in the reinforcement window. He died in the gap. Two percent."*

Evelyn does not offer reassurance. She offers presence. *"Two percent is a number. The soldier was a person. You are learning the difference."*

Dorian is quiet. *"I do not know how to be a person who matters. I only know how to be a person who calculates."*

*"Then calculate this: how many people can you save with the skills you used to hurt them. That is your equation now."*

Something shifts. Not much. But the next day, Dorian provides tactical intelligence that specifically minimizes civilian exposure in the planned operation. He does not announce this. He just does it. The party notices.

**Chapters 10 through 11 — The Variable He Chose.** Dorian becomes the party's tactical backbone. His debuff strategy is essential during the Witch's campaign, where enemy strength requires every advantage the party can create. He deploys Attrition Protocol on entire enemy armies, reducing their effectiveness across the battlefield. He uses Disposition Shift to protect vulnerable positions. He is in his element — calculating, optimizing, serving.

He begins to participate in camp conversations — not warmly, but honestly. He offers tactical assessments of the party's emotional state: *"Corin, your perimeter checks have increased by forty percent in the last three days. You are anxious. The data suggests you should talk to someone."* Corin talks to him. It helps.

He and Mireille develop a grudging respect. She is warmth; he is calculation. They are the party's two supports, operating from opposite registers, and the party benefits from both. Mireille plays a melody that makes him pause. He does not smile. But he stops calculating for exactly three minutes. She notices. She files it away.

**Chapter 11 — The Last Calculation.** The final battle. Dorian deploys Checkmate at the critical moment — page 47 open, bloodstain visible, every debuff on the battlefield active. The damage is devastating. He has spent his entire career calculating acceptable losses. For the first time, he is calculating acceptable wins.

When Evelyn begins to vanish, Dorian does something he has never done: he closes his ledger. He sets it down. He stands and he does not calculate. He does not assess. He does not quantify. He simply stands, the way Kaelen stands, the way Silas stays, the way Mireille stops playing. He is present. It is the most uncalculated thing he has ever done.

**Epilogue — The Numbers That Matter.** Dorian does not return to the Church. He does not become a hero. He becomes a teacher — of tactics, of logistics, of the mathematical realities of war. He opens a small school in a village that was once damaged by a Church operation, and he teaches the village's children how to read maps, calculate supply lines, and understand the cost of every decision in numbers that represent people.

He still carries his ledger. Page 47 is still bloodstained. He has not cleaned it. He uses it as a teaching tool — not to show his guilt, but to show the cost of error. *"This number is not a number. It is a person. Every number in this book is a person. Remember that when you calculate."*

He visits Rosethorne once. He stands in the square where the festival was broken. He does not know Mireille's story — he knows the operation, not the people. But he stands there, and he is quiet, and he does not calculate anything. He just stands. It is enough.

## Relationships

### With Evelyn

Dorian and Evelyn share a relationship built on utility that deepens into genuine respect. Initially, Dorian sees Evelyn as a variable — an unpredictable, powerful force that the Church created but could not control. He is fascinated by her not as a person but as a data point: what happens when an experiment develops agency?

Their bond deepens when Evelyn demonstrates that she is not a variable — she is a person, with intentions, with warmth, with a dry humor that Dorian does not understand but gradually appreciates. The turning point is Chapter 9, when she tells him to calculate how many people he can save. It is the first time someone has given him a moral equation instead of a tactical one.

Dorian's version of affection for Evelyn is tactical. He optimizes her routes, calculates her encounter probabilities, and provides intelligence that specifically protects her from Church operations he once would have designed. He does not say he cares. He demonstrates it through data.

**Bond moment:** In Chapter 9, when Dorian confesses the two percent error that killed a soldier, Evelyn tells him to calculate how many people he can save. He processes this as an equation — a new variable, a new objective function. *"I do not know how to be a person who matters. I only know how to be a person who calculates."* Evelyn's tail is still. *"Then calculate this: how many people can you save with the skills you used to hurt them. That is your equation now."* Dorian is quiet for a long time. He opens his ledger. He writes a new heading. It is the first page that is not a recalculation of the past.

### With Evan

Evan and Dorian share the most complicated relationship in the party. Evan is a former Church hunter who served the institution in the field. Dorian is a former Church tactician who served it from a desk. Both were cogs in the same machine. But where Evan is redeemed by his defection and his service to Evelyn, Dorian is still calculating his own redemption — and Evan is not sure Dorian deserves it.

Their relationship begins with tension. Evan does not trust Dorian — not because Dorian is untrustworthy, but because Dorian represents the face of the Church that Evan cannot confront: the bureaucrats, the calculators, the people who wrote orders without ever seeing the people those orders killed. Evan faced monsters in the field. Dorian designed the operations that created them.

Over time, Evan's assessment shifts. He watches Dorian provide intelligence that saves lives. He sees Dorian recalculating old campaigns with genuine remorse. He does not forgive Dorian — he does not have that authority — but he accepts him as a variable that has changed its sign.

**Key dynamic:** When Evan and Dorian clash over a tactical decision, the argument is cold and precise. Evan argues from field experience; Dorian argues from mathematical models. Eventually, they learn to combine both: Evan's instinct for enemy behavior plus Dorian's probability calculations create the party's most effective tactical planning.

### With Silas

Dorian and Silas share a relationship built on contrasting approaches to the same goal: keeping people alive. Silas heals bodies with warmth and compassion. Dorian prevents damage with cold calculation and debuffs. They are the party's two approaches to survival, and they complement each other perfectly.

Silas is one of the few people who treats Dorian with genuine warmth — not the warmth of friendship, but the warmth of a healer who sees a patient in need. When Dorian is recalculating old campaigns obsessively, Silas says gently: *"Dorian. You are running the same equation hoping for a different answer. It will not change. The soldier is gone. You are still here. Be here."* Dorian stops. He closes the ledger. He says: *"Thank you, Silas."*

In combat, Silas's healing and Dorian's debuffs create a powerful combination: Silas keeps the party alive while Dorian makes enemies easier to kill. Silas's Sanctuary + Dorian's Checkmate is the party's most devastating encounter-turning sequence.

**Key dynamic:** Silas once asked Dorian: *"What is the calculated probability that you will ever forgive yourself?"* Dorian considered this. *"Low. Approaching zero."* Silas nodded. *"Then I will keep treating your wounds until the probability increases. I have time."*

### With Kaelen

Dorian and Kaelen share a professional respect rooted in their shared military background. Both served the Church — Kaelen in the shield wall, Dorian at the desk. Both deserted. Both carry the weight of their service. Both express it differently: Kaelen through protective action, Dorian through tactical precision.

In combat, Dorian's debuffs and Kaelen's threat generation create the party's most effective control combination. Dorian weakens enemies; Kaelen holds their attention. Kaelen trusts Dorian's tactical intelligence completely — if Dorian says the weak point is the northern flank, Kaelen positions the shield wall on the northern flank.

Off the battlefield, they share a quiet understanding. Kaelen does not demand warmth from Dorian, and Dorian does not pretend to provide it. They exchange tactical information, nod, and move on. It is the most comfortable relationship Dorian has in the party.

**Key dynamic:** Kaelen once said to Dorian: *"You calculate the cost of everything. Have you calculated the cost of not forgiving yourself?"* Dorian was quiet. *"That variable is incalculable."* Kaelen nodded. *"Then stop trying to solve it."* Dorian does not stop. But he calculates it less often.

### With Lyna

Dorian and Lyna share a functional, slightly distant relationship. Lyna is a hunter who relies on instinct, patience, and reading the environment. Dorian is a tactician who relies on data, calculation, and mathematical models. Their approaches are different but compatible.

Dorian provides Lyna with enemy disposition data that enhances her already excellent target selection. Lyna provides Dorian with field observations that improve his calculations. They exchange information efficiently and without social overhead. Both prefer it this way.

In combat, Dorian's Vulnerability Assessment on Lyna's priority targets creates the party's single most lethal damage combination. Lyna under Vulnerability Assessment is the party's highest single-target damage source, and both of them know it.

**Key dynamic:** Lyna once asked Dorian for a probability assessment of a long-range shot under difficult conditions. Dorian calculated it: 67%. Lyna took the shot. She hit. She looked at Dorian and said: "Your math is good. My aim is better." Dorian adjusted his model. 89%. He did not tell her.

### With Ione

Dorian and Ione share a relationship built on mutual incomprehension that gradually softens into respect. Ione experiences the world as a web of living connections. Dorian experiences it as a system of calculable variables. They are philosophical opposites.

Ione finds Dorian's coldness unsettling — not threatening, but sad. She sees a person who has disconnected himself from the living world in favor of numbers, and she pities him. Dorian finds Ione's animism imprecise — not wrong, but unverifiable. He cannot calculate the value of a tree's life, and this frustrates him.

Their relationship improves when Dorian provides tactical intelligence that specifically protects a grove Ione cares about. He does not do this for her — he does it because the grove provides tactical cover for the party's approach route. But Ione notices that he chose the route that minimized damage to the grove when other routes were mathematically equivalent. She says: *"You calculated the cost to the trees."* He says: *"I calculated all costs."* She smiles. *"Yes. You did."*

**Key dynamic:** Ione names Dorian "Grey-Page" after the colour of his ledger's glow. He does not use the name. But he does not object to it either, which from Dorian is nearly a compliment.

### With Brighid

Dorian and Brighid share a relationship of maximum contrast. Brighid is loud, warm, food-obsessed, and instinctual. Dorian is quiet, cold, calculation-obsessed, and analytical. They are the party's most opposite pair.

Brighid tries to feed Dorian. He declines. She insists. He accepts. He eats without commenting on the food. She is offended by his lack of reaction. He explains that his lack of reaction is data — the food is adequate, and he is registering it as such. She decides this is the best review she has ever received.

In combat, Dorian's debuffs and Brighid's aggression create a powerful combination. Weakened enemies are easier for Brighid to hold and damage. Brighid does not understand Dorian's math. She understands the result: enemies go down faster. She approves.

**Key dynamic:** Brighid names one of her dishes "The Calculator" — a precisely portioned, mathematically balanced stew where every ingredient is measured to the gram. Dorian eats it. He says: "The proportions are optimal." Brighid beams. It is the most positive review she has ever received.

### With Mireille

Dorian and Mireille share the party's most productive opposition. They are the two supports — Mireille buffs the party, Dorian debuffs the enemy. They operate from opposite emotional registers: she is warmth and music, he is cold and calculation. The contrast is deliberate and essential.

Initially, they clash. Mireille finds Dorian cold and inaccessible. Dorian finds Mireille's performative warmth imprecise and emotionally manipulative. They avoid each other. The party suffers — their skills are most effective when combined, and their avoidance creates tactical gaps.

The turning point comes when they are forced to coordinate during an encounter. Mireille's Rallying Chorus + Dorian's Vulnerability Assessment creates the party's most powerful damage window. They see it work. They do not comment. But the next encounter, they coordinate without being asked.

Over time, they develop a grudging respect. Mireille plays a melody that makes Dorian stop calculating for three minutes. Dorian provides intelligence that specifically protects a village where Mireille once performed. Neither acknowledges the other's gesture. Both notice.
