# Kaelen

**Role**: Party Tanker — The Shield That Walked Away
**POV Chapters**: Joins Chapter 4, full presence Chapters 5 through 11
**Visual**: Burnished iron and deep crimson palette. Broad and imposing — not fat, not bodybuilder, but the heavy solidity of someone who has carried weight for years and grown into it. His face is scarred: a jagged line across the left cheekbone from a blessed blade, a burn mark on the right jaw from a failed experiment's backlash. His hair is black, cropped close to the skull in the old Church guard style, though he has let it grow out enough to show grey at the temples. His eyes are the colour of cold iron — grey-blue, steady, and tired. His silhouette is unmistakable: tower shield slung across his back like a second spine, heavy pauldron on the left shoulder only (the right arm must be free to swing), a half-cape of dark fabric that catches the wind like a banner. Soft cartoon aesthetic — his bulk is rounded, not angular; his scar reads as history, not horror.

---

## Backstory

Kaelen was born in a Church garrison town — not a village, not a city, but the kind of place that exists to house soldiers and nothing else. His father was a guardsman. His father's father was a guardsman. The Church did not need to recruit Kaelen; it needed only to wait for him to grow up. He joined the guard at sixteen, not out of conviction but out of gravity. It was the only path he had ever seen.

He was good at it. Not gifted — good. Kaelen's talent was not speed or brilliance but endurance. He could stand a line longer than anyone. He could absorb punishment that would break a lighter soldier and keep his shield up. He learned early that the Church valued this quality: soldiers who could hold a position, take the hits, and not break were worth more than soldiers who won duels.

He served for twelve years. He rose to sergeant. He led a shield-wall unit — six men and women who stood behind his signal and held the line while strike teams flanked and cleared. They were effective. They were also the Church's first line of defense against its own creations. When a Church-made creature broke containment, Kaelen's unit was the one sent to contain it.

He saw things no one should see. Creatures that had once been people, still conscious inside bodies that did not obey them. A young woman whose skin had turned to glass, screaming silently as her own joints shattered. A child-soldier the Church had transformed into a hound-thing that still recognized its own name. Kaelen held the line against them all. He told himself he was containing a threat. He told himself the creatures were already gone. He told himself a lot of things.

The breaking point was not dramatic. It was quiet. A routine containment operation: a Church-made construct had breached a transport near the Weald's edge. Kaelen's unit moved in. The construct was a woman — adult, conscious, her arms replaced with Church-forged blade prosthetics. She was weeping. She raised her blade-arms and charged. Kaelen held his shield. She struck it again and again, crying, apologizing, begging someone to kill her. Her strikes cracked his shield. He held. His unit flanked and destroyed the construct. Afterward, Kaelen sat on the ground and looked at his cracked shield and realized he had been holding the line for the wrong side.

He deserted that night. Not with a speech, not with a scene — he simply did not report for morning muster. He left his Church insignia on his bunk, took his shield, and walked. The Church marked him as a deserter. They did not pursue him aggressively — deserters were common, and most starved within weeks. Kaelen did not starve. He found work as a mercenary for villages that needed protection from creature attacks. He did not ask what the creatures were. He held the line. That was enough.

He joins Evelyn's party in Chapter 4 when the party is ambushed by Church knights in a forest pass. Kaelen is traveling the same road, hired by a merchant caravan. The ambush catches both groups. Kaelen sees the Church knights, recognizes their tactics, and instinctively steps between them and the nearest civilian — Evelyn. His shield absorbs a blessed-energy volley that would have crippled her. When the dust settles, he recognizes what Evelyn is. He also recognizes the Church insignia on the dead knights.

*"Church strike team. Standard formation. They were hunting you specifically."* He looks at her — her ears, her eyes, the way she holds herself. *"Good. I hope they failed."*

He joins because the Church made him into a wall, and he has spent the last year deciding what he wants to protect. Evelyn is worth protecting. The party is worth protecting. And for the first time in his life, he gets to choose.

## Personality

**Core traits:** Steadfast, dryly observant, emotionally patient, carries guilt without performing it, quietly fierce about consent and choice.

Kaelen is a man who spent his life being told where to stand and what to protect. His entire identity was built around obedience to a line — hold here, shield up, do not break. Leaving the Church was the first choice he made for himself that was not assigned. He is still learning what it means to choose.

**Behavioral patterns:**

- **Stillness under pressure.** Kaelen does not flinch, does not rush, does not panic. When danger arrives, he plants his feet, raises his shield, and assesses. This is not bravery — it is training so deep it has become temperament. The party learns to trust this: when Kaelen is calm, the situation is manageable.
- **Dry observation over humor.** Kaelen does not tell jokes. He makes quiet, accurate observations about the absurdity of situations, delivered with a straight face. *"We are being chased by Church knights through a forest that is actively trying to eat us. I have had better Tuesdays."* His observations are funny because they are true, not because he is trying to be funny.
- **The weight of the shield.** Kaelen carries his shield everywhere. He cleans it, repairs it, tightens its straps. The shield has a crack from the construct woman's strikes — he never fixed it. It is a reminder of the day he stopped following orders. When he is thinking, his hand rests on the shield's grip. When he is anxious, he runs his thumb along the crack.
- **Protective without being paternal.** Kaelen protects people because it is what he does, not because he thinks they are weak. He does not coddle. He does not tell people to stay behind him because they cannot handle themselves — he tells them to stay behind him because his job is to take the hit, and he is good at it. The distinction matters.
- **Guilt without confession.** Kaelen does not apologize for his time in the Church. He does not give speeches about regret. He carries the weight of what he did — what he failed to do — silently. The only sign is his absolute refusal to follow an order he has not chosen to accept. If Evelyn suggests a plan, he will argue it, question it, and then commit to it if he agrees. If she orders him, he will look at her and wait for her to rephrase it as a request.
- **Unexpected tenderness.** Kaelen is gentle with things that are small, broken, or scared. He adjusts his stance so his shadow does not fall on a frightened villager. He speaks softly to injured animals. He once spent twenty minutes helping a child fix a broken toy cart because "it is not right for something to stay broken."

## Voice

**Vocal quality:** Low, gravelly, measured. His voice carries the weight of someone who has shouted orders over battle and learned that quieter voices are listened to more carefully. He speaks slowly, not because he is slow but because he chooses his words. When he is emotional, his voice does not crack — it gets quieter, more deliberate, as if each word is a stone he is placing carefully.

**Speech patterns:**

- **Tactical vocabulary.** Kaelen uses the language of defense: *hold, line, shield, position, cover, brace, anchor, wall.* He applies these terms to non-combat situations naturally. *"We need to hold this position emotionally. We are not ready to move forward yet."* *"Your argument has no cover. Let me give you some."*
- **Short declarative sentences.** Kaelen speaks in statements, not questions. When he asks a question, it is because he genuinely needs information, not because he is thinking aloud. *"The Church will send a second wave. We should be ready."* *"Evelyn, are you injured? Answer directly."*
- **He uses full names.** Kaelen addresses people by their full name when he is being serious, their first name when he is being warm. *"Evelyn Cross, put that down before you hurt yourself."* vs. *"Evelyn, that was well done."* The shift is noticeable and intentional.
- **No contractions when reporting.** When Kaelen is describing a tactical situation or reporting information, he drops contractions. *"The Church has three squads in the area. They are converging on our position."* When he is speaking personally, contractions return. *"I have been thinking about what you said. You are right, and I do not like it."*
- **Rare emotion.** Kaelen rarely shows strong emotion. When he does, it is significant. Anger makes his voice go very quiet. Grief makes him go still and silent — he will stand and stare at nothing for a long time. Joy makes him smile without showing his teeth, a small, private thing.
- **He does not perform certainty.** When Kaelen does not know something, he says so. *"I do not know if this plan will work. I know it is the best we have. I am with you."* This honesty is one of his defining traits — he never pretends to confidence he does not feel.

**Emotional tells:**

- When Kaelen is worried about someone, he positions himself between them and the nearest threat. It is unconscious — he does not realize he is doing it.
- When he is grieving, he cleans his shield. The crack gets more attention than the rest. He runs his thumb along it repeatedly.
- When he is genuinely happy, his shoulders drop. The permanent tension in his posture releases, and he looks younger, less burdened. It happens rarely, and the party notices every time.
- When he is lying (which is rare), his voice stays the same but his eyes shift left. It is a tiny tell, and only people who know him well would catch it.

**Formality level:** Moderate-low. Kaelen speaks like a soldier who has learned to talk to civilians — clear, direct, without jargon unless it is needed. He is warm without being casual, authoritative without being commanding.

## Combat Role

Kaelen is the party's primary aggro manager and damage absorber. His combat design centers on controlling enemy attention, mitigating incoming damage, and creating windows for the party to operate safely. He is not a damage dealer — his power is measured in the damage he prevents and the enemies he controls.

He uses a **tower shield** and a **mace**. The shield is his primary identity — an oversized, reinforced Church-issue shield with a visible crack running diagonally across its face. The mace is secondary: a heavy, single-handed iron mace with a worn leather grip. He does not swing it fast — he swings it hard. His combat animations are deliberate and heavy, emphasizing weight over speed.

### Skills

**1. Bulwark**

Kaelen plants his feet and raises his shield, entering a defensive stance that reduces incoming damage by 60% for a short duration. While in Bulwark, Kaelen generates significant threat, drawing enemy attacks toward him. If an attack would critically hit an ally within 3 meters, Kaelen intercepts it automatically (consuming remaining Bulwark duration).

*Gameplay purpose:* Core defensive ability. Used reactively when enemies focus squishy party members or proactively before telegraphed heavy attacks. The threat generation makes Kaelen the party's aggro anchor.

*Tactical note:* Bulwark reduces Kaelen's movement speed by 80%. He becomes an immovable object — powerful but stationary. The party must work around his position.

**2. Shield Bash**

Kaelen charges forward with his shield, dealing moderate damage to a single target and stunning them briefly (1.5s). If the target is already targeting Kaelen (highest threat), the stun duration doubles. The charge covers a moderate distance and can interrupt enemy telegraphs.

*Gameplay purpose:* Interruption and crowd control. Shield Bash is Kaelen's primary tool for stopping dangerous enemy abilities. The threat-sensitive stun reward encourages smart timing — bashing an enemy that is already focused on Kaelen is significantly more impactful.

*Tactical note:* Shield Bash generates a burst of threat on hit, making it an excellent aggro tool. However, it has a moderate cooldown and leaves Kaelen briefly vulnerable if it misses.

**3. Defiant Stand**

Kaelen slams his shield into the ground, creating a protective zone around himself (5m radius) for a moderate duration. Allies within the zone gain 40% damage reduction and immunity to knockback. Kaelen himself gains 20% additional damage reduction while inside his own zone. Enemies that attempt to enter the zone are slowed by 30%.

*Gameplay purpose:* Area denial and party protection. Defiant Stand is deployed during high-pressure encounters — boss AoE phases, ambushes, or when the party needs a safe space to reposition. The knockback immunity is tactically significant against enemies that disrupt formations.

*Tactical note:* Defiant Stand is anchored to Kaelen's position. He can move within it slowly, but the zone does not follow him. This forces the party to stay near Kaelen, reinforcing his role as the party's anchor.

**4. Unbroken**

Kaelen's ultimate ability. For a short duration, Kaelen becomes immune to all crowd control effects (stun, slow, silence, root) and reflects 25% of incoming damage back to attackers. Additionally, any party member who takes damage while Kaelen is in Unbroken has 50% of that damage redirected to Kaelen instead. Kaelen's threat generation triples during this window.

*Gameplay purpose:* Encounter-turning defense. Unbroken is the party's "everything goes through me" button — deployed during boss enrage phases or when the party is being focused down. The damage redirect protects vulnerable party members at the cost of Kaelen's own HP, creating meaningful risk/reward.

*Tactical note:* Unbroken does not make Kaelen invulnerable — he still takes damage, he just redirects it from others. Using Unbroken when Kaelen is low HP is a high-risk play that requires the healer to keep up. This reinforces party interdependence.

### Vulnerabilities

- **Low mobility.** Kaelen is the slowest party member (SPD 0.9). His skills further reduce his movement. He cannot chase down fast enemies or reposition quickly. The party must fight on his terms or bring the fight to him.
- **Weak against percentage-based damage.** Kaelen's high DEF makes him excellent against flat damage, but enemies that deal percentage-based HP damage bypass his primary defense.
- **Threat dependency.** Kaelen's effectiveness depends on enemies using ThreatMeter targeting. Against AITargeting-mode enemies (assassins that prioritize low-DEF or low-HP targets), Kaelen's threat generation is less impactful.
- **Energy costs.** Bulwark and Defiant Stand draw from the same energy pool. Maintaining both simultaneously drains Kaelen quickly, leaving him unable to use Shield Bash or Unbroken.

## Emotional Arc

**Chapter 4 — The Wall That Chose.** The party encounters Kaelen during a Church ambush in a forest pass. He is traveling with a merchant caravan, hired muscle. When the Church attacks, he instinctively protects the nearest civilian — Evelyn. His shield absorbs a blessed-energy barrage. After the fight, he recognizes what the Church was hunting and what they made her into. He does not flinch. He does not reach for a weapon. He reaches for his shield straps and tightens them.

*"Church strike team. Standard formation. They were hunting you specifically."* He looks at her. *"Good. I hope they failed."*

He joins because Evelyn represents the first thing he has chosen to protect that was not assigned to him by orders. The choice matters. He treats it with the gravity it deserves.

**Chapters 5 through 6 — Learning to Choose.** Kaelen finds his rhythm with the party. He is in his element — holding the line, absorbing punishment, making dry observations about their collective recklessness. But he is also learning something new: what it means to be part of a team rather than a unit. In the Church, his shield-wall unit was a tool. Here, the party is people.

He begins to participate in camp conversations — not as a leader, not as a follower, but as an equal. He listens more than he speaks. When he does speak, the party listens back. This is new for him. In the Church, his opinion was never asked. Here, it is valued.

**Chapter 7 — The Crack in the Shield.** The party raids a Church facility and discovers records of Kaelen's old unit. Most of them are dead — killed containing creatures they did not create. One survived: a young guardswoman named Ressa who was reassigned after Kaelen deserted. The records show that Ressa took over Kaelen's position in the shield wall and held the line against the same creatures he once fought.

Kaelen reads the records in silence. That night, he sits alone and cleans his shield. The crack gets extra attention. Evelyn finds him. She does not ask if he is alright. She sits beside him and waits.

*"I held the line for twelve years. I thought I was protecting people. I was protecting the Church's secrets."* He runs his thumb along the crack. *"Ressa is still there. She thinks she is doing the right thing. I was wrong too, once. I do not know how to tell her without breaking whatever she has left."*

Evelyn does not offer answers. She offers presence. *"You do not have to fix it tonight. You just have to decide what you will do when you see her."* Kaelen nods. *"I will stand between her and the people who would hurt her. Even if she does not want me to."*

**Chapters 8 through 10 — The Choice to Stand.** Kaelen becomes the party's tactical anchor in combat and its emotional anchor in crisis. When the Witch's shadow spreads, Kaelen does not panic — he plants his feet and asks what needs holding. When Evelyn shows signs of the weight she is carrying, Kaelen notices. He does not push. He positions himself closer to her, a silent offer of protection she can accept or decline.

He begins to accept that his past does not define his present. The Church made him into a wall, but he gets to decide what the wall protects now. The distinction is everything.

**Chapter 11 — The Last Line.** The final battle. Kael Unbroken absorbs damage that would have killed the party three times over. He uses Shield Bash to interrupt the Witch's most dangerous telegraphs. He holds the line. When Evelyn begins to vanish, he does not try to stop it — he knows, with the certainty of someone who has watched too many things slip through his shield, that this is not something he can block.

Instead, he does what he has always done: he stands. He plants his feet. He raises his shield one last time — not to block the vanishing, but to shield Evan from having to watch it alone. *"I am here,"* he says. *"I will hold this position. You hold her hand."*

**Epilogue — The Shield Remains.** Kaelen does not return to the Church. He does not become a mercenary again. He settles near the village Evelyn protected — the village she can no longer visit — and he stands guard. Not because anyone ordered him to. Because he chose to. Villagers learn to recognize the broad man with the cracked shield who walks the perimeter at dusk. Children learn that if they are lost or scared, the big man with the iron eyes will find them and walk them home.

He cleans his shield every night. The crack is still there. It always will be. It is not a wound anymore. It is a scar.

## Relationships

### With Evelyn

Evelyn is the first person Kaelen chose to protect. Not because he was ordered to, not because it was his job, but because he looked at her — cursed, transformed, hunted, still helping villagers — and decided she was worth standing for. This choice is the foundation of everything.

Their bond deepens through shared understanding of institutional betrayal. Evelyn was experimented on by the Church. Kaelen enforced the Church's will for twelve years. They are on opposite sides of the same crime, and they both know it. Kaelen never asks Evelyn to forgive him. Evelyn never asks Kaelen to apologize. They meet in the space between guilt and grace and decide that what they do now matters more than what they did before.

Kaelen is one of the few people who treats Evelyn as someone worth protecting without treating her as weak. He respects her power. He shields her because that is his role, not because she needs saving. Evelyn values this. She has been pitied and feared. Kaelen does neither.

When Evelyn vanishes, Kaelen does not speak. He stands at the edge of where she disappeared and holds his shield. He stands there until Evan touches his arm and leads him away. Later, he settles near her village. He walks the perimeter at dusk. This is how he remembers her: by doing what she would have wanted — protecting people who cannot protect themselves.

**Bond moment:** After discovering the records of his old unit in Chapter 7, Kaelen tells Evelyn about the construct woman — the one who cracked his shield. *"She was weeping. She was apologizing. She was begging us to kill her. I held my shield and told myself I was doing my job."* Evelyn listens. Her tail is still. Her ears are flat. *"The Church made me into something I did not choose. They made her into something she did not choose. The difference is I got to walk away."* Kaelen touches the crack in his shield. *"You did. She did not. I will spend the rest of my life making sure the next one does."* This is the moment their bond crystallizes: not in shared guilt, but in shared commitment to do better.

### With Evan

Evan and Kaelen share the deep, unspoken respect of two people who served institutions that betrayed them. Evan was a hunter who believed in the Church's mission. Kaelen was a guard who enforced it. Both discovered the truth. Both redirected their conviction.

Their relationship is built on tactical competence. Evan plans the route; Kaelen holds the line. Evan identifies the threat; Kaelen absorbs it. They do not need to discuss this division of labor — it is natural, earned, and mutually respected. Evan trusts Kaelen to keep the party alive. Kaelen trusts Evan to lead them to the right fight.

Their conversations are practical and warm. They talk tactics, positioning, enemy behavior. Occasionally, they talk about the Church — not with anger, but with the clear-eyed assessment of people who know exactly how the machine works because they were cogs in it.

**Key dynamic:** When Evan overthinks a plan, Kaelen cuts through it with tactical simplicity. *"You are worrying about three contingencies. The Church will do what the Church always does: send bodies at the problem. I will hold the line. You find the head. That is the plan."* Evan exhales. He needed to hear that.

### With Silas (Healer)

Silas and Kaelen have the most symbiotic relationship in the party. Kaelen takes the damage; Silas repairs it. Kaelen protects Silas with fierce dedication — *"The healer stays behind me. That is not negotiable."* Silas patches Kaelen's battle wounds with the steady hands of someone who has treated too many soldiers.

Their bond is built on mutual dependence. Kaelen cannot do his job without Silas keeping him standing. Silas cannot do his job without Kaelen keeping enemies off him. Neither treats this as transactional — it is partnership. Kaelen once took a blessed-energy blast meant for Silas and said nothing about it. Silas noticed. He treated Kaelen's wounds that night with extra care and said nothing about it either.

**Key dynamic:** In combat, Kaelen and Silas operate as a unit. Kaelen reads Silas's energy levels and times his defensive skills to cover the healer's recovery windows. Silas reads Kaelen's HP and prioritizes him when his shield is down. They do not communicate this verbally — it is instinct, earned through hundreds of fights.

### With the Witch

Kaelen views the Witch with the clear-eyed assessment of a soldier who has faced overwhelming force before. He does not fear her power — he respects it. He does not agree with her method — he understands it. If they were to meet, Kaelen would not argue with her. He would plant his shield between her and the party and say: *"You will have to go through me."*

He does not hate her. He recognizes that she is doing what she believes is necessary, the same way he once held the line for the Church. The difference is that Kaelen stopped. She has not.

### With Other Party Members

- **Support 1 (Church corruption tie):** Kaelen shares a complicated bond with this member. Both were Church personnel who defected. Kaelen understands the weight of leaving everything you knew behind. He is the person this member goes to when the Church's betrayal feels personal — because Kaelen has felt it too, and he has not broken.
- **Support 2 (Village tie):** Kaelen respects this member's connection to the ordinary world. He grew up in a garrison town — he has never known the peaceful village life this member represents. He listens to their stories about crops, festivals, and neighborly disputes with the attention of someone learning what he fought for without knowing it.
- **Archer 1 (Evan's former colleague):** Kaelen observes the tension between this member and Evan with the patience of someone who has watched institutional loyalty fracture before. He offers no commentary. He positions himself between them if the tension becomes physical. That is his language.
- **Archer 2 (Joins in Witch arc):** Kaelen welcomes this member with tactical directness. He assesses their combat capability, identifies their weaknesses, and adjusts his positioning to cover them. This is his version of warmth — he protects the people he cares about, and he starts protecting them from the moment they join.

## Loot and Equipment Identity

**Weapon: Iron Mace "Anchor"**

Kaelen's mace is a heavy, single-handed iron weapon with a worn leather grip and a pommel shaped like a flattened disc — designed to be slammed into the ground as a secondary brace for his shield. The mace shows heavy use: dents, scratches, the leather grip replaced multiple times. It is not elegant. It is not fast. It hits like a falling wall.

**Shield: Cracked Tower Shield "The Line"**

Kaelen's shield is his identity — an oversized, reinforced Church-issue tower shield with a visible diagonal crack running from the upper left to the lower right. The shield is iron-faced with a wooden core, reinforced at the edges with steel banding. The Church insignia that once adorned its face has been scraped off, leaving a rough, scarred patch. The crack was never repaired. Kaelen refuses to fix it. *"It reminds me why I am holding this thing."*

**Armor: Deserter's Plate**

A mismatched set of heavy armor assembled from Church-issue pieces Kaelen kept after deserting and civilian reinforcements he added himself. The left pauldron is Church steel, dented and re-forged. The right pauldron is plain iron, shaped by a village blacksmith. The chest piece is reinforced leather over chainmail — mobility over maximum protection. The colors are muted: iron grey with crimson accents (the crimson is from a dye Silas gave him — "Something warm, so you do not look like a walking anvil").

**Equipment Sets:**

- **Deserter Set (Early Game):** Mismatched armor, cracked shield, heavy mace. Functional, scarred, unpolished. Represents Kaelen as the player finds him — capable but carrying the weight of his past.
- **Bulwark Set (Mid Game):** Enhanced defense focus items — reinforced shield bracing, weighted mace head, armor plates with improved damage absorption. The set bonus increases Bulwark duration and Defiant Stand radius. Visually, the shield gains subtle reinforcement along the crack (not fixing it — supporting it), and the armor takes on a more cohesive appearance.
- **Unbroken Set (Late Game):** A personal set built around items Kaelen has chosen — not been given — since joining the party. A shield reinforcement forged by the village blacksmith. A mace grip wrapped in cloth from Evelyn's spare supplies. A pauldron engraved with a small symbol by the party's support member: a shield with a crack through it, not as a flaw but as a badge. The set bonus amplifies all defensive abilities when Kaelen is below 40% health, representing the way his determination hardens under pressure. Visually, the shield's crack glows faintly when Unbroken is active — not hiding the wound, but turning it into a source of strength.

## Party AI Behavior Notes

Kaelen's RL-trained agent operates on the Tanker behavior profile with the expertise scalar (0.0 to 1.0) governing decision-making quality, timing, and resource management.

### Expertise 0.0 – 0.3 (Low)

At low expertise, Kaelen's agent follows reactive patterns with poor resource management:

- **Threat generation:** Uses Bulwark reactively after taking damage, not proactively before enemy attacks. Generates threat inconsistently.
- **Interrupt timing:** Uses Shield Bash on any enemy in range regardless of whether they are telegraphing a dangerous ability. Wastes the interrupt on non-threatening attacks.
- **Positioning:** Stays near the highest-threat party member (usually Evelyn) but does not anticipate enemy flanking. Often ends up on the wrong side of the fight.
- **Energy management:** Spams Bulwark until depleted, then has no energy for Shield Bash or Defiant Stand. Does not pace defensive abilities.
- **Unbroken usage:** Uses Unbroken at high HP (above 70%), wasting the redirect potential. Does not coordinate with the healer's energy state.
- **Overall behavior:** Kaelen feels like a damage sponge — he absorbs hits but does not control the fight. The party gets value from his raw DEF but not from his toolkit.

### Expertise 0.4 – 0.6 (Medium)

At medium expertise, Kaelen's agent demonstrates competent threat management with occasional missteps:

- **Threat generation:** Uses Bulwark proactively before telegraphed heavy attacks. Maintains consistent threat on priority targets.
- **Interrupt timing:** Uses Shield Bash on telegraphing enemies most of the time. Occasionally misses the window or targets the wrong enemy.
- **Positioning:** Positions between enemies and squishy party members (healer, mage). Adjusts to enemy movement but reacts rather than anticipates.
- **Energy management:** Paces Bulwark and Defiant Stand usage. Maintains enough energy for Shield Bash in most encounters. Occasionally over-commits.
- **Unbroken usage:** Uses Unbroken during high-pressure moments (boss phases, ambushes). Sometimes uses it too early or too late, but the timing is generally useful.
- **Overall behavior:** Kaelen feels like a competent tank — the party can rely on him in most situations, but he does not turn fights around.

### Expertise 0.7 – 1.0 (High)

At high expertise, Kaelen's agent operates as an elite aggro manager and encounter controller:

- **Threat generation:** Pre-emptively uses Bulwark before enemy attack cycles begin. Maintains threat on all ThreatMeter-mode enemies simultaneously. Uses Shield Bash threat burst to snap aggro from Evelyn instantly when she pulls too much attention.
- **Interrupt timing:** Uses Shield Bash with frame-perfect timing on the most dangerous enemy telegraph (boss AoE, healer-killer ability, summon). Prioritizes interrupts based on threat assessment — which ability would cost the most if it lands.
- **Positioning:** Anticipates enemy movement patterns and positions Defiant Stand zones to maximize party coverage while minimizing enemy approach angles. Denies enemy flanking before it happens.
- **Energy management:** Maintains optimal energy rotation: Bulwark for predictable damage, Defiant Stand for sustained AoE pressure, Shield Bash for interrupts, energy reserved for Unbroken. Never runs dry.
- **Unbroken usage:** Deploys Unbroken with precision — when the boss enters enrage, when multiple party members are below 50% HP, when the healer has energy to sustain the redirect damage. Coordinates implicitly with Silas's Sanctuary timing.
- **Party coordination:** At maximum expertise, Kaelen's agent demonstrates emergent coordination behaviors:
  - Delays Bulwark by 0.5s if Silas is mid-cast on Ward, allowing the shield to absorb the overlap
  - Positions Defiant Stand to cover Silas's casting position, not Kaelen's current position
  - Uses Shield Bash to interrupt enemies targeting Evelyn even when they are not targeting Kaelen (sacrificing personal threat for party safety)
- **Overall behavior:** Kaelen feels like the party's immovable anchor. Fights flow through him. The party takes less damage, makes fewer mistakes, and can play more aggressively because they trust the line to hold.

### Training Notes for RL Agent

- **Reward signals:** Reward threat generation consistency, successful interrupt timing, damage prevented (not damage dealt), and party HP maintained. Penalize energy depletion, missed interrupts, and Unbroken usage at high HP.
- **Key challenge:** The agent must learn to value *prevention* over *reaction*. Low-expertise behavior is reactive (block after hit); high-expertise behavior is proactive (block before hit). The reward function must heavily weight proactive actions.
- **Coordination dependency:** Kaelen's agent benefits from shared state awareness with Silas's agent. Training them together (or with a shared reward component) produces better emergent behavior than training them independently.
- **Expertise scalar impact:** The expertise scalar should modulate the agent's look-ahead horizon. Low expertise = 1-second planning window. High expertise = 5-second planning window with enemy ability prediction.
