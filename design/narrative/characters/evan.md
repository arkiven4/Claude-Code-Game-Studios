# Evan

**Role**: Deuteragonist — Church Hunter
**POV Chapters**: Chapter 1, Chapters 3 through 11
**Visual**: Steel blue and gold palette. Broad-shouldered, practical armor with Church insignia that he progressively removes or covers as the story progresses. Clean-cut, earnest face that grows harder and more lined as the chapters advance. His silhouette is that of a working soldier — no ornamentation, no flourish, just function. By the epilogue, he wears plain clothes with no markings at all.

---

## Backstory

Evan grew up in a world where the Church was the only institution that made sense. He was not born into privilege — his family were ordinary people, perhaps farmers or tradespeople in a village that had been saved from a monster attack by Church knights. He saw the Church arrive, kill the monster, and leave the village safe. To a child, that looks like heroism. To an adolescent, it looks like purpose. To a young man, it looks like a calling.

He joined the Church as a knight — not as a priest or a researcher, but as a soldier. He was good at it. He had the instincts for tracking, the patience for investigation, and the discipline to follow orders without question. He hunted magical creatures across the countryside, and he believed in the righteousness of every kill. The Church taught him that monsters were an aberration, a corruption of the natural order, and that he was the hand that cleansed it. He believed this because the evidence he saw supported it: monsters attacked villages, and the Church stopped them.

He was not a true believer in the way that Bishop Aldric was a true believer. Evan's faith was practical rather than theological. He did not preach. He acted. He was the kind of knight who reported accurately, questioned privately, and kept his doubts to himself because he assumed they were his own failing, not the institution's.

Then the Church sent him to investigate a village where monsters had been dying mysteriously — killed by something the villagers could not identify. He arrived with his detector, his training, and his certainty. He found Evelyn. They fought. She defeated him and showed mercy.

Everything after that is the story of a man rebuilding his world from the ground up.

Evan investigated the Church because Evelyn's mercy did not fit the doctrine. A monster would have killed him. She did not. This crack in his understanding widened into investigation, investigation into discovery, and discovery into betrayal: the Church creates the monsters it claims to fight. Evelyn was human once, and the Church turned her into a vampire.

The Church marked him as a traitor. Evelyn saved him. He joined her cause — not out of guilt or obligation, but out of the same practical conviction that had always driven him: this is what needs doing, and I will do it.

He fought through the fall of the Church. He bonded with the party. He fell in love with Evelyn — not in a single moment, but in the accumulation of a hundred small ones: the way she joked to deflect fear, the way her tail curled when she was happy, the way she said his name when it mattered.

And then the Witch died, and Evelyn vanished, and Evan was left alone in a world that did not need him anymore.

He is the witness. The one who remembers everyone who was lost.

## Personality

**Core traits:** Dutiful, earnest, dryly humorous, deeply loyal, slow to doubt but unshakeable once committed.

Evan is a good man who served a bad institution. This is not a contradiction — it is the tragedy of his first act. He joined the Church because it did good things, and he was good at the work. The fact that the Church's goodness was selective and its evil systematic does not diminish the sincerity of his service.

**Behavioral patterns:**

- **Action over reflection.** Evan processes the world by doing. When he is confused, he investigates. When he is afraid, he prepares. When he is grieving, he works. He is not emotionally stunted — he is practically oriented. His feelings are real and deep; he just expresses them through what he builds, protects, and fights for.
- **Dry humor.** Evan's humor is understated and often self-directed. He makes quiet observations that are funny because they are true. *"I joined the Church to hunt monsters. Turns out I was one of the monsters they were making."* His humor is not deflection — it is processing. He uses it to make sense of things, not to avoid them.
- **Loyalty as identity.** Evan's defining trait is loyalty. He was loyal to the Church until the Church proved it did not deserve it. Then he transferred that loyalty, entirely and without reservation, to Evelyn and the party. When Evan commits to a person or a cause, he stays. This is his greatest strength and, in the epilogue, his greatest wound — there is no one left to be loyal to.
- **Moral clarity, earned through doubt.** Evan does not arrive at moral nuance easily. He thinks in terms of right and wrong, duty and failure. The Church's betrayal shattered this framework, and the rebuilding process was painful. But once he rebuilt — once he understood that the world is not simple but that choosing good within it still matters — his moral clarity became unshakeable. He does not waver.
- **The burden of survival.** After Evelyn vanishes, Evan carries the weight of being the only one left. He does not crumble — that is not his nature — but he becomes quieter, slower, more deliberate. He walks through the world remembering everyone who is gone. This is his epilogue state: not broken, but hollowed.

## Voice

**Vocal quality:** Grounded, steady, measured. His voice carries the calm of someone who has faced danger repeatedly and learned that panic helps nothing. He speaks with the directness of a soldier giving a report, but softens considerably as the story progresses.

**Speech patterns:**

- **Formal early, softer later.** In Chapter 1, Evan's speech is structured and precise. He uses full sentences, avoids contractions when reporting, and defaults to Church terminology ("the creature," "magical signature," "purification"). By Chapter 5, contractions appear naturally. By Chapter 9, he speaks with the relaxed cadence of someone who no longer needs to prove himself.
- **Hunting jargon.** Evan uses the vocabulary of his training: *track, sign, spoor, trail, quarry, line, hot, cold, fresh.* These terms appear even in non-combat contexts — a verbal habit from years of hunting. *"The trail is cold here, but something passed through recently."* After leaving the Church, he gradually drops institutional terms like "purification" and "cleansing" in favor of plain language like "stop" and "end."
- **Directness.** Evan does not hedge. He says what he means, even when it is uncomfortable. He does not sugarcoat, but he is not blunt for the sake of bluntness — he is honest because he believes honesty is respectful. *"I was sent to kill you. I cannot do that now. I need to understand what you are."*
- **Questions as process.** When Evan is confused or processing something new, he asks direct questions. He does not muse aloud the way Evelyn does — he interrogates. *"Why did you spare me? You had the opening. You chose not to take it. Why?"*
- **Rare profanity.** Evan swears only when his emotional control fractures. When he does, it is significant: *"damn"* when frustrated, *"hell"* when grieving, and very rarely *"god"* when shattered. The player learns to recognize these moments as emotional breaking points.
- **He uses Evelyn's name when he is being sincere.** Early on, he calls her "the creature" or "Evelyn" with distance. Later, "Evelyn" is said with warmth. In Chapter 11, it is said like a prayer.

**Emotional tells:**

- When Evan is doubting himself, his speech becomes more formal — he retreats to the structure of training when he is uncertain.
- When he is angry, his voice gets quieter, not louder. The angriest he has ever been, he speaks the softest.
- When he is moved — genuinely, unexpectedly moved — he goes quiet. He does not have a ready response for tenderness. This happens often around Evelyn.
- In the epilogue, his internal monologue is the primary voice we hear. It is quieter than his spoken voice, more reflective, carrying the weight of someone who has no one left to talk to.

**Formality level:** Moderate. He is not stiff, but he is not casual. He speaks like someone trained to report clearly and who carries that training into civilian life. He loosens up around people he trusts, especially Evelyn.

## Abilities

Evan is not a magical creature. He is a human with exceptional training, discipline, and Church-honed skills. His abilities are entirely learned and practiced.

### Combat Skills

- **Monster Tracking:** Evan can read signs, tracks, magical residue, and behavioral patterns to locate and identify magical creatures. His detection skills are unmatched among Church knights.
- **Close Combat:** Trained in Church sword techniques, Evan is a skilled melee fighter. He fights with efficiency and precision rather than brute force. His combat style is adaptive — he reads his opponent and adjusts.
- **Tactical Thinking:** Evan approaches combat as a hunter, not a brawler. He identifies weaknesses, uses terrain, and plans engagements strategically. In party combat, he serves as the tactical coordinator.
- **Church-Trained Techniques:** Standard Church knight combat forms, including blessed weapon strikes, defensive stances, and anti-magical-creature tactics. After leaving the Church, he adapts these techniques to fight Church enemies rather than creatures.

### Non-Combat Skills

- **Investigation:** Evan is a skilled investigator. He reads documents, cross-references records, and follows evidence chains. His investigation of the Church in Chapters 3 and 4 is driven by his own deductive work.
- **Essence Resonance Detection:** Evan carries a Church-issued detector that reads magical energy signatures. He has learned to interpret its readings with unusual accuracy, distinguishing creature types, power levels, and — crucially — the "dirty" signatures of Church-made creatures.
- **Survival:** Tracking, foraging, shelter-building, first aid. Evan is self-sufficient in the field. He is the party member who ensures everyone has what they need.
- **Diplomacy (Limited):** Evan is not a natural diplomat, but he is honest and fair, which earns him respect from people who value those traits. He is particularly effective with former Church personnel and military-trained individuals.

### Growth Arc

Evan begins as a competent hunter working within a system. By the end, he is a self-directed fighter choosing his own battles. His combat skills remain constant, but their purpose transforms: he goes from hunting creatures to protecting people, from following orders to making calls, from serving an institution to serving the people he loves.

## Emotional Arc

**Chapter 1 — Certainty and Loyalty.** Evan begins the game as a true believer. He hunts monsters competently, reports to the Church faithfully, and sees the world in clear terms. Cracks appear — strange magical energy in dead monsters, Church deflections when he asks questions — but he dismisses them. He trusts the institution that raised him.

**Chapters 3 through 4 — Doubt, Confusion, and Betrayal.** Evan encounters Evelyn. She defeats him and shows mercy. This does not fit his framework. He investigates the Church and discovers the truth: they create monsters, and Evelyn was human. The Church marks him as a traitor. Evelyn saves him. His world shatters and rebuilds in the span of a single chapter.

**Chapters 5 through 7 — Purpose and Bonding.** Evan joins Evelyn's cause with the same conviction he once gave the Church — but this time the conviction is earned, not inherited. He bonds with the party. He falls in love with Evelyn. He fights through the fall of the Church. He is at his most alive during this period, purposeful and connected.

**Chapters 8 through 10 — Determination and Growing Awareness.** The Witch's shadow falls across the world. Evan fights with determination, but he is becoming aware of the weight building around Evelyn. He notices the way she looks at people. He notices the pauses. He does not know what is coming, but he senses loss approaching, and it makes him hold on tighter.

**Chapter 11 — Loss and Solitude.** The Witch dies. Evelyn vanishes. Evan is left alone. He does not scream or break — he goes quiet. The silence after Evelyn vanishes is the loudest moment in the game, and Evan is the one who has to carry it.

**Epilogue — Remembrance.** Evan walks through a world without magic, without Evelyn, without the party. He is the witness. He visits the places they went together. He remembers. He survives. The world is safe. He is alone. This is not a happy ending, and it should not be. But it is honest, and Evan would not trade the memory of what he had for a easier forgetting.

## Relationships

### With Evelyn

Evelyn is the person who broke Evan's world and gave him a better one. Their relationship begins with a fight — he was sent to hunt her, and she defeated him. Her mercy was the first crack in his certainty. After the Church's betrayal, she saved him, and he transferred his loyalty to her completely.

Evan loves Evelyn with the same intensity he brings to everything: entirely, practically, and without reservation. He does not write her poems — he stands beside her in every fight, investigates every lead that might help her, and remembers every small thing she says because he knows her humor masks pain and he wants to understand both.

His love is expressed through action and presence. He is there. He stays. When the world tells him to run, he does not. In Chapter 11, when Evelyn is vanishing, he does not let go — not physically, and not afterward, in the epilogue, where he carries her memory like a duty.

Evelyn calls him by name when it matters. He says her name like it is the only word left.

### With the Church (Before and After the Truth)

**Before:** Evan served the Church with practical conviction. He was not a zealot — he was a professional who believed in the mission. He respected Bishop Aldric, followed orders, and hunted creatures because he believed it was the right thing to do. His faith was not blind; it was evidence-based. The evidence he had pointed to the Church being good.

**After:** The betrayal was total. The Church did not merely lie — it inverted every value Evan believed in. It created the monsters he hunted, experimented on humans, and used its own knights as instruments of deception. Evan's response was not to become cynical but to redirect his conviction. He now fights the Church with the same skill and dedication he once gave it. The energy is the same; the target has changed.

He does not hate the Church with the Witch's purity. He opposes it with the focused determination of someone who knows exactly how it works and what it costs.

### With the Church Bishop (Bishop Aldric)

Evan respects Aldric because Aldric is genuinely good. This makes the relationship complicated: Aldric represents what the Church should be, and Evan knows that good people can serve bad institutions without realizing it. If they cross paths after the truth is revealed, Evan does not try to convince Aldric with anger — he presents evidence. He believes Aldric is smart enough and good enough to see the truth if it is shown to him clearly.

### With the Witch

Evan views the Witch as a sympathetic antagonist. He understands her grief — he has lost people too — but he rejects her method. Where the Witch sees magical creatures as suffering that must end, Evan sees Evelyn and knows that magical creatures can love, choose, and build meaning. His argument against the Witch is not philosophical; it is personal. *"You say they suffer. She does not. She chose this. She chose us."*

There is also an unacknowledged parallel: the Witch lost the Mage, and Evan loses Evelyn. He does not become her — he chooses to remember rather than to erase — but he understands the weight of being the one left behind.

### With Party Members

Evan is the party's tactical anchor. He is not its emotional heart — that is Evelyn — but he is its operational center. He plans routes, reads situations, and keeps everyone alive through competence.

- **Support 1 (Church corruption tie):** Evan shares a professional respect with this member. Both were used by the Church in different ways. Evan understands institutional betrayal and helps this member process it.
- **Support 2 (Village tie):** Evan's relationship with this member is filtered through his connection to the village where he met Evelyn. This member represents the normal life Evan left behind — not that he regrets leaving it.
- **Healer 1 (Personal loss to magical creatures):** Evan navigates this relationship carefully. He understands the healer's grief because he once shared the Church's view that magical creatures are dangerous. His personal evolution is a model for the healer's potential growth.
- **Tanker 1 (Defected Church guard):** Professional camaraderie. Both are trained soldiers who chose to leave the Church. They respect each other's combat skills and share tactical knowledge.
- **Archer 1 (Former colleague):** This relationship carries the weight of Evan's old life. The archer represents who Evan was before the truth. Their interactions are professional but carry an undercurrent of sadness — two people who used to work together on opposite sides of a war neither asked for.
- **Archer 2 (Joins in Witch arc):** Evan mentors this member, passing on the tracking and investigation skills that defined his own career. This is one of the ways he processes his grief — by teaching others what he knows.

## Key Dialogue Samples

Each line is under 120 characters, demonstrating Evan's voice across emotional registers.

> "The trail is fresh. Whatever made this sign is still close. Stay sharp."

> "The Church sent me to clean this area. I will not come back empty-handed."

**Doubt (Chapters 3 through 4):**

> "She could have killed me. She did not. Every rule I know says she should have."

> "This energy signature— it matches Church vials. That is not possible. Is it?"

**Resolve (Chapters 5 through 7):**

> "I served the Church because I believed it was right. I was wrong. I will not be again."

**Tenderness (Chapters 8 through 9):**

> "You joke to hide it, but I see you. I am not going anywhere."

**Loss (Chapter 11):**

> "Evelyn— no. No, there has to be— Evelyn, please."

> "I was sent to hunt you. I stayed because I loved you. I will remember you because I have nothing else."

> "The village is quiet now. No monsters. No Church. No one to protect. Just quiet."

> "I sit where you sat. I do not know what else to do."
