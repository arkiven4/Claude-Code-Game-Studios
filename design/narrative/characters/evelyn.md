# Evelyn

**Role**: Protagonist — Vampire Girl
**POV Chapters**: Prologue (human-to-cat), Chapters 2 through 11
**Visual**: Deep purple and crimson palette. Cat ears atop her head, expressive tail at her lower spine. Pale skin from the vampire transformation, red-tinged eyes visible in certain lighting, elongated canines when she smiles. Soft cartoon aesthetic — her silhouette is unmistakable: slender human frame crowned with feline ears, a tail that betrays every emotion she tries to hide.

---

## Backstory

Evelyn was an ordinary human woman living an ordinary life. Her defining moment of misfortune came when she wandered into the Deep Weald — fleeing something mundane: bandits, a storm, a lost path, it does not matter which. In the Weald she encountered the Cat Magical Beast, a Primal Beast of ancient and alien nature. She laughed at it. Not from malice, but from the human instinct to dismiss what seems impossible. To the Cat, this was refusal to see — the gravest form of disrespect. It cursed her.

The curse rewrote her ontological category. Cat ears appeared on her head. A tail emerged from her lower spine. She gained enhanced physical abilities: high jump, wall crawl, heightened senses, and a passive perception filter that made most humans unconsciously overlook her unusual features. The curse was irreversible. She was no longer fully human — a hybrid entity, part human, part magical beast.

She fled the Weald. The Church found her. They recognized what she was — something unusual, something they could use. They captured her and brought her to a Small Lab, where Inquisitor Veyss personally oversaw her transformation into the Church's most ambitious experiment: a vampire.

The process used concentrated essence harvested from the Mage's execution, archetype catalyst distilled from centuries of vampire folklore, and base essence from multiple Church-made creatures. The transformation succeeded beyond their expectations. Evelyn became a vampire of unprecedented power. But the Control Litany failed. Her half-cat nature — the dual magical signature of human and cat-beast in superposition — created an interference pattern the Church's control seal could not resolve. The seal shattered.

Free, furious, and furious at them, Evelyn tore through her captors and escaped. She found her way to a village on the edge of the Weald — the same village she would come to protect in secret. By day she helped villagers with mundane tasks, carrying water, tending gardens, mending fences. By night she patrolled the perimeter, fighting off monsters that would otherwise breach the walls. She became their secret guardian — the village never knowing what she was, only that something protected them.

Her life was small, quiet, and purposeful. Then Evan arrived.

## Personality

**Core traits:** Graceful under suffering, warm by nature, dryly humorous, fiercely protective, quietly hopeful.

Evelyn carries her curse with dignity. She does not wallow, but she does not pretend it does not hurt either. She has learned to hold two truths at once: her life was stolen from her, and she has built something meaningful from the pieces. She helps villagers not because it redeems her — she does not believe she needs redemption — but because it is who she is. Helping people is not a vampire trait or a cat trait. It is an Evelyn trait.

**Behavioral patterns:**

- **Humor as a shield.** She deflects pain with self-deprecating wit. When someone asks about her ears, she makes a joke. When she is scared, she cracks a smile. This is not avoidance — it is survival. The vampire transformation amplified this tendency; her humor became drier, sharper, more precisely aimed.
- **Service as identity.** Evelyn proves to herself that she is still good by doing good. Every monster she kills protecting the village is a small vote in the argument against her own despair. She needs this. The nights are long, and the silence is loud.
- **Emotional honesty with people she trusts.** With strangers she is polite and guarded. With the village Elder she is respectful but distant. With the blacksmith she is warm and genuine. With Evan, eventually, she is entirely herself — afraid, hopeful, angry, tender, all of it, nothing held back.
- **The vampire hunger for connection.** The transformation amplified her emotional sensitivity. She feels things more deeply than she used to — warmth is warmer, grief is heavier, humor lands sharper. She experiences a hunger not for blood but for closeness, for belonging, for the proof that she is still a person and not just a thing that was made.
- **Quiet rage at the Church.** She does not hate with the Witch's purity. Her anger is more complex: fury at what was done to her, pity for the researchers who thought they were doing science, grief for the subjects who did not survive, and a steady determination that no one else will suffer as she did.
- **Ambivalence toward her own power.** She is grateful for the abilities that let her protect the village, but she resents that they were given without consent. Every time she uses her vampire strength, she is reminded that she did not choose this.

## Voice

**Vocal quality:** Light, warm, slightly breathy. Her voice carries the softness of someone who speaks gently because she knows what it feels like to be shouted at. When she is decisive, her voice firms without rising in volume — she does not need to be loud to be heard.

**Speech patterns:**

- She pauses when thinking. Not the nervous kind — the deliberate kind. She considers her words because she knows they matter.
- She uses contractions naturally. Her speech is not formal or archaic. She talks like a person who learned to speak around a hearth, not in a hall.
- She uses humor to deflect when she is uncomfortable, but the humor is never cruel — not to others, and rarely to herself. The self-directed jokes are gentle, the kind that invite the listener to smile with her rather than at her.
- She rarely swears. When she does, it is quiet and precise: "damn" when something frustrates her, "hell" when something grieves her. She does not reach for profanity as emphasis; her words carry their own weight.
- She has a verbal tic of trailing off mid-sentence when she is about to say something vulnerable, then either completing the thought softly or replacing it with a deflection. *"I thought maybe— well. Doesn't matter."*
- She addresses people by name when she is being sincere. With Evan, she uses his name most often when she is saying something important.

**Emotional tells:**

- Her tail betrays her when her words do not. It flicks when agitated, curls when content, bristles when threatened. She cannot control this. People who know her learn to read it.
- Her ears twitch toward sounds and flatten when startled. They are an emotional barometer she cannot hide.
- When she is truly afraid, her voice gets quieter, not louder. She speaks as if trying not to disturb the air.
- When she is genuinely happy, her laughter is light and surprised — as if joy still catches her off guard sometimes.

**Formality level:** Informal but never sloppy. She is respectful with strangers, warm with friends, and tender with those she loves. She never uses titles unless she is being deliberately distant.

## Abilities

Evelyn's abilities are the combined result of the Cat Magical Beast's curse and the Church's vampire experiment. She is the only entity in the game with this specific combination of powers.

### Cat Abilities (from the Curse)

- **High Jump:** Explosive leg power allows her to leap distances that would require equipment for a normal human. Core gameplay mechanic enabling vertical level design.
- **Wall Crawl:** Micro-adhesive properties in her fingertips and toes let her cling to and scale vertical surfaces, including ceilings. Opens alternative paths.
- **Enhanced Senses:** Cat ears hear frequencies no human ear can detect. She can sense changes in air pressure, temperature, and magical energy. Supplements her vampiric senses.
- **Feline Instincts:** Intuitive understanding of cat and cat-like magical beast body language. Heightened reflexes and spatial awareness.
- **Perception Filter:** Passive effect causing normal humans to unconsciously overlook her cat features. Her brain fills in "a girl" and moves on. Magical beings, Church detectors, and the Witch see her perfectly.

### Vampire Abilities (from the Experiment)

- **Enhanced Strength:** Physical strength far exceeding normal humans. Can shatter stone, bend metal, and overpower most magical creatures in direct combat. Primary combat attribute.
- **Enhanced Speed:** Supernaturally fast movement, reflexes, and reaction time. Translates to combat mobility and dodge capabilities.
- **Enhanced Durability:** Can take damage that would kill a human and continue fighting. Rapid wound healing, though significant injuries still slow her down.
- **Night Vision:** Sees clearly in darkness, with a slight red tint to her vision in low-light conditions.
- **Blood Magic:** Channels vampiric energy into offensive magical attacks — blood-infused strikes, energy projectiles, and area-of-effect bursts. Primary magical combat system.
- **Life Drain:** Absorbs life energy from defeated enemies to restore health. Under her conscious control — she chooses when to use it and when to restrain it.
- **Sensory Magic:** Senses magical energy, living beings, and emotional states through vampiric senses. Overlaps with and supplements her cat-beast sensory abilities.

### Vulnerabilities

- **Sunlight:** Not destroyed by sunlight, but weakened. Prolonged direct sunlight causes discomfort and gradual power drain. Prefers dawn, dusk, and night.
- **Holy Energy:** Church-blessed weapons and rituals cause additional pain and damage. The Church designed the vampire archetype to be vulnerable to its own power — a failsafe that did not require the control seal to function.
- **Detection:** Her unique tripartite magical signature (human + cat-beast + vampire) triggers specialized Church detectors without fitting any known profile. She is an anomaly.

### Immunity

- **Church Control Seals:** Her half-cat nature creates a dual magical signature in superposition that control seals cannot resolve. This is the one absolute that makes her free when every other Church-made creature is bound.

## Emotional Arc

**Prologue — Disoriented and Cursed.** Evelyn begins as a normal human who disrespects something ancient and pays a price she does not understand. Her curse is confusing, frightening, and irreversible. She is captured before she can process what has happened to her. The player meets her at her most vulnerable and watches her taken.

**Chapters 2 through 4 — Secrecy and Discovery.** Having escaped, Evelyn builds a small, quiet life as the village's secret guardian. She helps people by day and protects them by night, unseen and unthanked. She is lonely but functional. Then Evan arrives — a Church hunter sent to investigate the village. They fight. She defeats him and shows mercy. She does not know it yet, but this moment begins everything.

**Chapters 4 through 7 — Bonding and Purpose.** The truth is revealed: the Church creates monsters, Evelyn was human, Evan's faith shatters. Evelyn saves Evan. They join forces. The party forms. For the first time since her curse, Evelyn is not alone. She bonds with her companions. She laughs. She belongs. The player falls in love with her — her warmth, her humor, her quiet strength, the way her tail curls when she is happy and she pretends not to notice.

**Chapters 8 through 10 — Deepening and Dread.** The Witch's shadow falls across the world. The party faces its greatest challenges. Evelyn shines — she is radiant, purposeful, fully alive. But beneath the surface, something is building. The player who pays attention can sense it: the weight in her pauses, the way she looks at people a moment too long, the conversations that feel like they are being stored for later. She suspects what the ending will cost. She does not say it aloud. She will not take this from them.

**Chapter 11 — Acceptance and Vanishing.** The final confrontation. The Witch dies, and the Vanishing begins. Evelyn fades. She knows what is happening. She is afraid — not of death, but of leaving. Her final exchange with Evan is quiet, tender, and devastating. She does not rage against it. She accepts it. She vanishes.

**Epilogue — Gone.** Evelyn does not appear. Her absence is the point. The world is safe. It is also smaller.

## Relationships

### With Evan

Evan is the person who was sent to hunt her and became the person she could not live without. Their relationship begins with violence — he investigates her, they fight, she spares him. It deepens through shared truth — he learns she was human, she learns he believed in something worth believing. It crystallizes when she saves him and they choose to fight together.

Evelyn loves Evan because he sees her — all of her, human and cat and vampire, cursed and created, afraid and brave — and does not look away. He is the first person since the blacksmith who treats her as a person, but with Evan it goes further: he treats her as someone worth staying for.

Her love is not dramatic or declarative. It is in the way she says his name when he is doubting himself. It is in the way her tail curls when he enters the room. It is in the quiet of their last conversation, when she chooses to make it gentle for him even as she is disappearing.

Evan survives alone. This is the tragedy Evelyn does not get to grieve.

### With Party Members

Evelyn is the heart of the party — not its leader, but its emotional center. She connects with each member individually, and these bonds are the vehicle for the game's "Earn the Ending" pillar.

- **Support 1 (Church corruption tie):** Evelyn shares a bond with this member over their mutual understanding of institutional betrayal. She was betrayed by the Church's experiments; this member was betrayed by its lies. They find solidarity in surviving the same broken system from different angles.
- **Support 2 (Village tie):** This member joins after a side quest connected to the village. Evelyn's relationship with them is rooted in shared love of the place she protects. They represent the life she could have had if she had not been cursed.
- **Healer 1 (Personal loss to magical creatures):** This is the most complex relationship. The healer has lost someone to a magical creature — possibly one the Church created. Evelyn must navigate being a magical creature herself while offering genuine compassion. Her ability to hold both truths — the healer's grief and her own existence — models the nuance the game asks of its players.
- **Tanker 1 (Defected Church guard):** Shared understanding of breaking free from the Church's control. The tanker chose to leave; Evelyn was made to leave. They respect each other's agency.
- **Archer 1 (Evan's former colleague):** Evelyn's relationship with this member is filtered through Evan. She watches Evan's old life intersect with his new one and feels the weight of what he left behind for her.
- **Archer 2 (Joins in Witch arc, personal stake):** This member joins during the Witch's campaign. Evelyn sees in them the same determination she has — the refusal to let grief be the last word. Their bond is one of shared resolve.

### With the Village Elder

The Elder suspects what Evelyn is but never asks. This unspoken trust is one of the most emotionally resonant relationships in the game. Evelyn respects the Elder's wisdom and is grateful for the grace of unasked questions. She protects the village partly because it is the right thing to do and partly because it is the closest thing she has to a home.

### With the Blacksmith

The blacksmith is the first person who treats Evelyn normally — not with suspicion, not with gratitude, not with the careful distance people keep from someone they do not understand. "Ears don't change how you swing a hammer." The blacksmith becomes Evelyn's first genuine friend and the emotional anchor that makes the village feel like home. Evelyn treasures this friendship without fully realizing how much she needs it.

### With the Witch (Unknowing Parallel)

Evelyn and the Witch are mirrors of each other — both victims of the Church, both transformed by forces beyond their control, both carrying the weight of what was taken from them. The Witch would eliminate Evelyn without hesitation, believing it an act of mercy. Evelyn, if she understood the Witch's full story, would pity her — not condescendingly, but with the recognition of someone who also knows what it is to lose everything.

They never share a scene of genuine connection. This is intentional. The tragedy is that they could have understood each other, and the world did not give them the chance.

## Key Dialogue Samples

Each line is under 120 characters, demonstrating Evelyn's voice across emotional registers.

**Warmth / Humor:**

> "Cat ears. Vampire fangs. If I had a tail that wagged, I'd be someone's pet."

> "I do not need to eat, but have you tasted bread with butter? I refuse to give that up."

**Deflection / Vulnerability:**

> "Am I still me? I keep asking. The mirror does not answer."

> "I thought maybe— well. It is late. We should rest."

**Resolve / Combat:**

> "I will not let them take this village. I have already lost everything else."

> "They made me a weapon. I choose who I point it at."

**Tenderness / Evan:**

> "Evan. When this is over— never mind. Just stay close, alright?"

> "You were supposed to hunt me. Instead you stayed. I will never understand that."

**Acceptance / Final Moments:**

> "Do not look at me like that. I got to live. Most do not get even this."

> "Remember the village. Remember the bread. Remember— just remember me, Evan."
