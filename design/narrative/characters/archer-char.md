# Lyna

**Role**: Party Member — The Markswoman
**POV Chapters**: Joins Chapter 3, Chapters 5 through 11
**Visual**: Burnt orange and charcoal palette. Wiry and compact — she moves like someone who has spent years drawing a bow and living lean. A long coat reinforced with stitched leather panels, fingerless archery gloves, a bandolier of trick arrows across her chest. Her hair is dark brown, kept in a messy half-tie with a few stubborn strands always falling across her face. Her eyes are amber-gold and miss nothing. Her silhouette is unmistakable: the recurve bow on her back is longer than she is tall, the coat tails flare when she moves, and she is always, always scanning the horizon.

---

## Backstory

Lyna grew up in a border settlement between Church territory and the Deep Weald — a place where monsters, Church patrols, and smugglers all passed through, and the people who lived there learned early to trust no one and shoot first. Her parents ran a waystation: food, shelter, and information for travelers heading into or out of the Weald. She learned the bow before she learned to read.

She was not born with talent — she was born with obsession. Where other children played, she practiced. Where other archers were satisfied with hitting a target, she was dissatisfied until she could hit the same spot on the target a hundred times in a row. By sixteen she could split an arrow already lodged in wood. By nineteen she could track a deer by its footprints alone, draw silently, and make a shot in wind that would knock another archer off balance.

Her skill caught the attention of the Church's Border Guard — not the knights, not the hunters, but the perimeter watchmen who kept monsters from spilling into settled land. She joined as a marksman under Captain Harrow, a veteran who recognized that Lyna's obsession was not about glory but about control. If she could see the shot, she could take it. If she could take it, she could survive.

The Church's Border Guard was one of the few institutions the Church had not yet fully corrupted. They were genuine — not holy warriors, just people who kept watch so others could sleep. Lyna respected that. She worked alongside Evan during joint operations in the border zones. They were not close — he was Church, she was Border Guard, and their chains of command rarely crossed — but she trusted him. He was competent, and competence mattered more than rank.

Then the Church's internal reorganization absorbed the Border Guard into the Inquisition. Captain Harrow resisted. The next week, a "monster incursion" killed him and half his unit. The official report called it a breach of the Weald perimeter. Lyna read the report and saw the lie: the attack came from inside the settlement, not outside. The Church had purged its own border watch because they would not cooperate with the Inquisition's new "essence collection" operations.

Lyna went rogue. She took her bow, her bandolier, and a list of names — the Church officers responsible for the purge. She spent months tracking them, one by one, using every tracking skill Captain Harrow had taught her and every instinct the Weald border had forged. She was not killing for revenge — she was killing for evidence. Each officer carried documents, orders, ledgers. She collected them because she understood something the Church did not expect: the truth was more dangerous than any single death.

She crossed paths with Evelyn and Evan in Chapter 3, when her hunt for evidence intersected with their investigation into the Church's experiments. She recognized Evan from their border days. She recognized Evelyn from the way the Church was talking about her — with the same panic they had used when talking about Captain Harrow. Lyna joined them not out of sentiment but out of calculation: together they could reach evidence she could not reach alone, and together they had a chance of bringing the Church down rather than just picking off its officers one at a time.

She stays because the party becomes the family the border took from her. She does not say this aloud. She shows it by never missing a shot when someone she loves is in the line of fire.

## Personality

**Core traits:** Observant, sardonic, fiercely loyal beneath the surface, patient to a fault, quietly sentimental.

Lyna sees everything. This is not a superpower — it is a survival mechanism. On the border, missing a detail meant death. In the Church, missing a lie meant complicity. She watches people the way she reads wind: instinctively, constantly, and with the understanding that the smallest shift changes everything.

**Behavioral patterns:**

- **The watchful pause.** Lyna does not respond immediately. She takes a breath, looks at you, and processes. This is not hesitation — it is the same pause she uses before releasing an arrow. She is calculating: what you said, what you meant, what the consequences of her response will be. People who know her learn that the pause means she is taking you seriously.
- **Sardonic humor.** Lyna's humor is dry, observant, and often aimed at authority. She does not mock for cruelty — she mocks for clarity. A joke that exposes a lie is her preferred form of truth-telling. *"The Church calls it 'purification.' I call it murder with extra steps."* Her humor is her border-town armor: the world is absurd, so you might as well laugh at it before it kills you.
- **Competence as love language.** Lyna does not say "I care about you." She says "I checked your gear while you were sleeping. Your bowstring was fraying." She shows love by making sure the people around her are prepared, protected, and alive. She will never admit this is emotional. She will claim it is practical. The party knows better.
- **Patience as weapon.** Lyna can wait. She can sit in a tree for six hours, motionless, waiting for a single shot. She can endure silence, discomfort, boredom, and hunger without complaint. This patience translates to her emotional life: she does not rush into trust, but once she has it, it is unshakeable. She is the last person to join the party emotionally and the first to put herself in danger for them.
- **Sentimental hoarding.** Lyna keeps things. A broken arrow from Captain Harrow's last stand. A pressed flower from the border settlement's only garden. A scrap of the party's first camp banner. She does not display these items — she keeps them in a small leather pouch at her belt, and she touches it when she thinks no one is looking. It is the only concession she makes to grief.
- **Refusal to perform vulnerability.** Lyna will not cry in front of the party. She will excuse herself, go somewhere alone, and process. If someone finds her, she will make a joke and deflect. This is not because she does not feel — she feels intensely — but because vulnerability, on the border, was a liability. Unlearning this is part of her arc.

## Voice

**Vocal quality:** Low, raspy, understated. Her voice carries the flat accent of the border — neither Church-formal nor village-warm, but practical and direct. She does not raise her voice to be heard; she speaks at the volume she needs and lets people come closer if they want to listen.

**Speech patterns:**

- **Short sentences.** Lyna speaks economically. She does not waste words. Her sentences are often fragments, not out of ignorance but out of efficiency. *"Wind's wrong." "Two hostiles. Left ridge." "Got it."* When she does speak at length, the party knows something important is happening.
- **Border idioms.** Her speech is colored by border-town expressions that the Church and village characters do not use: *"The wind knows," "Steel holds," "Clear line, clear conscience," "Don't shoot what you do not intend to hit."* These are practical sayings, not philosophical ones — they come from a culture that learned wisdom from survival.
- **Technical precision.** When discussing combat, tracking, or tactics, Lyna's vocabulary becomes precise and jargon-heavy: *draw weight, anchor point, windage, elevation, grouping, nock, release, follow-through.* She uses these terms with Evan and expects him to understand them. With non-combat party members, she translates.
- **Sarcasm as deflection.** When Lyna is uncomfortable — emotionally, not tactically — she reaches for sarcasm. It is her equivalent of Evelyn's humor-as-shield, but where Evelyn's is warm and self-deprecating, Lyna's is sharp and outward-directed. She aims it at authority, at absurdity, and occasionally (gently) at herself. *"Oh, brilliant plan. Walk straight into the Church fortress. I am sure nothing could go wrong."*
- **She uses people's names when she is being sincere.** Like Evelyn and Evan, Lyna uses names deliberately. With Evan, she uses his full name when she is annoyed ("Evan, you idiot") and just "Evan" when she is being earnest. With Evelyn, she uses "Evelyn" with respect and warmth. With the party at large, she rarely uses names — she uses roles ("healer," "tank") until she is close enough to earn the right to use their actual names.
- **She does not do speeches.** Lyna will never deliver a rallying cry. Her version of inspiration is a quiet "I have your back" before a fight. If the party needs a speech, Evelyn gives it. Lyna gives them an arrow in the right place at the right time.

**Emotional tells:**

- When Lyna is angry, she goes very still and very quiet. The angrier she is, the fewer words she uses. At maximum anger, she says nothing at all — she just draws and shoots.
- When she is moved, she looks away. She cannot handle direct eye contact during emotional moments. She will find something to check — her arrows, the horizon, a distant sound — anything to break the line of sight.
- When she is genuinely happy, she makes a soft, almost inaudible sound — not quite a laugh, not quite a hum. It is the sound she makes when she is relaxed enough to let her guard down. The party hears it rarely, and it is always a good sign.
- When she is grieving, she cleans her bow. Methodically, obsessively, every string and joint. It is her version of meditation, and the party learns to recognize it as the sign that she is processing something hard.

**Formality level:** Low. Lyna speaks like someone who learned to communicate in conditions where words were secondary to action. She is not rude — she is efficient. With the party, she relaxes enough to be genuinely funny, though the humor remains dry.

## Combat Role

Lyna is the party's ranged DPS and tactical controller. She excels at positioning, area denial, and single-target elimination. Her RL agent is trained to maximize damage output while maintaining safe distance from melee threats, using terrain for cover and trap skills to shape enemy movement.

### Core Combat Identity

**Range Dominance.** Lyna is most effective at medium to long range. She deals significant single-target damage and can control enemy positioning through traps and debuffs. Up close, she is vulnerable — her coat hides light armor, and her combat training is archery-first, not melee.

**Positional Play.** Lyna's combat effectiveness is tied to where she stands. Good positioning means clear lines of fire, escape routes, and elevation advantages. Bad positioning means she is trapped and out of her element. The player learns to treat Lyna's placement as a tactical priority.

**Ammunition Economy.** Lyna uses different arrow types for different situations. She carries a limited quiver, so arrow selection matters. Running out of a specific arrow type mid-fight forces adaptation.

### Skills

**1. Piercing Shot**

A high-velocity arrow that penetrates armor and damages everything in a straight line behind the primary target. Lyna channels draw weight into a single, devastating release.

- *Tactical purpose:* Boss weak-point targeting, armored enemy elimination, crowd piercing (hitting the front-line enemy and damaging those behind)
- *Cooldown:* 8 seconds
- *RL agent note:* Prioritize high-value armored targets. Use when three or more enemies align.

**2. Snare Arrow**

An arrow tipped with a Weald-vine compound that erupts on impact, creating a zone of entangling vines that slow and root enemies within its radius for 4 seconds.

- *Tactical purpose:* Area denial, escape tool, enemy grouping disruption, protecting vulnerable party members
- *Cooldown:* 12 seconds
- *RL agent note:* Deploy between enemies and squishy allies. Use to prevent enemy flanking. Combo with Evelyn's AoE for trap-and-burst.

**3. Marking Shot**

A flare arrow that sticks into the ground or a target, marking all enemies within its radius for 6 seconds. Marked enemies take 15% increased damage from all party attacks and their positions are revealed through walls.

- *Tactical purpose:* Intelligence gathering, damage amplification setup, boss engagement opener
- *Cooldown:* 15 seconds
- *RL agent note:* Open engagements with this skill. Mark clusters, not single targets. Coordinate with party AoE windows.

**4. Dead Calm (Ultimate)**

Lyna enters a state of absolute focus for 6 seconds. During Dead Calm, her attack speed doubles, all shots become precision shots (ignoring 50% of enemy armor), and she is immune to interruption. She can fire up to 8 arrows during this window.

- *Tactical purpose:* Burst damage phase, emergency elimination of priority targets, clutch survival
- *Cooldown:* 45 seconds
- *RL agent note:* Reserve for boss vulnerability windows or when a priority target must be eliminated immediately. Do not waste on low-value enemies.

### Passive: Border Instinct

Lyna gains a 10% damage bonus when attacking from elevation or cover. She also detects hidden enemies within a 15-meter radius, revealing them to the party.

## Emotional Arc

**Chapter 3 — Introduction: The Lone Hunter.** Lyna enters as a competent, self-sufficient operative with her own mission. She is not looking for a party — she is looking for evidence. Her initial interactions with Evelyn and Evan are professional, guarded, and transactional. She helps them because it serves her goals. The player sees her skill immediately: she takes a shot that saves Evan's life without being asked, then says nothing about it.

**Chapters 4 through 5 — Reluctant Integration.** Lyna travels with the party out of necessity, not desire. She keeps her distance, handles her own gear, and volunteers information only when it is tactically relevant. But cracks appear: she checks the party's perimeter at night without being asked. She shares her rations with the healer. She corrects Evan's archery form with a familiarity that suggests they have done this before. She is integrating whether she intends to or not.

**Chapters 6 through 7 — The Bond Forms.** The turning point comes when the party discovers evidence of the Church's Border Guard purge — Lyna's people. Evelyn does not offer pity. She offers solidarity: "They took people from me too. Different people. Same method." Lyna's response is characteristically understated: "I know." But something shifts. She stops traveling at the back of the party and moves to the middle. She starts using people's names. She keeps her leather pouch of mementos visible instead of hidden.

**Chapters 8 through 9 — Full Commitment.** Lyna is now one of the party's emotional anchors. She is not its heart — that is Evelyn — but she is its spine: the one who holds the line when everything else is falling apart. During the Witch's campaign, Lyna's border-honed survival skills become invaluable. She navigates corrupted zones, identifies ambushes before they trigger, and keeps people alive through sheer competence. She also begins to show her sentimental side: she starts collecting small mementos from the party's journey, the way she kept Captain Harrow's arrow. The party notices. She pretends they do not.

**Chapters 10 through 11 — The Cost.** As the story approaches its end, Lyna's arc tightens around a single theme: what it means to stop running and let yourself belong. She has spent her life on the border — between the Church and the Weald, between belonging and independence, between caring and not caring. The final chapters force her to choose. She chooses the party. She fights with everything she has, not because the cause is right (though it is) but because these are her people.

**Epilogue — The Watch Continues.** After Evelyn vanishes, Lyna does not leave. She stays with the remaining party members, and she becomes the one who keeps watch. Not because she has to — because she chooses to. She visits the border settlement sometimes. She places Captain Harrow's broken arrow on the waystation's mantle. She does not talk about Evelyn, but she keeps Evelyn's camp banner scrap in her pouch, and she touches it when she thinks no one is looking. Some habits do not change.

## Relationships

### With Evelyn

Lyna respects Evelyn immediately — not because of what Evelyn is, but because of what she does. On the border, Lyna learned to judge people by their actions, not their nature. Evelyn protects the village without asking for credit. That is enough.

Their relationship deepens through shared experience of institutional betrayal. The Church took Evelyn's humanity and Lyna's found family, and neither woman asked for pity. They recognize each other as survivors who turned their pain into purpose. Evelyn is warm where Lyna is guarded, and Lyna finds Evelyn's warmth disarming — not threatening, but unfamiliar in a way that makes her cautious.

Lyna's bond moment with Evelyn centers on the leather pouch of mementos. Evelyn notices Lyna touching it and does not ask what is inside. Instead, she says: "I keep things too. Little proofs that I existed before all this." Lyna, after a long pause: "Yeah. Me too." It is the closest they come to a therapy session, and it is exactly enough.

In combat, Lyna positions herself to cover Evelyn's blind spots instinctively. She does not announce this. She just does it. Evelyn notices, and her tail does a small, grateful curl that Lyna pretends not to see.

### With Evan

Evan and Lyna have the deepest pre-existing history of any two party members. They worked together during joint Church–Border Guard operations before the purge. Their dynamic is professional respect layered over something warmer that neither has fully acknowledged.

Lyna calls Evan "Church" as a teasing nickname early on, dropping it only when she is being serious. Evan calls her "Lyna" from the start — he is one of the few people who does not need to earn the right to use her name.

Their combat synergy is the best in the party. They do not need to communicate verbally — a glance, a gesture, and they are coordinated. Evan tracks; Lyna shoots. Evan engages; Lyna covers. They fight like people who have done this a hundred times, because on the border, they did.

The emotional complexity comes from what is unsaid. Lyna left the Church's orbit when the Border Guard was purged, and Evan was still serving. She does not resent him — she understands he did not know — but there is a distance between them that only closes after the truth comes out. Once it does, their bond is unshakeable. Lyna trusts Evan with her life because she always has, and the revelation that the Church was corrupt did not change that. It changed who she was pointing her bow at, not who she trusted at her back.

### With the Healer

Lyna's relationship with the healer is one of the most emotionally layered in the party. The healer has lost someone to a magical creature, and Lyna has spent her career killing things that the Church called monsters. There is a tension here: the healer sees Lyna as someone who kills without understanding the cost, and Lyna sees the healer as someone who judges without understanding the context.

They resolve this through a shared moment: the healer tends to an injury Lyna takes in combat, and Lyna, wincing, says quietly: "I do not kill because I like it. I kill because if I do not, someone I care about dies." The healer, bandaging: "I know. I am sorry I did not see it before." It is not a dramatic reconciliation — it is two people choosing to understand each other.

### With the Tanker

Professional respect, pure and simple. The tanker holds the line; Lyna shoots what gets past. They do not need to talk much — their combat coordination speaks for itself. Off the battlefield, Lyna appreciates the tanker's straightforwardness. The tanker appreciates that Lyna never wastes a shot. They are the party's defensive backbone, and they know it.

### With Captain Harrow (Deceased)

Harrow was Lyna's mentor, commander, and the closest thing she had to family before the party. He taught her everything about tracking, shooting, and reading people. His death is the defining wound of her backstory, and his memory shapes every decision she makes. She does not talk about him often, but when she does, her voice is different — softer, less guarded, the way someone speaks about a person they are still grieving.

The broken arrow in her leather pouch is from Harrow's last stand. She found it where he fell. She has never used it in combat — it is not a tool, it is a relic.

## Loot and Equipment Identity

### Weapon: "Long Watch" Recurve Bow

A border-crafted recurve bow, longer than standard military issue, built for range and precision rather than rapid fire. Made from Weald ironwood reinforced with steel laminations. The grip is wrapped in worn leather — Lyna has re-wrapped it multiple times over the years. The bow is engraved with a single border motto along the limb: *"Steel holds."*

- *Stat identity:* High single-shot damage, moderate attack speed, excellent critical hit chance
- *Upgrade path:* Reinforced laminations → Weald-core string → Harrow's sight (enchanted targeting lens)

### Signature Arrow Types

- **Piercing Heads:** Armor-penetrating arrow tips, crafted from hardened Weald steel
- **Snare Shafts:** Hollow arrows filled with Weald-vine compound, developed during her border years
- **Flare Tips:** Magnesium-core flare arrows for marking — originally Church-issued, now modified by Lyna
- **Dead Calm Arrows:** Precision-fletched arrows with balanced weight distribution, used exclusively during her ultimate

### Equipment Set: "Border Warden"

A light armor set designed for mobility and range. Each piece reinforces Lyna's identity as a marksman who values positioning and survival over brute defense.

- **Coat:** Reinforced leather longcoat — provides moderate defense, maximum mobility
- **Gloves:** Fingerless archery gloves — enhance draw speed and precision
- **Bandolier:** Trick arrow harness — increases ammunition capacity and swap speed
- **Boots:** Silent-step boots — reduce movement noise, enhance stealth positioning

### Set Bonus

**"Clear Line"** — When Lyna has an unobstructed line of sight to her target for 3+ seconds, her next shot deals 25% bonus damage. Rewards patient, intelligent positioning.

## Party AI Behavior Notes

Lyna's RL agent is trained with the following behavioral profile across expertise levels:

### Expertise 0.0 — Novice

- Stays at maximum range, rarely repositions
- Fires basic attacks without arrow type optimization
- Uses Snare Arrow reactively (only when enemies are already in melee range)
- Marks random targets rather than high-value ones
- Dead Calm triggers on cooldown regardless of tactical value
- *Player experience:* Functional but inefficient. Deals damage but wastes opportunities.

### Expertise 0.5 — Competent

- Repositions when enemies close within 10 meters
- Uses Piercing Shot on armored targets
- Deploys Snare Arrow proactively to protect squishy allies
- Marks enemy clusters before party AoE engagements
- Dead Calm reserved for priority targets but not always optimally timed
- *Player experience:* Reliable ranged DPS. Contributes meaningfully to party strategy.

### Expertise 1.0 — Expert

- Maintains optimal range with predictive repositioning — moves before enemies commit
- Chains skills optimally: Mark -> Piercing Shot -> follow-up, or Snare -> Evelyn AoE -> cleanup
- Uses terrain for cover and elevation bonus consistently
- Dead Calm reserved exclusively for boss vulnerability windows or emergency priority elimination
- Coordinates with Evan's tracking for guaranteed precision shots on marked targets
- Adapts arrow type to enemy composition without player input
- *Player experience:* Feels like playing with a veteran archer. Every arrow lands with purpose. The player trusts Lyna to handle her lane.

### Behavioral Tendencies (All Levels)

- **Aggression:** Moderate — Lyna is a sustained damage dealer, not a burst-first character (except during Dead Calm)
- **Self-Preservation:** High — she prioritizes survival and positioning over risky damage opportunities
- **Team Coordination:** High — her Marking Shot and Snare Arrow are team-enabling skills, and her RL agent learns to use them for party benefit, not personal DPS
- **Adaptability:** High — Lyna's RL agent learns enemy patterns quickly and adjusts arrow selection and positioning accordingly
