# Corin

**Role**: Party Member — The Trapsman
**POV Chapters**: Joins Chapter 9, Chapters 9 through 11
**Visual**: Olive drab and ash-grey palette. Lean and wiry — not tall, but built with the compact tension of someone who has spent years coiling tripwire and checking his perimeter. His hands are scarred with burn marks from misfired bolts and calloused from winding wire. He wears a heavy canvas rig — a harness of leather loops, pouches, and clips that hold crossbow bolts of every type: broadheads with red fletching, slender silver bodkins, chunky explosive heads with visible fuse caps. His crossbow is compact but heavily modified — a repeating crank-crossbow with a underslung wire-spooler and a bolt-magazine that looks like it has been repaired and rebuilt more times than anyone can count. His hair is dark, shaggy and uneven, cut short with his own knife. His eyes are pale grey and constantly moving — scanning edges, corners, overhead branches, the ground at his feet. His silhouette is unmistakable: the crossbow rig across his chest like a bandolier, the wire-spooler at his hip, the way he checks every surface before he puts weight on it. He never stands in an open doorway. He always stands with his back to a wall.

---

## Backstory

Corin grew up in a village called Oakhaven — a small, unremarkable settlement on the eastern edge of the Weald that survived on trapping, foraging, and minding its own business. His father was a trapper who taught him wire, snare, and patience. His mother was a cooper who taught him precision and the value of a tight seal. Corin learned both. He was not the strongest kid in the village or the fastest, but he was the most thorough. If Corin checked a trap line, nothing had slipped through. If Corin set a perimeter, nothing got in without him knowing.

He was good at preparation because preparation was the one thing that made sense. You could not control the weather, or monsters, or the Church, or luck. But you could control whether your wire was taut, your bolts were oiled, and your trip-lines had tension. Preparation was honest. Preparation kept you alive.

Then the witch-hunt purge came.

It was not a monster attack. It was the Church — an Inquisition detachment that swept through three villages in a single night, killing anyone suspected of harboring magical sympathies, harboring creatures, or simply being in the wrong place at the wrong time. Oakhaven had done nothing wrong. It did not matter. The detachment's commander had a quota, and Oakhaven was on the list.

Corin survived because he was paranoid. He had set perimeter traps the night before — not because he expected an attack, but because he always set perimeter traps. When the Church detachment approached, the tripwires triggered his alarm: a string of tin cans and bone chimes strung through the tree line. He woke, grabbed his crossbow and his rig, and ran. He did not try to fight. He did not try to save anyone. He ran.

From the treeline, he watched. He saw the torches. He heard the screaming. He counted the shots — forty-seven crossbow bolts from the Church detachment, twelve screams from his village, then silence. He did not cry. He catalogued: number of attackers, their formation, their equipment, their withdrawal route. He stored the information because information was the only thing he could do with it.

He spent the next months alone, moving through the Weald's outer forests, surviving on traps and whatever he could forage. He avoided all settlements. He avoided all roads. He avoided all people. People were variables he could not control, and variables got you killed.

He survived the Witch's spreading shadow the same way he survived everything: by preparing for the worst and assuming every shadow contained a threat. He set traps around his camps, checked them twice, slept with his crossbow loaded, and never stayed in one place for more than two nights.

He joins the party in Chapter 9 when they arrive at a sanctuary of survivors — a hidden encampment of refugees, defectors, and people who had nowhere else to go. Corin is there because the sanctuary is the first place he has found that has walls, guards, and a perimeter he did not have to build himself. He does not trust it. He does not trust anything. But he is tired, and the sanctuary has food, and he is running out of bolt heads.

The party needs someone who knows area denial, trapcraft, and the kind of methodical thinking that turns a defensible position into an unbreakable one. Corin is that person. He does not join out of loyalty — he joins because the sanctuary's leader asks him to, and Corin respects the chain of request even if he does not respect the person making it.

He stays because the party proves, slowly and repeatedly, that they check their own perimeter. That they watch each other's backs. That they are, in the only way that matters to someone like Corin, prepared.

## Personality

**Core traits:** Anxious, methodical, hypervigilant, quietly brave for others, deeply competent beneath the nerves.

Corin is a man whose survival depends on noticing things. This has kept him alive and made him exhausted. He notices the snap of a twig, the wrong angle of a shadow, the absence of birdsong. Most of the time, it is nothing. All of the time, it costs him energy. He is nervous not because he is cowardly but because he is always, always calculating the ways things could go wrong.

**Behavioral patterns:**

- **The perimeter check.** Corin checks everything. New room? He checks corners, ceiling, under furniture, behind doors. New campsite? He walks the perimeter, tests the ground, checks for existing traps, and adds his own. He does this without announcing it. The party learns that when Corin stops to "inspect" something, he is actually keeping them alive.
- **Preparation as language.** Corin processes emotion through preparation. When he is anxious, he organizes his bolts. When he is afraid, he checks his wire. When he is grieving, he methodically disassembles and cleans every piece of his rig, then reassembles it in silence. The state of his equipment reflects the state of his mind: immaculate order on the outside, controlled chaos underneath.
- **Brave for others.** Corin is terrified of most things — crowds, open spaces, magical creatures, the Church, the dark, loud noises. But when someone else is in danger, his fear disappears, replaced by the cold clarity of a problem that needs solving. He will walk into a burning building if someone is inside. He will stand between a monster and a child with nothing but a tripwire and a crossbow. He does not do this because he is fearless — he does it because someone else needs him to be.
- **Refusal to delegate safety.** Corin does not trust anyone else to check his gear, set his traps, or verify his perimeter. This is not arrogance — it is trauma. The last time he trusted someone else's preparation, his village burned. He will not make that mistake again. Eventually, with the party, he learns to let others verify his work. It is one of the hardest things he does.
- **Dry, technical humor.** Corin's humor is nerdy, specific, and usually about traps. *"That door has more tripwires than my entire camp. Whoever built this was either brilliant or very lonely. Probably both."* He uses humor to make people comfortable in dangerous situations — if he is making a joke about the explosives, it means he has the situation under control.
- **Sentimental about tools.** Corin keeps his father's wire-cutters. They are rusted, dull, and nearly useless — but he carries them in a leather pouch at his belt, and he touches them when he is thinking. It is the only thing he has left from Oakhaven, and it is the only memento he allows himself.

## Voice

**Vocal quality:** Quick, slightly breathless, prone to rushing. His voice carries the nervous energy of someone who expects to be interrupted by danger at any moment. He speaks fast, often tripping over his own words when excited or anxious. When he is calm — which is rare — his voice slows down and becomes precise, almost clinical.

**Speech patterns:**

- **Run-on sentences when nervous.** Corin talks fast when he is anxious, which is most of the time. His sentences spill into each other, punctuated by self-corrections and tangents. *"Okay so the — no, wait, the wire goes — actually, let me just — the left flank needs a snare because if they come from the left we're — right, left flank, got it."* When he catches himself, he stops, breathes, and tries again more slowly.
- **Technical precision when focused.** When discussing traps, crossbows, or tactics, Corin's vocabulary becomes exact and jargon-heavy: *tension, gauge, trigger, deadfall, tripwire, bore, velocity, spread, detonation radius, safe distance.* He expects people to keep up. If they do not, he will explain — patiently, carefully, the way his father taught him.
- **Trap metaphors.** Corin describes non-combat situations using trap and preparation vocabulary. *"This plan has no fallback. It is a deadfall with no release mechanism."* *"We need redundancy. One wire breaks, another catches."* *"That argument is a misfire — the bolt is in the chamber but it is not going anywhere."*
- **He uses people's names when he is trying to be brave.** Corin is not naturally confident. When he needs to project it — for someone else — he uses their name. It grounds him. *"Evelyn, I have this. I promise. Stay behind me."*
- **He apologizes too much.** Corin says sorry for things that are not his fault: loud noises, bad weather, other people's mistakes, the existence of danger. The party gradually teaches him to stop. He does not fully succeed, and they do not fully expect him to.
- **He does not do speeches.** Corin will never deliver a rallying cry. His version of inspiration is a quiet "I have the perimeter covered" before a fight. If the party needs a speech, Evelyn gives it. Corin gives them a tripwire exactly where the enemy will step.

**Emotional tells:**

- When Corin is angry, he goes very quiet and very still. His hands stop fidgeting. His eyes fix on the target. He speaks in short, precise sentences. This is the most dangerous version of Corin.
- When he is moved, he looks at his hands. He cannot handle direct eye contact during emotional moments. He will find something to adjust — a bolt, a wire, a strap — anything to give his hands something to do.
- When he is genuinely happy, he hums. It is a quiet, tuneless sound — the same thing he hums when he is working on his rig. The party learns to recognize it as the sound of Corin at peace.
- When he is grieving, he builds something. A trap, a device, a repair — anything that requires focus and precision. He channels pain into creation because destruction is what took everything from him.

**Formality level:** Low. Corin speaks like someone who learned to communicate in forests and workshops, not courts or academies. He is not rude — he is distracted. With the party, he relaxes enough to be genuinely funny, though the humor remains nervous and technical.

## Combat Role

Corin is the party's area denial specialist and tactical controller. He excels at shaping the battlefield, controlling enemy movement, and creating zones where enemies cannot safely operate. His RL agent is trained to maximize battlefield control while maintaining safe positioning, using traps and explosive bolts to funnel enemies into kill zones.

### Core Combat Identity

**Zone Control.** Corin is most effective when the battlefield is shaped to his advantage. He places traps before combat begins, uses explosive bolts to break enemy formations, and maintains tripwire networks that punish enemies for moving freely. The more prepared the battlefield, the more powerful Corin becomes.

**Preparation Phase.** Unlike other party members who are equally effective from combat start, Corin has a ramp-up period. The first 10–15 seconds of an encounter are his setup window — deploying traps, laying wire, establishing control zones. After setup, his effectiveness increases dramatically.

**Ammunition Diversity.** Corin carries multiple bolt types, each with distinct tactical applications. Bolt selection and swap timing matter. Running out of a specific bolt type mid-fight forces adaptation.

### Skills

**1. Explosive Bolt**

A heavy crossbow bolt loaded with a volatile chemical compound that detonates on impact, dealing area damage in a moderate radius and applying a brief knockback to enemies caught in the blast.

- *Tactical purpose:* Formation breaking, clustered enemy damage, interrupting enemy channels, clearing summoned minions
- *Cooldown:* 10 seconds
- *RL agent note:* Prioritize groups of three or more enemies. Use to break enemy formations before they engage melee allies. Do not waste on single targets unless they are high-value.

**2. Tripwire**

Corin deploys a nearly invisible wire trap across a chosen path. When an enemy crosses the wire, it triggers a snare that roots the enemy for 3 seconds and deals moderate damage. Up to three Tripwires can be active simultaneously.

- *Tactical purpose:* Area denial, chokepoint control, enemy pathing manipulation, protecting vulnerable party members
- *Cooldown:* 6 seconds per wire, 3 active max
- *RL agent note:* Place at chokepoints, between enemies and squishy allies, and around the party's rear flank. Rotate placement to prevent enemies from learning patterns. Combo with Evelyn's AoE for trap-and-burst.

**3. Deadfall**

Corin rigs a heavy debris trap that drops from above (or triggers from the ground if no overhead cover exists) when an enemy enters its radius, dealing heavy damage and applying a slow for 4 seconds. Enemies caught in the center take double damage.

- *Tactical purpose:* Single-target elimination, boss phase damage, punishing enemy advances, protecting objectives
- *Cooldown:* 18 seconds
- *RL agent note:* Reserve for high-value targets or boss vulnerability windows. Place where enemy pathing is predictable. Do not use on scattered low-value enemies.

**4. Perimeter Lock (Ultimate)**

Corin deploys a rapid network of traps, wires, and explosive charges across a large area for 8 seconds. During Perimeter Lock, any enemy that enters the zone is slowed by 60%, takes periodic damage, and is marked for the party. Corin gains unlimited Explosive Bolts and zero cooldown on Tripwire placement during this window.

- *Tactical purpose:* Encounter control, emergency zone lockdown, boss containment, party protection during vulnerable phases
- *Cooldown:* 50 seconds
- *RL agent note:* Deploy when the party needs breathing room — during boss enrage, when overwhelmed, or to protect a reviving ally. Maximize Explosive Bolt spam during the window.

### Passive: Overprepared

Corin gains a stacking defense bonus for every active trap he has placed (up to 5 stacks). Each stack provides 4% damage reduction and 3% increased trap damage. Corin is strongest when the battlefield is fully prepared.

## Emotional Arc

**Chapter 9 — Introduction: The Survivor Who Trusts Nothing.** Corin enters as a competent, deeply anxious operative who has survived entirely through preparation and isolation. He is not hostile — he is wary. He checks the sanctuary's perimeter three times before he agrees to meet with the party. When Evelyn introduces herself, Corin's eyes track her ears, her tail, her fangs — not with fear, but with the calculation of someone cataloguing threat vectors. He joins because the sanctuary's leader asks him to, not because he trusts the party. His first words to Evelyn: *"You are a vampire. Okay. Okay. I have read about vampires. Not — not the Church books. Other books. From before."*

**Chapters 9 through 10 — Reluctant Integration.** Corin travels with the party but keeps his distance. He sets his own traps around the campsite, checks his own perimeter, and sleeps with his crossbow loaded. He volunteers tactical information when it is relevant — *"That pass has three natural chokepoints. We can hold it with two people and a wire line."* — but he does not share personal information. He is integrating functionally but not emotionally.

The first crack appears during a combat encounter: Corin sees an ally (the healer) about to be flanked and, without thinking, drops everything and sprints to lay a Tripwire between the healer and the enemy. He saves the healer's life. He does not acknowledge this afterward. The healer does.

**Chapter 10 — The Wound Opens.** The party discovers a ruined village that mirrors Oakhaven — burned, abandoned, the remnants of a Church purge. Corin walks through the ruins in silence. His hands are steady. His voice is not.

*"This is Oakhaven. Not — not mine. Another one. Same pattern. Church detachment, night attack, no survivors reported."* He touches a blackened beam. *"I ran. I heard the — I heard everything. I ran."* He says it like a confession. Like it is the worst thing he has ever said.

Evelyn does not offer pity. She offers solidarity: *"I did not run when the Church came for me. I was in chains. I could not. If I could have run, I would have. You survived. That is not a failure."*

Corin looks at her. His eyes are wet. He does not cry. *"I count them. Every night. Forty-seven bolts. Twelve screams. I count them so I do not forget."*

*"Then count them,"* Evelyn says. *"And count the people you keep alive now. That matters too."*

Something shifts. Corin stops checking his perimeter alone. He starts asking the party to verify his traps — not because he does not trust his own work, but because he is practicing trust. It is the hardest thing he does.

**Chapter 11 — Full Commitment.** Corin is now one of the party's tactical anchors. His trapcraft becomes essential during the Witch's campaign, where area control and chokepoint defense are critical. He fights with a bravery that surprises everyone — not because he is fearless, but because he has decided that the people behind him are worth being afraid for.

He uses Perimeter Lock to protect the party during the final battle's most dangerous phases, deploying trap networks that hold back waves of enemies. He does not fight with the Witch's rage or Evelyn's desperation. He fights with the cold precision of someone who has decided that this is the line he will not cross.

**Epilogue — The Line He Holds.** After Evelyn vanishes, Corin does not leave. He stays with the sanctuary — the place that gave him his first wall, his first perimeter he did not have to build alone. He becomes its chief defender, redesigning its defenses, training its guards, and checking the perimeter every night. Not because he has to — because he chooses to.

He visits the site of Oakhaven sometimes. He does not rebuild it. He places a single tripwire around the ruins — not to trap anything, but as a memorial. A line that says: *this mattered.* He touches his father's wire-cutters and he counts — not the bolts and screams anymore, but the people he has kept alive since.

Some habits do not change. Some do.

## Relationships

### With Evelyn

Corin's relationship with Evelyn begins with calculation and ends with devotion. When he first meets her, he catalogues her as a magical creature — threat vectors, capabilities, potential risks. He has read about vampires in pre-Church books, and what he has read makes him cautious but not hostile.

Their bond deepens through shared understanding of Church violence. Evelyn was transformed by the Church; Corin's village was destroyed by it. Both carry the weight of what the Church took from them, and both respond by protecting other people. Evelyn protects her village; Corin protects the sanctuary. The parallel is not lost on either of them.

Corin's bond moment with Evelyn centers on the counting. When he confesses that he counts the bolts and screams every night, Evelyn does not tell him to stop. She tells him to count the living too. This reframes his trauma from a memorial of failure into a ledger of survival. It is the closest thing to therapy Corin allows himself.

In combat, Corin positions his traps to protect Evelyn's blind spots instinctively. He does not announce this. He just does it. Evelyn notices, and she makes a point of stepping on his tripwires — carefully, deliberately — to show him that she trusts his work. Corin pretends not to see. He checks the wire afterward to make sure she did not damage it. She did not. He is relieved.

### With Evan

Corin respects Evan's tactical competence immediately. Evan is a trained hunter who reads terrain, tracks enemies, and plans engagements — all things Corin values. Their relationship is built on shared operational language: perimeter, threat, engagement zone, fallback position.

Evan is one of the few people who does not try to calm Corin's nerves. He understands hypervigilance — he has it himself, honed by years of hunting. Instead of telling Corin to relax, Evan engages with it: *"What is your assessment of this pass?"* *"How many traps do we need to hold this position?"* He treats Corin's anxiety as a tactical asset, which is exactly what it is.

Corin calls Evan "Hunter" as a professional courtesy — not a nickname, a title of respect. Evan calls Corin by name from the start, which Corin notices and appreciates without saying anything.

Their combat coordination is strong but not instinctive like Evan and Lyna's. Evan tracks; Corin traps. They complement each other: Evan identifies where enemies will be, and Corin makes sure those places are dangerous.

### With Silas (Healer)

Corin's relationship with Silas is the most emotionally transformative in the party. Silas is the person Corin saves with his Tripwire during the flanking incident — the moment that begins Corin's journey toward trust.

Silas treats Corin's anxiety the same way he treats every other condition: with patience, professionalism, and zero judgment. When Corin apologizes for something that is not his fault, Silas says gently: *"You do not have to apologize for the rain, Corin. It is not your storm."* Corin does not fully understand this, but he files it away.

Silas is also the person who notices Corin's burn marks — the scars from misfired bolts and chemical burns — and treats them without asking how he got them. *"These are old. Do you want me to look at them?"* Corin declines, but the offer matters. It is the first time someone has noticed his injuries and cared without wanting something in return.

Their bond crystallizes when Silas asks Corin to teach him basic trap-setting for the clinic's perimeter. Corin is touched — not by the request itself, but by the trust it implies. Silas is asking Corin to keep him safe. Corin takes this very seriously. He spends three days designing the clinic's trap network. Silas does not have the heart to tell him it is overkill.

### With Kaelen (Tanker)

Professional respect, absolute and mutual. Kaelen holds the line; Corin makes sure the line is where enemies want to be. They do not need to communicate much — their combat coordination speaks for itself. Kaelen positions himself in Corin's trap zones, drawing enemies into kill zones. Corin places his wires where Kaelen can funnel enemies. They are the party's defensive spine, and they know it.

Off the battlefield, Kaelen's stillness calms Corin's nerves. Kaelen is the most stable person Corin has ever met — immovable, unflappable, solid. Corin finds this restorative. He does not say this aloud. He shows it by checking Kaelen's shield straps without being asked. Kaelen notices. He does not comment. He adjusts the straps himself, slowly enough that Corin can see he is doing it right.

**Key dynamic:** Kaelen once asked Corin: *"Do you ever stop checking?"* Corin answered: *"No."* Kaelen nodded. *"Good. Neither do I."* It was the most intimate conversation either of them had that week.

### With Lyna (Archer)

Lyna and Corin are the party's ranged specialists, and their relationship is one of the most complex. Both are archers — but where Lyna is a long-range precision marksman, Corin is a close-to-mid-range trapsman. Both are hypervigilant survivors of Church violence. Both process emotion through preparation. But where Lyna's vigilance is outward-facing — she watches the horizon — Corin's is inward-facing — he watches the perimeter.

They recognize each other immediately. The first time they share a campsite, they both check the perimeter separately, compare notes, and silently agree that neither missed anything. It is the closest thing to a compliment either of them gives.

Their combat synergy is excellent but different. Lyna controls the long game — marking, snaring, eliminating priority targets. Corin controls the near game — traps, wires, area denial. Together, they create a battlefield where enemies are punished at every range. The player learns that playing Lyna and Corin together makes the entire party more effective.

Emotionally, they are mirrors. Both lost their found families to Church violence. Both survive through competence and preparation. Both struggle with trust. But where Lyna has learned to channel her grief into sardonic humor and loyalty, Corin is still in the anxious, isolating phase of survival. Lyna recognizes this — she was there once, after Captain Harrow died. She does not push Corin to open up. She shows him, through action, that the party is safe. She checks his bolt supply before a fight. She covers his blind spots. She does what she does: competence as love language.

Corin notices. He does not say thank you. He sharpens Lyna's arrowheads for her. She notices. She does not say thank you either. It is exactly enough.

### With the Witch

Corin views the Witch with the same analytical distance he applies to all threats. He understands her power — he has seen what magical creatures can do — but he does not share the party's philosophical engagement with her tragedy. To Corin, the Witch is a hostile force that is endangering people he is responsible for protecting. Her reasons are irrelevant to his job.

If they were to meet, Corin would not argue with her. He would not try to understand her grief. He would set a Tripwire at her feet and fire an Explosive Bolt into her position. He fights the Witch not because of what she represents but because of what she does. This practical clarity is both his strength and his limitation — he does not engage with the moral complexity of the Witch's campaign because his moral framework is simpler: protect the people behind me, stop the threat in front of me.

### With the Sanctuary

The sanctuary is the closest thing Corin has to a home after Oakhaven. It is not perfect — the walls are makeshift, the guards are undertrained, and the food runs low — but it is the first place he has found where someone else checked the perimeter first. This matters more than he can articulate.

He becomes the sanctuary's chief defender not out of obligation but out of choice. He redesigns its defenses, trains its guards, and walks the perimeter every night. The sanctuary's children learn to recognize the nervous man with the crossbow rig who checks their sleeping area before he checks his own. They are not afraid of him. He is gentle with them — adjusting his rig so the bolts do not poke them, teaching them basic trap safety, humming his tuneless hum while he works.

## Loot and Equipment Identity

### Weapon: "Second Chance" Repeating Crossbow

A compact, heavily modified crank-crossbow built for close-to-mid-range combat. The body is ironwood reinforced with steel brackets — some forged by Corin himself, others salvaged from Church crossbows he has repurposed. The crank mechanism allows rapid reloading without breaking stance. The underslung wire-spooler feeds tripwire directly from a drum mounted on the stock. The bolt-magazine holds six rounds and has been repaired so many times that barely any original parts remain.

Engraved along the stock, in Corin's own hand: *"Check twice. Fire once."*

- *Stat identity:* Moderate single-target damage, high area control utility, excellent trap synergy
- *Upgrade path:* Reinforced crank → Tension-calibrated spooler → Oakhaven sight (hand-carved targeting lens from his father's tools)

### Signature Bolt Types

- **Explosive Heads:** Volatile chemical compound bolt tips — Corin's signature area denial tool, crafted from salvaged Church munitions
- **Tripwire Bodkins:** Slim, silver-tipped bolts designed to carry and anchor wire traps — precision tools, not weapons
- **Deadfall Rounds:** Heavy, blunt-head bolts that trigger structural collapses — developed from his father's deadfall trap designs
- **Perimeter Lock Charges:** Custom-made explosive charges used exclusively during his ultimate — each one hand-assembled

### Equipment Set: "Perimeter"

A light-to-mid armor set designed for mobility, trap deployment, and survival. Each piece reinforces Corin's identity as a trapsman who values preparation and area control over direct engagement.

- **Rig:** Canvas and leather bolt harness — increases ammunition capacity, swap speed, and bolt-type organization
- **Coat:** Reinforced canvas field coat — moderate defense, maximum pocket space for tools and wire
- **Gloves:** Burn-resistant trap-setting gloves — enhance trap deployment speed and reduce misfire chance
- **Boots:** Soft-soled scout boots — reduce movement noise, enhance stealth positioning for trap placement

### Set Bonus

**"Overprepared"** — For every active trap Corin has placed, he gains 4% damage reduction and 3% increased trap damage (stacks up to 5). Rewards patient, intelligent battlefield preparation.

## Party AI Behavior Notes

Corin's RL agent is trained with the following behavioral profile across expertise levels:

### Expertise 0.0 — Novice

- Places Tripwires randomly rather than at chokepoints
- Fires Explosive Bolts at single targets, wasting area damage
- Rarely maintains three active Tripwires simultaneously
- Deadfall used on low-value targets instead of high-priority enemies
- Perimeter Lock triggered at suboptimal times, often when enemies are already scattered
- *Player experience:* Functional but inefficient. Traps do damage but do not control the battlefield.

### Expertise 0.5 — Competent

- Places Tripwires at natural chokepoints and between enemies and squishy allies
- Uses Explosive Bolts on enemy clusters (three or more)
- Maintains two to three active Tripwires, rotating placement
- Deadfall reserved for high-value targets or boss phases
- Perimeter Lock deployed during high-pressure moments — boss enrage, ambushes, ally revival windows
- *Player experience:* Reliable area denial. Shapes enemy movement meaningfully. Contributes to party strategy beyond raw damage.

### Expertise 1.0 — Expert

- Pre-combat trap placement: enters encounters with Tripwires already positioned at predicted chokepoints
- Chains skills optimally: Tripwire funnel -> Explosive Bolt into trapped zone -> Deadfall on boss
- Maintains full Overprepared stacks (5 active traps) throughout extended encounters
- Perimeter Lock reserved exclusively for encounter-turning moments, maximizing Explosive Bolt spam window
- Coordinates with Kaelen's positioning — traps are placed where Kaelen can funnel enemies
- Adapts bolt type and trap placement to enemy composition and terrain without player input
- Predictive trap rotation: replaces triggered Tripwires before enemies can adapt to the pattern
- *Player experience:* Feels like playing with a veteran tactician. The battlefield itself becomes a weapon. Enemies walk into death by design. The player trusts Corin to hold his lane.

### Behavioral Tendencies (All Levels)

- **Aggression:** Low-to-moderate — Corin is a controller, not a damage-first character. His damage comes from traps and explosives, not sustained fire.
- **Self-Preservation:** Very high — he prioritizes safe positioning and perimeter maintenance over risky damage opportunities. Will reposition before attacking.
- **Team Coordination:** Very high — his Tripwire and Perimeter Lock are team-enabling skills, and his RL agent learns to use them for party benefit, not personal DPS.
- **Adaptability:** High — Corin's RL agent learns enemy movement patterns quickly and adjusts trap placement and bolt selection accordingly. Excels against predictable, pathing-dependent enemies.
